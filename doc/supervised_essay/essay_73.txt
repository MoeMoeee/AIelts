Revised:

In recent years, there has been a significant increase in the number of individuals with health complications who prefer alternative medicines and treatments over visiting their regular doctors. While some argue that having the same doctor has disadvantages, I believe that alternative treatments are crucial for treating patients.

Those who trust alternative medications more than doctors may have valid reasons. Firstly, nowadays medical science itself has become a lucrative profession, and doctors often charge high fees for prescriptions. Due to this, patients are not willing to pay their fees just for diagnosis and instead turn to internet sources or home remedies to find solutions. They feel that spending money on doctors is useless. Secondly, if their symptoms are mild, they may not consider visiting a doctor and may believe that they can cure themselves.

Despite the arguments mentioned above, in my opinion, trying various different treatments is worthwhile. On the one hand, having a choice of treatments can significantly improve one's health and provide satisfaction with alternative methods of cure. For instance, options like yoga, acupuncture, and Chinese medicines have proven to be effective in treating patients. On the other hand, alternative therapies are often more cost-effective and promote overall well-being. Practices like meditation and yoga are almost free and provide relief from depression, anxiety, and other similar conditions. For example, many diabetes patients have successfully managed to reverse their sugar levels simply by practicing yoga regularly.

In conclusion, although there are drawbacks to relying solely on alternative medicines and treatments, I firmly believe that exploring other methods of treatment can be more beneficial for patients.
