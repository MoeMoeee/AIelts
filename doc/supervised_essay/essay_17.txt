Revised:

One of the widely discussed issues nowadays is whether schools should teach students how to be financially competent in the world. It is argued that schools should add classes where children will be taught how to handle money. Personally, I tend to strongly agree with this viewpoint, as it offers great opportunities for children to become more informed and financially successful.

Firstly, it is well known that teaching financial literacy from an early age is more beneficial, as children grasp information easily at this stage of development. By learning the value of funds, they will become more accurate when making purchases. This early financial education will help them avoid complaining about the lack of money because they would have already developed a broad understanding of how to deal with different financial difficulties. For instance, children will be able to save and budget their money to afford expensive items like houses or cars, which is the ultimate goal for many adults.

On the other hand, it can be argued that financial education should not be the sole focus of school curriculum. It is important for teachers to pay equal attention to other subjects to provide students with a well-rounded education. It should be taken into consideration that not all children may be interested in finance, hence it is crucial to offer a diverse range of subjects to cater to their interests and needs.

In conclusion, while there are some valid arguments against incorporating financial education in schools, the benefits outweigh the drawbacks. Children can greatly benefit from early financial literacy lessons as it equips them with essential skills for their future. Therefore, I strongly believe that teaching students how to survive financially in the world today is not only necessary but also contributes to producing well-rounded and successful individuals.
