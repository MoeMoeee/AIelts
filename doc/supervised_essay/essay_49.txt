Revised:

Although some people believe that humans should use animals to satisfy their different needs, such as for food and research, there is a growing number of individuals who argue that animals should not be exploited and instead be granted the same rights as humans. In my opinion, animals deserve better treatment and should not be subject to abuse.

Throughout history, it was common practice to utilize animals for various tasks, such as transportation. Many animals were made to work their entire lives without respite, enduring abuse. Back then, most people agreed with this without questioning its morality.

However, more recent studies have shown that animals have emotions, albeit not identical to humans but similar in some ways. Nowadays, there are alternative methods available to fulfill our needs, such as technological advancements. For example, there are now diverse means of transportation, including eco-friendly options, and numerous substitutes for meat that are both nutritious and do not involve sacrificing animals.

Moreover, animals should have recognized rights to ensure they lead healthy and safe lives. While these rights may not be identical to those granted to humans, there should be laws in place to protect animals and consider their unique needs. For instance, in Brazil, there have been recent changes in laws to prohibit the use of animals in research and penalize animal abuse.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that animals should no longer be employed to satisfy human needs. Furthermore, animals should be granted rights globally to ensure they have a better quality of life, with laws taking into consideration their specific requirements, similar to human rights.
