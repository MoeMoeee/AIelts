Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Revised:

In this century, there is a growing number of people who are deeply concerned about animals' rights, making it a controversial and relevant topic of discussion.

Many individuals nowadays disapprove of situations and events involving animal exploitation. For instance, circuses have experienced a decline in popularity due to audience preference for human performances. Furthermore, legislation has been enacted to protect animal rights, leading to penalties and even imprisonment for those who abuse animals.

Additionally, people are now more aware of the cruelties involved in animal breeding and the subsequent killing and slaughtering of animals. The emerging generations are developing a greater sensibility towards animal rights. However, there is also a rise in extremist ideologies regarding this matter.

Despite the increasing number of vegetarians, many of whom follow this lifestyle as a fashion statement rather than a genuine concern for animal rights, there are still individuals with distorted views.

One area of concern is animal research, which plays a vital role in advancing medical studies and improving human welfare. Animal rights activists often spread misinformation that directly hampers progress in the research sector. For example, a few months ago, an animal rights group destroyed years of neurological research by freeing rats used in a laboratory, unaware that these animals were being treated with anesthesia during the experiments.

Taking these factors into consideration, I wholeheartedly support animal rights and oppose any form of animal exploitation. However, I do not endorse extremist views, particularly when it comes to medical research.

In conclusion, the topic of animal rights has gained significant attention in recent times. While there are valid reasons to advocate for animals' rights and oppose their exploitation, caution should be exercised to ensure that this advocacy does not hinder crucial advancements in medical research.
