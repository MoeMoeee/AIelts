Revised:

In daily life, people hold various positions around the world, with a differentiation between leaders and followers. While some individuals prefer to be leaders, enjoying their freedom, others prefer being followers, relying on others. Personally, I strongly believe that being a leader is better than being a follower.

To begin with, leaders are directors of a group and they are able to make decisions by considering both their own interests and the interests of the group. Unlike followers and other group members, leaders can act independently and make unilateral decisions in risky situations without the need for discussions. Furthermore, leaders can follow their own path when conflicts arise due to clash of ideas, as they have the power among the group. Therefore, leaders symbolize independence within the group and can play a dominant role.

Moreover, being a leader allows individuals to gain confidence, which can be applied in various areas of their lives. Followers often lack the ability to implement their own ideas and are frequently involved in discussions or conflicts where their ideas may not be adequately considered. However, by leading a society or crowd, individuals continuously develop self-confidence. This increased confidence enables leaders to make more rational decisions in stressful and challenging situations. Furthermore, this self-assuredness can ensure success for leaders, allowing them to excel and inspire others with their confidence. Consequently, being a leader also helps individuals to be more fulfilled and confident in different areas.

In conclusion, being a leader has numerous positive effects and consequences. Firstly, it allows individuals to experience freedom and independence. Secondly, it increases their confidence and determination. Personally, I would definitely prefer to be a leader.
