Revised:

There is a controversial statement heating up a debate over the fact that some recruiters favour candidates who have formal credentials for study over those with only life experience or good personality traits in today's society. This essay will look at some causes of this and I personally do not have a consensus with this idea.

Without a shadow of a doubt, academic degrees are valued more than life experience and character by some employers when they seek new staff. This is because they believe that potential employees with excellent qualifications are equipped with in-depth knowledge and useful skills which play a crucial part in their future careers. For instance, leading technology firms such as FPT and Vingroup highly appreciate prospective workers who have Information Technology degrees or Internet Engineering certificates, as they believe these qualifications ensure a high standard of competence in the field. Therefore, academic background is given priority in their recruitment procedures, as it is believed to contribute to an improved quality of life for candidates. Hence, taking academic qualifications as a priority in the recruitment process has certain benefits.

However, while the necessity of having a distinguished degree is widely acknowledged, the importance of personal qualities and life experiences should not be underestimated when selecting new employees. Many skills needed for a job can be acquired through experience, which helps people deal with difficult situations more effectively. For example, social and communication skills can be trained and practiced through experiencing ups and downs in life, and these skills are crucial in various professional settings. Thus, it is detrimental to assess an individual's working ability based solely on their academic performance.

In conclusion, although academic qualifications can have certain advantages in terms of knowledge and skills, they should not be regarded as the only or the most important factor when selecting the best workers. Personal qualities, life experiences, and the ability to adapt and grow are equally important. Therefore, employers should adopt a more holistic approach, considering both academic qualifications and personal characteristics, in order to make the best hiring decisions.
