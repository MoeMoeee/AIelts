Essay:

"The question of whether children’s leisure activities must be educational has sparked heated debate. Some regard that all events should have their aim or purpose, while others see entertaining events as also beneficial. I personally contend with the idea that leisure activities for kids are not necessarily educational, and other events are also meaningful. In this essay, I will elaborate reasons in more detail and provide my personal experience.

First of all, educational events with certain purposes usually bring pressure and can be easily observed. When parents and educators intentionally required students to participate in some particular activities, they become unwilling to cooperate and tend to lose their motivation and curiosity on certain topics. Since, it is their nature that people hate to be forced to do something and so are youngsters, thus, planned events with some goals consistently backfired and ultimately killed the possibility for them.

On the other hand, facts are proven that non-educational events can also result in educational values. Playing basketball, for instance, is seen as a mere recreational sport. However, it is well-known that individuals who joined basketball teams have performed better leadership, negotiation skills and teamwork ability. This is because when a team is playing to win, it grows related skills mentioned that equipped themselves, and makes them capable of winning. Therefore, we can end up learning from those events who regards as only complete waste of time.

Regarding my personal experiences, while often seen as a recreational activity, watching America TV shows (such as Friends) has brought unthinkable positive effects on me. Indulging in beloved dramas, my English improved to the next level since I was more familiar with its pronunciation and sentence structure. Surprisingly, it was events without a purpose that benefited me.

In conclusion, educational events do not always reach their original goals, and possibly cased opposite outcomes. Additionally, entertainment can otherwise provide valuable educational achievements. Therefore, activities for teens should not be detailly planned, and certainly shall not always be educational."


Revised:

"The question of whether children's leisure activities must be educational has sparked heated debate. Some people argue that all activities should have a purpose, while others believe that entertaining events can also be beneficial. I personally disagree with the idea that leisure activities for children must always be educational, as I believe other types of activities can also hold value. In this essay, I will elaborate on my reasons and provide relevant examples to support my stance.

To begin with, I believe that educational events with a specific focus can often result in added pressure and be easily observed. When parents or educators force children to participate in certain activities, the children tend to become unwilling to cooperate and lose their motivation and curiosity. It is natural for people, including children, to resist being forced to do something. Therefore, planned events with specific goals often backfire and ultimately hinder the potential for learning.

On the other hand, it has been proven that non-educational events can also have educational benefits. For instance, participating in recreational sports like basketball may seem purely recreational at first, but it is well-known that individuals who join basketball teams develop better leadership, negotiation skills, and teamwork abilities. When a team plays to win, members acquire these skills and become capable of achieving success. Therefore, it is evident that we can learn valuable lessons from activities that are sometimes dismissed as a waste of time.

Drawing from my personal experience, I have found that watching American TV shows, such as Friends, has had unexpected positive effects on my language skills. Despite being seen as a recreational activity, immersing myself in these beloved dramas has significantly improved my English proficiency. By familiarizing myself with the show's pronunciation and sentence structure, I was able to enhance my language abilities. Surprisingly, it was activities without a specific educational purpose that benefited me the most.

In conclusion, educational events do not always achieve their intended goals and can even have adverse effects. Encouraging children to participate solely in educational activities can limit their potential for growth and hinder their curiosity. It has been proven that non-educational events can also contribute to valuable educational achievements. Therefore, children's leisure activities should not always be strictly planned or focused solely on education."
