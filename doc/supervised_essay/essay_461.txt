Revised:

In the modern network society, the majority of people tend to write product reviews on social platforms to share their opinions about goods and services. While this can be beneficial for consumers, others argue that many of these reviews are unrealistic. This essay strongly supports the latter viewpoint.

On one hand, positive reviews on social media can indeed attract new customers and drive sales. When a product or service receives a large number of positive reviews, it becomes more appealing to potential buyers. For example, if there are two shops, A and B, in the same location and shop A has received five-star reviews from a thousand people while shop B only has three-star reviews from twenty people, it is evident that shop A would be the preferred choice for most customers. Consequently, businesses today strive to collect as many positive comments as possible on social networks to boost their growth, and some even offer promotions to customers for writing favorable reviews.

On the other hand, it cannot be denied that many online reviews are fake. Some users may exaggerate their review to support a business they are affiliated with, while others may leave negative feedback out of personal bias. Some are even paid to write bogus positive comments or negative reviews. For instance, YouTubers often receive financial support from sponsors, making it difficult for them to provide unbiased reviews. Therefore, it is important for consumers to experience a product or service firsthand rather than relying solely on these reviews.

In conclusion, although customer reviews on social media can have positive impacts on new customers and sales, the prevalence of fake reviews cannot be ignored. While it is understandable that positive reviews can sway consumer decisions, it is crucial for individuals to exercise caution and form their own opinions based on their own experiences.
