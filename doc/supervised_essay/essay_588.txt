Revised:

School is an institution that plays a crucial role in a child's growth, but it is not the sole factor that influences them. While some argue that schools have more influence on children than their parents, I believe that parental influence remains paramount. In the following paragraphs, I will elaborate on this perspective.

Firstly, children are shaped by their parents even before they enter school. Parents are the foundation upon which a child's personality is built. Although school facilitates further development and provides knowledge required for functioning in society, the influence of parents continues to play a significant role. Personally, I learned essential social skills from my parents and then applied and improved upon them while attending school. Thus, even after children enter school, parents retain their role and have a strong influence.

Secondly, children perceive their parents as fundamental to their lives, considering school as merely a component of their overall lifestyle. In my own experience, I never believed that school had greater influence than my parents did. I lived my school life utilizing the social skills I acquired at home, and after classes ended, I found solace in the thought of returning to my parents. This example highlights that even when children are in school, they continue to view their parents as the primary influencers in their lives.

In conclusion, parental influence is the foundation of a child's growth and cannot be undermined. While schools have a role in enhancing the knowledge children acquire from their parents, children understand that their existence stems from their parents, and school is an integral part of their lives. Thus, I disagree with the opinion that schools have more influence on children than their parents do.
