"The internet is undoubtedly one of the most important inventions of this century. High-speed internet has become widely available, leading to the increased popularity of social media. While some people argue that social media might be beneficial to some extent, I do believe that the massive use of social media is detrimental to our society.

Indeed, the public will feel the pressure to buy or own the advertised products, even though they do not need them at all.

In conclusion, although social media may allow  users to keep in touch with their families and friends, I believe that it has detrimental effects on our society such as loss of privacy and increase consumerism behaviour."


Revised:

In recent years, social media platforms have gained immense popularity due to the widespread availability of high-speed internet. While some argue that social media has its advantages, I firmly believe that its excessive use has detrimental effects on our society.

To begin with, it is indeed true that social media offers opportunities for users to communicate with people from around the world, enabling the sharing of ideas and inspiring stories. Additionally, social media has become a valuable tool for business purposes, with many young entrepreneurs advertising their products on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. These aspects of social media do have beneficial effects. However, the overwhelming popularity of these platforms has given rise to significant problems in our society.

One major concern is the loss of privacy caused by the extensive use of social media. Teenagers and young individuals tend to share every aspect of their lives, often posting inappropriate content publicly, such as videos or photographs depicting intimate moments, alcohol consumption, or provocative behavior. This lack of discretion can have negative consequences, including potential criminal activities.

Moreover, social media feeds into consumerism, instigating impulsive buying behavior. Popular social media influencers and celebrities constantly promote fashionable trends in clothing and showcase the latest gadgets. As a result, the general public feels compelled to purchase these advertised products, even if they do not genuinely need them.

In conclusion, although social media provides a means for individuals to connect with friends and family, I firmly believe that its excessive use has detrimental effects on our society. These include the loss of privacy and the promotion of consumerism. (214 words)
