Revised:

In recent times, an increasing number of individuals with health-related issues are turning to alternative methods of treatment such as herbal remedies instead of consulting their usual doctors. While these alternative medicines and treatments may have some benefits, I believe that this trend is a negative development because these drugs lack proper prescription, manufacturers have limited knowledge, and treatment areas often lack the necessary equipment.

To begin with, orthodox methods of treating illnesses and the conventional drug supply system can pose various risks. Although they are often more affordable, these methods can be dangerous due to the lack of proper dosage information. Individuals are left to their own devices, guessing the appropriate dosage, which can lead to various health conditions or even death. Moreover, it has been observed that individuals who carry out these treatments or produce these drugs often lack the necessary knowledge and training. Since they did not attend formal schooling for these procedures, they rely on trial and error, putting people's lives at risk.

Furthermore, treatment areas that offer alternative medicines and treatments may not have the necessary equipment to ensure safe and effective procedures. For instance, traditional bone setters manipulate bones without the aid of an X-ray machine, as they simply guess the correct position. This has resulted in numerous complications and puts individuals at risk of losing their limbs or even death.

In conclusion, although financial constraints may lead people to seek alternative medicines and treatments, it is crucial to utilize the resources available effectively. By availing themselves of good hospitals, even those provided by the government for free, people can ensure their safety, avoid drug abuse, and ensure that they are attended to by qualified medical professionals.
