Topic:

"Some say that music, art and drama are as important as other school subjects, especially at the primary level. Do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Some people believe that arts-related subjects are as important as other school subjects, especially for primary school children. I totally agree with this statement because this can help children to discover their talents from an early age and can increase their confidence. 
One of the reasons I agree that creative subjects have the same importance as other school courses in primary school is that it allows students to find out their potential talents early on. That is to say, school-age is the most convenient time for students to learn more about their interests by trying different activities as they are young enough to pursue their hobbies. They will probably not have any other chance later in their lives to discover that because they will be busy with difficult exams when they get older. For example, most famous singers were discovered by their music teachers at school from a young age, and they claimed that they could not be that successful if their teachers did not find out their talents when they were young.
Moreover, music, art and drama subjects help students to boost their confidence. That is because creative lessons teach students how to perform in front of lots of people and give them a chance to socialise with other students. As a result, students can realise their real potential and act more confidently. For instance, many psychologists suggest to students who are struggling with social anxiety to take drama lessons as it helps to enhance confidence. 
In conclusion, this essay completely agrees that music, art and drama have the same value as other subjects in primary school because it allows children to discover their hidden talents early on and increases their self-confidence."


Revised:

Some people believe that subjects related to music, art, and drama are equally important as other school subjects, especially for primary school children. I wholeheartedly agree with this statement because these subjects enable children to discover their talents at an early age and can significantly boost their confidence. 

One reason why I agree that creative subjects hold the same importance as other school courses in primary school is that they allow students to explore their potential talents. The school-age is the most opportune time for students to explore their interests through various activities, as they are young enough to pursue their hobbies. This may be their only chance to discover their talents, as they will later become preoccupied with more demanding academic pursuits. For example, numerous renowned singers were discovered by their music teachers during their early years in school. They assert that they would not have achieved the same level of success if their teachers had not identified their talents at a young age.

Furthermore, music, art, and drama subjects help students develop their self-confidence. Creative lessons teach students how to perform in front of a large audience and provide them with opportunities to socialize with their peers. Hence, students can realize their true potential and exhibit greater confidence. For instance, many psychologists recommend drama lessons to students who struggle with social anxiety as it helps enhance their self-assurance. 

In conclusion, this essay fully supports the notion that music, art, and drama hold the same value as other subjects in primary school. These subjects not only allow children to discover their hidden talents early on but also contribute to their self-confidence.
