For instance, people can read books or news via their phone whenever and wherever they wish as it does not require special lighting."


Revised:

It is widely believed that the popularity of printed newspapers and books will decrease in the future as people will have access to a wide range of online content without any cost. This essay agrees with this viewpoint and will be discussed in further detail.

In today’s digital era, the Internet offers a plethora of options for reading material, making it increasingly convenient for people to access news, books, games, and videos online. The usage of digital platforms is not limited to the younger generation, as even the elderly are becoming more proficient in utilizing online resources. In fact, the majority of book lovers, around 70%, have already embraced e-books, which indicates a potential decline in the demand for printed books.

Apart from its accessibility, digital content presents several advantages over traditional print media. Firstly, online resources are typically available free of charge, making them enticing for readers. Additionally, there is no need to physically visit a shop or spend money on transportation in order to acquire reading material. This convenience is further accentuated by the fact that digital texts can be accessed via smartphones, allowing readers to enjoy books and news at any time and in any place without the need for special lighting.

As a result of the increasing preference for digital text, the market for printed newspapers and books may not remain profitable. More and more people are choosing to consume news and literature through online platforms, making it economically impractical to produce physical copies. This could result in a shift towards the complete dominance of e-books in the near future.

In conclusion, the widespread availability of online content has led to an inclination towards digital platforms for reading materials. Factors such as convenience, cost-effectiveness, and ease of access have led to a decline in the demand for printed newspapers and books. Consequently, it is reasonable to expect that printed media will continue to lose popularity in the future.
