Topic:

"The private car has greatly improved individual freedom of movement and has become a status symbol for many people. Yet the use of private cars has contributed to some of today's most serious problems.

How can the use of private cars be reduced?"

Essay:

"Nowadays owning a car provides you with a lot more freedom due to the fact that you can travel anywhere without experiencing the excessive extra stops public transport offers. some people also view it as a status symbol, especially when you own a German one as they are very exclusive and really expensive. Nevertheless, private automobiles have been the cause of many environmental issues globally, and so, this essay will discuss a couple of solutions that could be implemented to reverse this problem. 

Daily usage of millions of cars brings about environmental issues such as air pollution contributing to breathing problems. Additionally, it also eventually thins out the ozone layer allowing harmful UV rays to enter the atmosphere causing skin cancer. To combat this, firstly, tax zone areas should be implemented in places that have a lot of congestion such as city centres, for instance. The more the public is taxed the less they are inclined to use their cars. 

weight gain is also a problem that comes about from the excessive use of cars. Many citizens that drive everywhere put on weight quicker, America, for example, is one of the nations that have the most morbidly obese residents worldwide. Therefore, I believe if more cities have pedestrian zones, the public would exercise passively as more local attractions would be easily accessible. Thirdly, carbon-free travel, such as trams which run on electricity, contribute to no pollution, will be an excellent addition to public transportation. 

In conclusion, governmental authorities should implement a couple of these easy fixes right away as they would greatly impact the global environment and even people's health positively."


Revised:

Nowadays, owning a car provides individuals with increased freedom of movement, allowing them to travel anywhere without the inconvenience of excessive stops common in public transportation. Additionally, many people view owning a car, particularly a prestigious German-made one, as a status symbol. However, the use of private cars has also contributed significantly to several serious problems. This essay will discuss potential solutions to reduce the use of private cars.

The daily use of millions of cars has led to environmental issues, including air pollution, which contributes to respiratory problems. Moreover, the emissions from cars have been responsible for depleting the ozone layer, enabling harmful UV rays to penetrate the atmosphere and cause skin cancer. To address this, one possible solution involves implementing congestion zones in areas with high traffic, such as city centers. By imposing higher taxes on private car usage, the public would be less inclined to rely on their vehicles.

Another problem associated with excessive car use is weight gain. Many citizens who predominantly rely on cars for transportation tend to gain weight at a faster rate. For instance, the United States has one of the highest rates of obesity globally. To mitigate this issue, increasing the number of pedestrian zones in cities would promote passive exercise and make local attractions easily accessible by foot. This would encourage the public to engage in physical activity as part of their daily routines.

In addition, the adoption of carbon-free travel options, such as electric trams, in public transportation systems would greatly reduce pollution levels. These environmentally friendly alternatives produce no emissions, making them a valuable addition to the transportation infrastructure.

In conclusion, it is crucial for governmental authorities to promptly implement these proposed solutions, as they would significantly improve the global environment and positively impact people's health. By introducing congestion zones, increasing pedestrian zones, and promoting carbon-free travel options, the use of private cars can be effectively reduced. These measures would contribute to a cleaner and healthier future for all.
