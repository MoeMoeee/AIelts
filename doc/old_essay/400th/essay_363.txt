Topic:

"Big salary is much more important than job satisfaction.

Do you agree or disagree?

Provide relevant examples if necessary."

Essay:

"A great number of us believe that a huge, tempting salary is more valuable than being content with our jobs. Indeed, the special time of enjoyment and contentment is when we spend our stacks of cash extravagantly during the holidays. The vast majority does not think for a second that jobs could make them happy. Generations of workers have their minds programmed to bear one thought; that you either have financial welfare or satisfaction with your occupation. Be that as it may, I completely disagree with this common view. One should certainly be enjoying their work, which should simultaneously be their passion. The following paragraphs will explore my personal perspective.

It is undeniable that stability is ensured once an adult secures a position in a high-paying corporation. Precisely, the basic office job which pays the bills, keeps the children’s tummies full, and affords good education. However, why would an individual follow a career path predetermined for them, in which their success has a definite limit? Moreover, most workers are depressed by how they spend their 9 AM-5 PM hours, but are brainwashed into believing it is the best way. In such a scenario, we must take a step back, and question what makes life worth living. A huge portion of one’s week lacks any hint of joy, and that is a conscious choice on their part. While those 40 hours are naturally filled with hard work, one is free to choose whether it is for a desk job for a company that does not care about employees, or for a passion.

Furthermore, large companies justify cruel treatment to their employees due to the amount of money they are paid. The most important lesson I have learnt in my lifetime is that no amount of cash can buy dignity. Thus, everyone should be treated with the utmost respect and consideration. In addition to that, when you have motivation and drive to achieve your dream occupation, you are more likely to. Certainly, you will not be glowing with determination in a position you dislike. Thus, once you do succeed in your preferred path, you kill two birds with one stone. On top of that, remember that life is not worth living without bliss. The fear to dismiss a conventional method is prevalent among society. Nonetheless, anyone brave enough to contradict the rules never regrets it.

In conclusion, it is true that conventional ways guarantee basic life needs and some other things every now and then. Nevertheless, no individual should be held captive by a corporation that redeems them as expendable. Turning your fantasies to reality require resilience and patience, but it is worth it. Thereafter, colors become vibrant as they once were in childhood. Ultimately, I disagree with the perspective of the majority. Bliss is undoubtedly of more value than income."


Revised:

A significant portion of the population believes that a high salary holds more importance than job satisfaction. Their reasoning is based on the notion that a large income allows for extravagant spending and enjoyment during holidays. However, I completely disagree with this viewpoint. In my opinion, it is essential to find joy and contentment in one's profession, which should also align with one's passion. This essay will present my perspective on this matter.

Undoubtedly, a stable income is crucial for meeting basic needs, providing for children, and ensuring a good education. Often, individuals are inclined to follow a predetermined career path to secure financial stability. However, this approach limits their potential for success and may leave them feeling unfulfilled. Many individuals spend their 9 AM-5 PM hours in jobs that offer no satisfaction. Yet, they are persuaded that this is the only way to lead a fruitful life. It is imperative to critically evaluate what truly makes life worth living. Spending a significant portion of our week devoid of joy is a conscious decision. While hard work is essential, one should have the freedom to choose a job that aligns with their passion and brings happiness.

Moreover, large corporations often believe that the high salaries they offer justify harsh treatment towards their employees. However, it is crucial to learn that no amount of money can buy dignity. Every individual deserves to be treated with respect and consideration, regardless of their remuneration. Moreover, having motivation and drive to pursue one's dream occupation increases the likelihood of achieving it. It is highly unlikely to be determined and ambitious in a job that one dislikes. By following one's passion, success in the preferred field can be achieved, leading to personal and professional fulfillment. Additionally, life is incomplete without happiness. While society may discourage deviating from conventional methods, those who have the courage to challenge norms rarely regret their decision.

In conclusion, while it is true that conventional ways of employment can provide for basic life needs and occasional luxuries, individuals should not be bound to corporations that consider them dispensable. Transforming dreams into reality requires perseverance and patience, but the rewards are worth it. Life becomes vibrant and fulfilling, reminiscent of the joy experienced in childhood. Ultimately, I disagree with the majority's perspective, as happiness holds greater value than income.
