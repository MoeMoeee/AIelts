Revised:

In this contemporary era, small-scale shops are rapidly declining. I firmly believe that the main reason for this trend is the emergence of large, luxurious stores that cater to people wanting to flaunt their wealth. This has detrimental effects on the income of small retailers and also poses challenges for individuals with limited spending capacity.

To begin with, a major factor contributing to this decline is the loss of income for small business owners. The enticing advertisements of massive supermarkets influence consumers, resulting in fewer customers visiting small shops and reducing their demand for products. Furthermore, the superior quality of goods available at these larger stores discourages people from buying from small-scale shops. An example of this phenomenon can be seen in a survey conducted in America, where the majority of individuals believe that giant malls are more efficient due to their wide range of products such as clothes, groceries, food items, and accessories. The convenience offered by these malls is a significant factor contributing to the decrease in sales at small markets.

Furthermore, people often choose to shop at large stores in order to showcase their status. These malls provide branded products that are considered fashionable and trendy, as people tend to imitate their peers by shopping at the same stores and spending large amounts of money, solely to maintain a desirable image. This adversely affects those in the community who cannot afford such expensive items. For example, many individuals with credit card limits suffer financial losses due to overspending at bigger stores, creating obstacles for them. Thus, small markets are a better option for purchasing goods in bulk at reasonable prices.

In conclusion, while large malls may offer benefits for purchasing partywear clothes and items for special occasions, small markets are advantageous in many ways. They allow people to save money and maintain a simpler way of life.
