Revised:

In the 21st century, it is difficult to accurately determine the impact of computers on young people's learning. While some argue that computers not only hinder learning but also have negative effects on children’s physical and psychological development, I believe that the effectiveness of computer usage depends on how children utilize them.

On the one hand, when children are taught how to use computers properly, they can offer certain advantages for the learning process. Firstly, computer technology advancements allow people from all walks of life to access traditional education without the limitations of physical factors, such as geographical distance, which was previously challenging or even impossible. Additionally, learning through computers provides flexible study hours, as individuals of all ages can cover learning materials at their own pace. This undoubtedly contributes to their enthusiasm for learning.

On the other hand, it is undeniable that excessive reliance on computers has detrimental effects on children's physical and mental development. From a physical health perspective, long-term and excessive computer usage can lead to obesity due to a sedentary lifestyle. Children who spend significant amounts of time staring at computer screens are at risk of vision problems and back pain. Furthermore, the misuse of computers can also negatively impact children's social lives. Research has shown that heavy computer users are more prone to social isolation as they lack real interactions and a sense of community.

Considering these factors, it can be concluded that the influence of computers on children's development is determined by each individual's approach, which can have both positive and negative aspects. Therefore, moderation is essential.

In conclusion, while computers offer certain advantages for learning, such as facilitating access to education and providing flexible study hours, their misuse can have negative effects on children's physical and mental development. Ultimately, it is crucial to strike a balance and use computers in moderation to ensure a healthy and productive learning environment for children.
