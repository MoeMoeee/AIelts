Topic:

"At present the population of some countries include a relatively large number of young adults, compared with the number of older people.
Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages? Give reasons for your answer with relevant example."

Essay:

"In recent times, some nations have a comparatively large number of young people than the number of older people. Possessing younger people than  older ones has both positive and negative sides as well. However,  positivity never outweighs the negativity. The following essay will explore the two sides and provide a plausible example. 

To start with, there is a saying that young adults are the future of any nation. The contribution of young adults in numerous sectors of the country is huge. Hence, with the help of their strength and ,fitness they easily take the leadership of any sector. For example, the economy, employment, sports and defence cannot be imagined without the performance of young people. Moreover, young people have the courage and motivation to take responsibility which cannot be done by  older people.  For example, a recent study demonstrates that, young people have 60% more stamina and courage than old people. Therefore, countries are fully dependent on the young generation more than the old ones.

Conversely, if the volume of the young generation outweighs the old ones obviously that will have some demerits. Building a nation with both younger and older people is necessary. The old people are the forerunners of  a country and the young generation always follow their footprints. For example, the old generation can easily be friends, philosophers and guides of the young generation. Furthermore, the old generation can help the young generation in learning new things and ideas and share their experiences with the younger generation. Therefore, to build a country both young and old people are required.

To conclude, although the volume of the young generation outweighs the old generation, it does not outweigh the merits of the younger generation. However, to achieve a balanced society and nation there are no alternatives for younger people."


Revised:

In recent times, some nations have a larger proportion of young people compared to older individuals. While having a higher number of younger adults has both advantages and disadvantages, I believe that the positive aspects do not outweigh the negative ones. This essay will discuss the pros and cons of having a larger young population and provide a relevant example.

On one hand, young adults are often seen as the future of a nation. They make significant contributions across various sectors, bringing strength, vitality, and energy to positions of leadership. For instance, the economy, employment, sports, and defense rely heavily on the performance of young individuals. Moreover, young people possess the courage and motivation to take on responsibilities that may be challenging for older generations. Research has shown that young people have 60% more stamina and courage compared to their older counterparts. Therefore, countries heavily depend on the young generation to drive progress and development.

On the other hand, if the number of young people outweighs the older population, there are certain drawbacks. The presence of both young and older individuals is essential for the development of a nation. Older individuals have valuable experience and wisdom, and they can serve as mentors, offering guidance to the younger generation. They can also foster a sense of tradition and continuity within society. Additionally, older individuals can assist the younger generation in learning new things, sharing their experiences, and passing down knowledge. Therefore, a balanced society and nation require the participation of both young and older individuals.

In conclusion, although having a larger proportion of young people compared to older individuals may seem beneficial, the advantages do not outweigh the merits of having a balanced demographic. While young adults play a pivotal role in driving progress, the presence of older individuals is crucial for societal stability and mentorship. Therefore, a mix of both young and older individuals is necessary to achieve a well-rounded society.
