Topic:

"The gap between rich and poor is increasing. What problems does it cause? What solution can you suggest?"

Essay:

"The discrepancy is incrementing between the wealthy and the impoverished people. Due to this, economic prosperity is hampered and education is not conferred to all. To rectify the entire scenario, it is imperative to impart free education and open myriad job avenues.
To embark on, financial progress is halted. As one section of the society earns enormous revenue and pays back to the nation in the form of income tax, the other part of the community hardly generates any income to survive. Thus, contributing none to the national development. To exemplify, as per the World Prosperity Report, the highest deviation between the upper and the lower section of the society has been witnessed in Pakistan, and the nation stands in the first 100 countries to have a poor financial status.
To combat the difference, government should edify the destitude children free of cost. By initiating such a campaign, poverty-stricken children would make the most of it. Hence, education will entitle the populace to grab distinct lucrative opportunities to feed the family; consequently, the entire state would be financially autonomous. Another fruitful way is to start creating more employment options so that none is unemployed, and masses could be a substantial part of national growth. For example, the United Kingdom takes the responsibility to educate each and every child and tops the list of wealthy kingdoms.
To encapsulate, to mitigate the gigantic issue of divergence in the monetary position of the high class and the low class, the administration should take up the onus to bestow every citizen with the fundamental right of knowledge and start up projects to give jobs to the natives; otherwise the repercussion of the disparity, national development, would take a toll."


Revised:

Here are some possible edits to improve the IELTS structure of the essay:

Introduction:
In today's world, there is a growing gap between the rich and the poor, which has many negative consequences for society. The purpose of this essay is to examine these problems and suggest some possible solutions.

Body Paragraph 1:
One major issue caused by income inequality is economic stagnation. When a small group of wealthy individuals hold a significant portion of the nation's wealth, there is less money available for investment in education, infrastructure, and other areas that could benefit the entire population. For example, Pakistan is one country where income inequality is particularly evident, and it is ranked poorly in terms of economic prosperity.

Body Paragraph 2:
Another problem caused by income inequality is lack of equal access to education. Poor families may not have the resources to send their children to school, and this can lead to less opportunities later in life. To address this issue, governments could provide free education to all citizens, regardless of their financial situation. If all children have access to schooling, they will be better equipped to find good jobs and contribute to the economy. For instance, the United Kingdom is considered a wealthy country in part because it has a strong commitment to public education.

Body Paragraph 3:
A third solution to income inequality is to create more job opportunities for everyone. When people have good jobs, they are less likely to live in poverty or rely on government assistance. Governments can encourage job growth by offering incentives to businesses that hire locally, supporting new entrepreneurs, and investing in infrastructure projects that create jobs. By doing so, unemployment rates could decrease and more people could participate in the workforce, leading to a healthier economy overall.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, income inequality is a complex issue that requires a multifaceted approach. By investing in education, job creation, and other solutions, governments can ensure that everyone has an equal chance to succeed. By doing so, the income gap could be narrowed and society as a whole could benefit.
