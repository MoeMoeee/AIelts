Revised:

Age is a crucial factor when it comes to children's education. Some countries advocate for children to start formal education at the age of four, while others believe that children should not begin school until they are seven or eight years old. In my opinion, children should commence their educational journey at the age of seven. At this age, their brains are better equipped for learning, and they are more likely to study effectively. Several reasons support this viewpoint.

Firstly, children at the age of seven possess a higher capacity for learning. They are developmentally ready and capable of understanding concepts more efficiently. With their brains sufficiently developed to differentiate between various situations, they can grasp new information with greater comprehension. This ability to take full advantage of their cognitive abilities is a significant advantage. Starting education at this age enables children to absorb and assimilate knowledge more quickly compared to those who begin formal education at a younger age. Consequently, initiating education at seven offers numerous benefits to children's learning progression.

Moreover, children at this age have the capability to manage stress and behavior while studying. This is of utmost importance since they need to focus solely on their task. Unlike four-year-olds, seven-year-olds are not only better equipped to control their emotions but also demonstrate higher maturity levels in the classroom. As a result, they are better able to manage themselves and concentrate on their lessons. Clearly, children at the age of seven possess the necessary emotional intelligence and self-control to engage in formal education successfully.

On the other hand, in some countries, children are enrolled in formal education as young as four. The idea behind this approach is to motivate children to learn from a young age, allowing them to develop learning skills that will benefit them later in life. However, the age of four should ideally be a period of play and entertainment. The formal learning environment can be overwhelming and overly demanding for children at this age. Therefore, this is not the optimal time to introduce formal education.

In conclusion, children should embark on their formal educational journey when they reach the age of seven. At this age, children have a higher capacity for learning, with their brains being sufficiently developed to comprehend lessons effectively. Additionally, they demonstrate greater maturity and self-control, making them better equipped to handle formal education. On the contrary, children at the age of four lack the necessary mental and emotional development to successfully engage in formal education.
