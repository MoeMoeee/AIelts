Revised:

Nowadays, we are exposed to a wide range of information through social media, newspapers, and television programs. The issue of whether all information should be shared freely or if certain valuable information should be withheld is a topic of debate. In this essay, I will discuss both points of view and present my own opinion.

On one hand, there are proponents of sharing as much information as possible. They argue that free information benefits many individuals, especially in the digital age. Platforms like YouTube and social networking sites provide a plethora of free and valuable information. This enables people from all walks of life to acquire new skills and knowledge without incurring any financial burden. For instance, individuals living in run-down areas and unable to afford private school education can access popular educational content on YouTube. Consequently, access to such information becomes a positive step towards achieving equality in terms of education.

On the other hand, some argue that certain information is too valuable to be shared freely. It is believed that when something is freely available, its worth diminishes. For example, a person who watches a television program on investment strategies may obtain valuable insights that they freely share with friends. However, these insights may lack professional expertise and could potentially mislead others. Hence, some people suggest that sharing such valuable information should come at a cost, ensuring that it is provided by professionals who possess the necessary knowledge and qualifications.

In conclusion, while both viewpoints have their merits, I agree with the notion that information should be shared as much as possible. The availability of information to all individuals allows for an exchange of knowledge and learning opportunities, which can contribute to personal growth and development. However, it is important to discern between valuable and useless information, as not all information holds the same level of significance. Therefore, instead of withholding valuable information, we should focus on raising awareness and educating individuals on how to differentiate between reliable and unreliable sources.
