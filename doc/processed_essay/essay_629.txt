Revised:

Some people argue that visitors to other countries should abide by the customs and behavior of the local culture, while others believe that the host country should embrace cultural differences. This essay will discuss both viewpoints and provide my own perspective.

Firstly, those who advocate for following local customs argue that it promotes the preservation of traditional cultures and helps tourists to experience and integrate into the local environment. By adhering to local customs, tourists have the opportunity to learn new things and avoid any potential risks associated with unfamiliar societal norms. Moreover, tourists may be unaware of crime rates and necessary security measures, making them more vulnerable in an unfamiliar setting. Following local customs can help tourists avoid standing out and becoming targets for criminal activities. For instance, there have been instances of tourists facing theft when they fail to conform to local customs. Thus, observing local customs may provide a sense of safety and facilitate integration.

On the other hand, there are those who argue that embracing cultural differences not only promotes cultural freedom but also drives the economy. By allowing tourists to experience different cultures and traditions, host countries can provide a unique and authentic experience. This can lead to an increase in tourism, foreign exchange, and trade routes, ultimately benefiting the country's economy. In addition, embracing cultural differences can foster cross-cultural understanding and mutual respect. For example, when UAE citizens travel to Pakistan and share their own traditions, it can enrich the local community and create opportunities for cultural exchange.

In my opinion, while it is important to respect and appreciate local customs and behavior, tourists should also adhere to the laws and regulations of the host country. It is crucial to strike a balance between preserving local cultures and ensuring the safety and well-being of tourists. Respecting the local customs is a way to show courtesy and appreciation for the host culture, but it should not justify any actions that are illegal or harmful. Ultimately, it is essential for visitors to engage with the local community while abiding by the rules of the country they are visiting.

In conclusion, there are valid arguments for both views regarding whether visitors should follow local customs or if the host country should embrace cultural differences. I believe that tourists should respect local customs, but they must also commit to following the laws and regulations of the host country. Finding a balance between cultural preservation and ensuring the safety and well-being of both tourists and locals is paramount.
