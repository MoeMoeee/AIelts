Topic:

"In some countries elder people are to work longer and not to retire. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of working beyond retirement age."

Essay:

"As more and more nations age, more and more elderly are encouraged to continue working over retirement age. In my opinion, there are possible benefits and drawbacks of extending individual working life.

On the positive side, working beyond retirement age can be beneficial in the following aspects. Firstly, continuing work improves the elderly's financial condition. More years in the job mean more experience, which in turn means more revenue. For skilled jobs, such as doctors, engineers and professors, people approaching retirement age tend to have rich experience and are also in the highest income period of their career. Furthermore, an elder person may find that having a job provides a sense of satisfaction. This is because people who have jobs have a sense of identity and feel they are part of society, which is necessary to maintain a good mental state. Not only that, but the active movement a job provides could help keep one’s body healthy. As physical exercise is important for everyone, especially for the elderly.

However, there are possible influences if people work after the average expected years. Obviously, it could damage their health condition. The older a human is, the more rest they need. When the elders do not get enough time to recover, it will pose a huge burden on  their bodies, leading to a series of physical problems, even death. Another disadvantage is that the interpersonal relationship can be affected. After investing so many years of one’s life into a career, they deserve a life of leisure and family happiness. If they are still occupied with a job, there will be less time to do what they love to do and enjoy with their family. In that case, some of them may develop a lonely lifestyle. 

In conclusion, there are benefits and risks to motivating old people to continue their careers. It could maintain health and acquire a sense of fulfilment, meanwhile, it could also result in more exhaustion and solitude. For those who continue to work beyond retirement age, it may be helpful to replace manual work with mental work as much as possible."


Revised:

In some countries, elderly individuals are being encouraged to work longer and postpone their retirement. While there are advantages to extending one's working life, there are also potential disadvantages. This essay will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of working beyond retirement age.

On the positive side, one advantage of continuing to work after retirement age is the improvement in the financial situation of elderly individuals. With more years of work experience, their income can increase, especially in skilled professions such as doctors, engineers, and professors. These individuals are likely to be in the highest earning phase of their careers approaching retirement age. Additionally, having a job can provide a sense of satisfaction and fulfillment for older individuals. It gives them a sense of identity and the feeling of being a productive member of society, which is important for mental well-being. Moreover, remaining active through work can contribute to physical health as it often involves regular movement and exercise, which is particularly beneficial for the elderly.

However, working beyond retirement age can also have negative consequences. One significant drawback is the potential negative impact on health. As individuals age, they typically require more rest and recovery time. When the elderly are not given enough time to recharge and recuperate, it can lead to physical strain and a host of health problems, including the risk of premature death. Another disadvantage is the impact on interpersonal relationships. After dedicating many years to the workforce, elderly individuals deserve a period of leisure and family happiness. If they continue to work, they will have less time to engage in activities they enjoy and spend quality time with their loved ones. This can result in loneliness and a decreased quality of life.

In conclusion, there are both benefits and drawbacks to elderly individuals working beyond retirement age. On one hand, it can improve financial stability and provide a sense of fulfillment, as well as contribute to physical health. On the other hand, it may have negative impacts on health and interpersonal relationships. It may be advantageous for those choosing to work beyond retirement age to focus on mentally stimulating tasks rather than manual labor as much as possible. Nevertheless, it is crucial for society to consider and respect the choice and well-being of elderly individuals as they navigate their later years in the workforce.
