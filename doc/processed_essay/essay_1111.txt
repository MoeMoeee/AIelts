Topic:

"Nowadays, women, as well as men, work full-time. Therefore, women and men should share household tasks equally. (e.g. cleaning and looking after children). To what extent do you agree or disagree?
"

Essay:

"Recent trends show that both men and women are working full-time compared to older days. Some people think that men cannot do most of the household tasks, whereas others argue that men should equally do work at home to help women. Balancing the household tasks equally for both is always debatable. 

To begin with, men always feel that they are responsible for managing the economic situation of the family such as daily needs, expenses, children's education and financial growth. So this contributes to men's mindset to focus on the financial stability of the family. Although women are responsible for children's needs, cooking, washing clothes and cleaning etc. For example these factor affects the recent days, where women are equally contributing to the family by working full time and managing household work is quite difficult these days. 

On the other hand, mature people think that they are equally responsible for household work to help women to be more balanced inside and outside the home. These people help out with cleaning, teaching kids, washing clothes, etc. where women need help with their day-to-day activities. Hence balancing  work at home would help to have a peaceful and understanding life between couples to avoid misunderstandings or conflicts. My colleagues experienced problems when they do not share the workload at home due to women facing more workloads and suffering. 

To conclude, I strongly agree that men should equally share the tasks with women at home to lead a peaceful and healthy life. With the growing culture of women working full-time and it would continue for future years, men should understand the difficulty and support women the most in the living location.
"


Revised:

Recent trends show that both men and women are increasingly working full-time jobs compared to the past. There is a growing belief that household duties should be shared equally among men and women, including cleaning and looking after children. In my opinion, I agree with this notion to a great extent.

It is often argued that men are primarily responsible for managing the family's economic situation, such as daily needs, expenses, children's education, and financial growth. This mindset may lead men to prioritize their focus on achieving financial stability for the family. On the other hand, women are typically burdened with responsibilities related to children's needs, cooking, washing clothes, and cleaning. However, with both men and women working full-time nowadays, managing household chores can be challenging and overwhelming for women.

On the contrary, mature individuals believe that household tasks should be evenly distributed to help ease the burden on women and create a sense of balance for everyone involved. Sharing the responsibilities of cleaning, teaching kids, and doing laundry, among other things, can provide valuable support for women in their day-to-day activities. Based on personal experiences shared by some of my colleagues, problems often arise when the workload at home is not shared equally. This can result in increased stress levels for women, leading to unhappiness and potential conflicts within the family.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that men should equally share the household tasks with women in order to foster a peaceful and harmonious family life. With the ongoing trend of women working full-time jobs, it is essential for men to understand and appreciate the challenges that women face in balancing work and home responsibilities. By shouldering the responsibilities together, couples can cultivate a healthier and more fulfilling relationship.
