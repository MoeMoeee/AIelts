Topic:

"Many students find it difficult to concentrate or pay attention at school. What are the reasons? What could be done to solve this problem?"

Essay:

"Some people argue that most students have difficulty focusing on classes. This essay will examine the main reasons for this argument and possible solutions to it.

Most of the students wake up very early for their busy schedules and feel sleepy in the first classes at school which makes it really hard to focus on any subject. Furthermore, there are generally many long and sequential course sessions, with boring and unattractive formats in general, with little breaks in between, which disables the students to recharge and energize herself/himself to get ready to learn new subjects. For instance, researchers published that teenagers' focus time is only 16 minutes, after that they got either distracted or bored. In that case, it does not make sense to hold up 40 mins sessions. In addition, technological devices such as tablets and smartphones are the top distractions, even one notification on the phone is enough to ruin the concentration of the students.

A possible solution can be a new arrangement of the time and content schedules at schools considering the new generations' challenges and advantages. Maybe arranging shorter sessions with more little breaks can help students to concentrate better. Besides, the formats of the contents might be more fun with gamification and usage of more attractive visuals and experiments if applicable. Another solution could be prohibiting smart devices throughout the learning time which blocks the possibility of being disturbed by them when focused.

In conclusion, the misarrangement of time and content format and smart devices can cause students to lose their focus, even though rearrangement of the school days and prohibiting smart devices can help the problem."


Revised:

Many students struggle with concentration and attention in school. This essay will discuss the main reasons for this issue and propose potential solutions.

One reason why students have difficulty focusing is due to their early morning schedules. Many students have packed routines and may feel tired and sleepy during their first classes, making it challenging to pay attention. Furthermore, the long and continuous nature of class sessions, coupled with uninspiring and monotonous teaching methods, contribute to students becoming disengaged. Without adequate breaks to recharge, students find it hard to absorb new information. Research has even shown that teenagers' attention span is typically only 16 minutes, after which they become easily distracted or bored. Therefore, it is impractical to have 40-minute sessions without incorporating regular breaks. Moreover, the prominence of technological devices, such as tablets and smartphones, poses a significant distraction. Even a single notification can disrupt a student's concentration.

To address these issues, one possible solution is to revise the school schedule and content arrangement to better cater to the challenges and preferences of the current generation. Shorter sessions with more frequent breaks could help students maintain their focus. Additionally, incorporating elements of gamification, attractive visuals, and hands-on experiments, where applicable, can make the content more engaging and enjoyable for students. Another potential solution is to enforce a ban on the use of smart devices during learning hours, preventing students from being distracted by notifications or other digital temptations.

In conclusion, the mismanagement of time, teaching methods, and the presence of smart devices are key factors contributing to students' difficulty in concentrating at school. However, by redesigning the school day, utilizing interactive teaching techniques, and implementing restrictions on smart device usage, the issue can be mitigated and student focus can be improved.
