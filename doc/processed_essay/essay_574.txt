Revised:

Many people believe that violence depicted on television and in computer games has a detrimental effect on society, while others argue that these factors have no significant influence on people's behavior. In my opinion, I believe that electronic media does indeed have negative effects on the community.

On one hand, it is evident that technology has provided a way for individuals to unwind and relax by watching television shows and playing video games. For instance, retired individuals who have ample amounts of free time often find solace in engaging dramas or movies that entertain and promote mental health. Additionally, children who play video games on electronic devices not only find them entertaining but also develop problem-solving skills that are applicable in real-life situations. Hence, playing video games can have long-term benefits in terms of cognitive development.

On the other hand, many television channels are not held accountable for the content they broadcast. Their primary goal is to attract viewership, rather than provide quality shows that can educate and promote intelligent thinking. Furthermore, excessive indulgence in video games can have a range of negative consequences, particularly for children. It is important to note that addiction can stem from various sources, and video games that are played irresponsibly can lead to a lack of focus on essential tasks such as completing homework, studying, and engaging with peers.

In conclusion, while there may be some benefits to activities such as watching television and playing video games, it is imperative to approach them with caution. Engaging in these activities excessively can lead to adverse effects. Therefore, it is important for individuals to exercise moderation and ensure that these forms of electronic media are enjoyed responsibly.
