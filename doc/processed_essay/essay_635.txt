Topic:

"Some pepple claim that not enough of the waste from home is recycled. They say that the only way to increase recycling is for governments to make it a legal requirement. To what do you think laws are needed to make people recycle more of their waste?"

Essay:

"During the contemporary era, preserving the environment is one of the key issues which seriously challenges our society. Any environmental can raise an argument, and we are facing one problem which is recycling household waste. Some people believe that the only way to boost recycling depends on legal requirements from the authorities. In this essay, I will discuss my agreement on imposing laws for household waste recycling. 

Firstly, taking Singapore a sound example of a thriving country with minimal street rubbish and household pollutants. In fact, they impose a certain number of regulations for waste, especially household waste. For instance, Singaporean residents or even visitors can be fined 2000 dollars per household if they tend to not categorize their family waste or not recycle bottles/food into certain bins. 

Secondly, one of the advantageous aspects of imposing laws is to enforce the importance of protecting the environment, recycling in particular. Most Asian people, for instance, seem to be indifferent to daily activities if those are not required by legislation. However, the necessity of the government is not only about punishments, but it is also related to setting some motivations for people and changing their mindsets based on a variety of weekly regulations or mandatory activities such as public recycling days, a bottle of litter, and so on. 

In conclusion, the escalating volume of ordinary waste poses a significant concern for our society. To address this issue, implementing recycling laws by governments emerges as an ideal solution. By drawing inspiration from successful models like Singapore and combining it with strategies to promote positive behavior change, we can ease the burden of waste on our environment."


Revised:

During the modern era, environmental preservation has become a pressing issue that challenges our society. One particular problem we face is the inadequate recycling of household waste. Some people argue that the only way to increase recycling is through government-mandated laws. In this essay, I will discuss why I agree that laws are necessary to encourage people to recycle more of their waste.

Firstly, Singapore serves as a prime example of a thriving country with minimal street trash and household pollutants. In this nation, there are regulations in place for waste management, particularly for household waste. For instance, Singaporean residents and even visitors can be fined $2,000 per household if they fail to properly categorize their waste or neglect to recycle items such as bottles or food waste.

Secondly, one of the main advantages of implementing laws is that it enforces the importance of protecting the environment, with recycling being a specific focus. Many individuals in Asian countries, for instance, may show indifference towards daily activities if they are not legally required. However, governmental intervention is not solely about imposing punishments. It also involves providing motivation for people to change their attitudes towards recycling through a range of initiatives, such as regular public recycling days or incentives for picking up litter.

In conclusion, the escalating amount of non-recycled waste is a significant concern for our society. To tackle this issue, it is crucial for governments to introduce recycling laws. Taking inspiration from successful models like Singapore, addressing this problem can be accomplished by combining legislation with strategies aimed at promoting positive behavioral change. By doing so, we can alleviate the environmental burden caused by waste.
