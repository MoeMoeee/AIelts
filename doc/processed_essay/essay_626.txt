Topic:

"If a product is good or it meets people's needs, people will buy it so advertising is unnecessary.

To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"There is a prevalent argument suggesting that advertising becomes unnecessary when merchandise possesses commendable conditions or effectively meets the demands of consumers. From my perspective, I strongly dissent from this perspective as it also facilitates the acquisition of new customers, enhances the level of competition, and disseminates information to society regarding the company's social initiatives.

One compelling justification for my perspective is that advertising plays a pivotal role in enabling companies to expand their market share. Advertisements play a crucial role in enhancing the visibility and perception of products or services, thereby increasing consumer awareness. It is important to note that the high quality of a product does not automatically result in widespread consumer awareness throughout the entire market. For example, a bakery specializing in the sale of delectable banh mi sandwiches may primarily enjoy a reputation among its local clientele. However, through the utilization of social network advertising, it has the potential to draw the attention of discerning food enthusiasts from various parts of the city.

One additional rationale for my dissent is that advertising significantly contributes to market competition. This phenomenon can be attributed to the inherent tendency of individuals to consistently pursue superior options for consumption. Due to this rationale, comprehensive advertising campaigns possess the ability to educate consumers about the offerings of a particular business and allure them towards transitioning their brand preferences. An additional explanation for my dissent lies in the utilization of advertising as a means to disseminate the social endeavours undertaken by corporations. In order to contribute to the betterment of society, individuals are encouraged to engage in the purchase of goods and services from companies that are recognized for their commitment to social responsibility. Vinmart, a prominent agency, experienced a notable increase in customer footfall in the subsequent months following a marketing campaign that publicized its initiative to offer red meals to underprivileged individuals amidst the COVID-19 pandemic.

In summary, there is a prevalent belief that advertising is superfluous when the quality of a product is deemed satisfactory. I vehemently disagree with this proposition due to the fact that advertising fulfils important objectives such as augmenting market share, facilitating competition with rival brands, and bolstering sales by informing the public about the enterprise's social initiatives. I would like to propose that companies consistently incorporate advertising initiatives into their commercial pursuits, irrespective of the exceptional quality of their products."
