"17.Some people believe that there should be fixed punishments for each type of crime. Others, however, argue that the circumstances of an individual crime , and the motivation for commiting it, should always be taken into account when deciding on the punishment. Discuss both these views and give your own opinion."

Essay:

"The allocation of punishment for criminals has been a highly debated topic. Many intellectuals are in favour of fixed sentences for different crimes, while others believe that judicial systems should take into account all the elements of a crime and sentence accordingly. This article presents both perspectives and elaborates on mine.

Firstly, the previous perspective places significant importance on pre-determined sentences for different criminal offences. This optimistic view is leading to a lack of diversity in punishments. As a result, the time-consuming judicial system will become more efficient, and the crime rate will be suppressed to a great extent. For example, fixed penalties can help create a fair work environment, which would boost public trust in the law. Therefore, by implementing a common policy for all criminals, the crime rate will decrease.

On the other hand, the latter view paints a picture of the flexibility of the legislative system for various crimes. This view focuses more on the seriousness of the crime, and therefore, the criminal should be punished. In addition, the circumstances and motives of the crime scene are also considered in the judgment. Although this type of jurisdiction is time-consuming, it can save innocent people. For example, a murder and an intentional assassination committed by a person in self-defence require different perspectives and punishments. Therefore, dynamic laws can effectively mitigate potential crimes.

In summary, the sentence regarding criminal activity has a significant impact on criminal behaviour. In my opinion, the dynamic characteristics of the jurisdiction are more favourable for society to effectively curb criminal activity."

Title: Fixed Punishments vs. Consideration of Circumstances in Criminal Sentencing

Introduction (Paraphrase the topic and provide an overview of the essay)

The allocation of punishments for criminals has long been a subject of debate. While some argue for fixed sentences for each type of crime, others contend that the circumstances and motivations of an individual crime should be taken into account when deciding on the appropriate punishment. This essay will explore both perspectives and present my own viewpoint.

Body Paragraph 1 (Supporting the first perspective)

One viewpoint advocates for predetermined punishments for different criminal offences. This approach promotes efficiency within the judicial system, leading to a reduction in the crime rate. By implementing a common policy for all criminals, the time-consuming nature of the judicial process would be minimized. Ensuring a fair work environment through fixed penalties also fosters public trust in the law. Consequently, a decrease in criminal activity can be achieved.

Body Paragraph 2 (Supporting the second perspective)

Conversely, another perspective highlights the importance of considering the specific circumstances and motives behind a crime when determining the appropriate punishment. This approach emphasizes the severity of the crime, ensuring that the punishment matches the gravity of the offense. By taking into account the unique factors surrounding a crime, such as the motive or self-defense, the judicial system can deliver more just outcomes. Although this approach may be time-consuming, it helps safeguard the rights of innocent individuals. Utilizing dynamic laws and flexible sentencing can effectively deter potential criminals.

Conclusion (Summarize the main points and state your opinion)

In conclusion, the severity of criminal activity heavily depends on the punishments allocated. While fixed punishments offer efficiency and fairness, considering the circumstances and motivations behind a crime allows for the delivery of more just outcomes. Personally, I believe that a more dynamic approach to sentencing is more favorable, as it effectively curbs criminal activity and ensures the protection of innocent individuals' rights. By striking a balance between fixed punishments and the consideration of individual circumstances, the judicial system can better serve society at large.
