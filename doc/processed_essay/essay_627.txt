Topic:

"26.In some countries the average weight of people is increasing and their levels of health and fitness are decreasing. What do you think are the causes of these problems and what measures could be taken to solve them?"

Essay:

"The average weight of people in some countries is increasing, while their physical fitness is decreasing. The reason for this problem will be explained in the following article, and I believe there are some practical solutions to fix it.

On the other hand, there are several reasons why people gain weight. First and foremost, unhealthy eating is caused by various reasons such as long working hours and the high cost of preparing healthy food. In other words, since people now work more than 8 hours a day, they do not have extra time to cook at home and therefore do not eat homemade food. Additionally, healthy meals can be costly in certain countries as not everyone is able to afford them. Therefore, they prefer to eat cheap junk food. Additionally, nowadays, due to changes in people's preferences, the amount of exercise is decreasing. For example, they tend to commute by car instead of walking.

On the other hand, both governments and individuals can solve this problem. For example, countries can impose a tax on junk food to decrease its consumption. Additionally, it can also subsidize the cost of healthy food. In addition, individuals should try making lifestyle changes to maintain physical fitness in different ways, such as incorporating walking into their daily routine, to compensate for the lack of adequate exercise.

All in all, the increase in society's average weight results in a decrease in their health and fitness levels. In my opinion, increasing taxes on junk food, providing more subsidies for healthy foods, and promoting individual lifestyle changes can be considered  solutions to this issue."


Revised:

"The average weight of people in some countries is increasing, while their levels of health and fitness are decreasing. In this essay, the causes of these problems will be discussed, followed by an examination of possible measures to address them.

Unhealthy eating habits contribute significantly to the increase in average weight. One reason is the long working hours that people in many countries have to endure. These demanding schedules often leave individuals with limited time to prepare home-cooked meals, leading them to rely on convenient, but often unhealthy, fast food options. Moreover, the cost of purchasing and preparing healthy food can be prohibitive for many individuals. As a result, they opt for cheaper, less nutritious alternatives. Another factor to consider is the dwindling levels of physical activity. With modern lifestyles, people increasingly choose to drive instead of engaging in activities such as walking or cycling.

Both governments and individuals can play a role in addressing these issues. Governments have the power to implement policy measures that can curtail the consumption of unhealthy foods. One effective strategy is to impose higher taxes on junk food, making it less affordable and thereby reducing its demand. Additionally, governments can provide subsidies to promote the consumption of healthy food, making it more accessible and affordable for the general population. Simultaneously, individuals can take personal responsibility by adopting healthier lifestyles. Encouraging habits such as incorporating walking or biking into daily routines can help compensate for the lack of exercise and contribute to improved levels of fitness.

In conclusion, the increasing average weight and declining fitness levels in certain countries are attributed to a combination of factors including unhealthy eating habits and reduced physical activity. However, there are practical solutions that can be implemented. Governments can discourage the consumption of unhealthy food by imposing higher taxes while also encouraging the consumption of healthy alternatives by providing subsidies. At the individual level, incorporating physical activities into daily routines can help combat the detrimental effects of sedentary lifestyles. By addressing these issues collectively, it is possible to reverse the trend of declining health and fitness levels in affected countries."
