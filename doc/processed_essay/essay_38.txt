Topic:

"Atomic energy is becoming more and more popular energy source. However, some people argue that nuclear power puts life on the Earth to danger.
What is your opinion?"

Essay:

"As atomic energy becomes more commonly used in many nations, people start to be aware of its threats to human civilization. From my perspective, despite the high-cost construction and possible dangers, I am certain that the benefits, which are sustainable and environmentally friendly, outweigh the drawbacks it exerts.

To begin with, a newly emerged source of power exerts positive impacts on humans and the environment. It is a universal consensus that nuclear power could play an essential role in the energy supply and does not exist in a limited amount like coal or gas. Therefore, after precious minerals being entirely depleted, people can replace them with atomic power, which is sustainable over centuries. Moreover, possibly the immense advantage of nuclear power is that it is eco-friendly energy. Unlike fossil fuels, the nuclear reactor does not create air pollution or release greenhouse gases to mitigate climate change.

Meanwhile, many people are afraid of the dangers of nuclear plants, such as Japan's nuclear disaster in 2011. In addition, investors claim that atomic plant construction costs a fortune and is a waste of money. However, I think those thoughts are rather ill-founded as the facts are opposite. When running a nuclear plant, scientists usually take accurate measures to ensure the regular operation and prevent any potential risks. Also, the dreadful disaster occurring in Japan was a mere accident, and no similar disaster was recorded until now. Additionally, despite the high-cost construction, nuclear plants are inexpensive to operate, which means it is a long-term benefit. As a result, it would be unwise to abandon this alternative energy.

In conclusion, nuclear power not only sustains along with the development of the human race, which can fulfill global needs, but also releases unpolluted waste to the environment. Although people think that such alternative energy may cause new dangers and is expensive to invest in, it is hard to deny these overwhelming advantages."


Revised:

As atomic energy becomes increasingly popular worldwide, concerns about its potential dangers to human civilization are being raised. In my opinion, despite the high costs of construction and potential hazards, the benefits of nuclear power, which are both sustainable and environmentally friendly, outweigh the drawbacks.

To begin with, the emergence of nuclear power has had positive impacts on both humans and the environment. It is widely recognized that nuclear power could play a crucial role in meeting the growing global energy demands, as it is not limited in supply like coal or gas. Consequently, when these precious resources become exhausted, atomic power can serve as a viable substitute, providing sustainable energy for centuries. Another significant advantage of nuclear power is its eco-friendly nature. Unlike fossil fuels, nuclear reactors do not emit harmful pollutants or contribute to climate change, making it a cleaner and greener energy source.

However, many people express concerns about the dangers associated with nuclear plants, such as the Fukushima nuclear disaster in Japan in 2011. Additionally, there are arguments that building these plants is extremely costly and a waste of money. Nevertheless, I consider such opinions to be unfounded, as they do not align with the actual facts. When operating a nuclear plant, scientists carefully implement various safety measures to ensure its smooth functioning and minimize potential risks. It should also be noted that the Fukushima incident was an unfortunate accident, and similar disasters have not been documented since then. Furthermore, while the initial construction costs are high, nuclear power plants are relatively inexpensive to operate in the long run, making them a beneficial and sustainable investment. Therefore, it would be imprudent to dismiss nuclear power as an alternative energy source.

In conclusion, nuclear power not only meets the energy demands of a growing human population in a sustainable manner but also leaves behind a minimal environmental footprint. Despite concerns regarding potential new dangers and the high costs of construction, the benefits of nuclear power cannot be overlooked. Overall, it is clear that the advantages of atomic energy outweigh any potential drawbacks.
