For instance, if a Chinese client wishes to do business with an Indian individual, it may be challenging for the Indian person to communicate effectively in Chinese. Thus, placing too much emphasis on English as the primary foreign language in every school may not be practical.

In conclusion, it is my belief that while English should not be the sole language taught in every school, it is important for schools to incorporate basic English education into their curriculum. This will ensure that students have a basic understanding of English and can communicate comfortably in English-speaking countries if necessary.
