Topic:

"Some people believe that it is more important to teach secondary/high school students literature of their own country, while others think that it is more important to study literature from all over the world. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Literature has a great influence on our moral values, cultural awareness, perception of other people and our range of interests. Thus, some people suppose that it is essential to read and understand the writings of their nation country, while another group of individuals believe that studying literature from all over the world is extremely important. I think that a knowledge of huge different kinds of compositions from different countries has a great impact on our life. 

First of all, poetry and prose from our own country help young people to dive into the world of historical values and traditions of their country and deeply understand different events in the past. Knowing the history of their own country, individuals are able to prevent the same mistakes in the present. For instance, knowledge about some problems with the export of different types of products in the past could help with selling these goods nowadays. 

On the other hand, if students are taught international writing, they become more culturally aware, which is crucial nowadays. Understanding a system of moral values and national traditions of different countries has immense benefits for all individuals, like visiting a large number of countries, having an international business and working at international companies. Besides, reading foreign literature can broaden our horizons and make people more intelligent and creative. 

To conclude, I believe that a knowledge of a wide range of writings is vital for all individuals due to the fact that people are becoming more culturally aware and intelligent. Moreover, international study, work and business require an understanding of the traditions of this country."

Title: The Importance of Studying Literature from Both Local and Global Perspectives

Introduction

Literature plays a significant role in shaping our moral values, cultural awareness, perception of others, and expanding our range of interests. While some argue for the importance of focusing on the literary works of one's own country, others believe that studying literature from all over the world is equally crucial. This essay will explore both perspectives before providing a personal opinion.

Body Paragraph 1

Proponents of studying literature from one's own country argue that it enables students to immerse themselves in the historical values and traditions of their nation. By delving into the writings of their country, students develop a deeper understanding of past events, thereby preventing the repetition of mistakes in the present. For example, knowledge about historical problems related to the export of goods can inform current trade practices, facilitating smarter decisions in the global market.

Body Paragraph 2

However, there are strong arguments in favor of studying literature from various countries. Exploring international literature broadens students' cultural awareness, which is essential in today's interconnected world. Familiarity with different systems of moral values and national traditions offers numerous advantages, such as the ability to thrive in diverse environments, engage in international business, and work for multinational corporations. Furthermore, exposure to foreign literature can enhance intelligence and creativity, expanding one's intellectual horizons.

Conclusion

In conclusion, embracing a wide range of literary works is essential for individuals in today's globalized society. Cultivating cultural awareness and intelligence brings immense personal and professional benefits. Consequently, a comprehensive understanding of both local and global literature is indispensable for success in various endeavors, including international studies, work, and business ventures.
