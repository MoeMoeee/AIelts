Essay:

Imagine lying in your comfortable bed, wearing comfy pajamas, and buying that gorgeous jacket from eBay or completing your office assignment drowned in piles of paperwork, still getting your favorite sandwich from Subway. That's how convenient and fast life is with online shopping facilities. People now have a whole bunch of brands in their gadgets, be it ZARA, NIKE, or Pizza Hut. You name it, and there's an app waiting to be downloaded on your phone or tablet. A few clicks, and you have that newly launched pair of shoes from ZARA at your doorstep. Online marketplaces have not only made shopping easier and faster, but they have also made the lives of introverts a lot more comfortable. At least now we do not have to feel awkward when a sales staff member is glued to us while choosing items. Most people do not like to visit physical outlets due to various factors such as traffic and parking issues, extensive physical activity for elderly people, and crowded outlets. Having the best available products at your fingertips is always a pleasure.

The growth of virtual marketplaces and online stores is turning out to be the biggest reason for the closure of shops and stores. The primary reason is the cost-to-benefit ratio. Running physical shops and stores incurs extensive overhead costs, such as lighting, music, staff costs, interior design, and rent. Regardless of whether 1 or 100 customers visit on a daily basis, the store has to bear fixed costs, which leads to declining profits. Selling online is way cheaper as it offers the flexibility of on-point service. When a customer is genuinely interested, they can visit the virtual store and make a purchase. Companies also employ bots to handle the queries and complaints of online customers, which helps provide enhanced customer service. When there are no massive fixed costs attached, the profits are higher in comparison.

One real example of the dissolution of physical outlets is FOREVER 21. This apparel brand has shut down its stores and is now solely focusing on online sales. A few more major examples of online shopping's boost are Amazon and Ali Express. These tech giants have challenged and disrupted the idea of having physical stores.

In my opinion, online shopping is the future, and brands should now adapt to the changing environment; otherwise, they will be left as devastated as BlackBerry was when touchscreen smartphones came into power. 

To conclude, the growth of online shopping has had a significant impact on the closure of physical shops and stores. The convenience, cost-effectiveness, and wide variety of options offered by online shopping have made it increasingly popular. Brands who fail to adapt to this changing trend are likely to suffer the consequences.
