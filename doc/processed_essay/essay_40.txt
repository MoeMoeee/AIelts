Revised:

It is commonly believed that children nowadays face increasing pressure from academic, social, and commercial perspectives. This essay aims to discuss the causes of these pressures and suggest measures to alleviate them. 

One prominent cause of academic pressure on children is the intense competition they encounter. In today's globalized world, children are not only competing with their peers from their own neighborhood or town, but also with children from around the world. As a result, parents often expect their children to excel in all areas, which leads to excessive academic workload. For instance, many children are enrolled in specialized schools that burden them with school bags weighing more than their own bodies. Additionally, students often attend extra tuition classes after school, leaving them with little time to explore other interests and activities. 

Another major source of pressure for children, particularly teenagers, is the social and commercial pressure they experience. Modern teenagers tend to rebel against traditional values, resulting in constant disagreements with their parents, which further adds to their stress. Moreover, children may face difficulties dealing with their peers, some of whom may bully or exclude them. This can lead to feelings of anxiety and depression. The influence of commercialism also puts pressure on children as they see their peers owning the latest technological gadgets, but not every family can afford such luxuries. Consequently, these unattainable desires can lead children to feel left out and inadequate. 

To address these issues, it is crucial that we protect children's innocent childhood and well-being. Firstly, adults should assist children in discovering their true passions and talents. By guiding them towards their interests, children can experience a sense of fulfillment and develop a well-rounded personality. Additionally, it is essential to create a supportive environment at home and school. Parents should foster open communication and understanding with their children, respecting their opinions and encouraging independence. Similarly, schools should provide a nurturing atmosphere that emphasizes personal growth rather than solely focusing on academic achievement. 

Furthermore, efforts must be made to address the negative influence of commercialism on children. Society should encourage parents to teach their children the value of money and the importance of contentment. Additionally, educational institutions should promote values such as empathy, compassion, and inclusivity to combat peer pressure and bullying. 

In conclusion, the increasing pressures faced by children in academic, social, and commercial aspects can have detrimental effects on their overall well-being. Parents, schools, and society must work together to alleviate these pressures by guiding children towards their true passions, fostering supportive environments, and addressing the negative influence of commercialism. Only then can we ensure that children can enjoy their innocent childhood and achieve holistic development.
