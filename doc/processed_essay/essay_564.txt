Revised:

Nowadays, the world has changed tremendously from the past, but some people still cling to their traditions. In certain countries, it is common for individuals to live with their parents and siblings until they reach old age. In my opinion, while there may be some advantages to this behavior, the disadvantages outweigh them.

On the one hand, there are various advantages to living with parents until a certain age. Firstly, residing with parents can bring them happiness, particularly when accompanied by grandchildren. The close proximity allows for stronger family bonds, fostering a sense of support and togetherness. Additionally, it can alleviate feelings of sadness and loneliness that may arise when one lives far away from their parents. Furthermore, staying with parents provides an opportunity for younger generations to learn about past traditions and values. For instance, in some cultures, elderly individuals pass on their knowledge of how life was in the past and how they adapted to it. Lastly, living with parents can be advantageous if they become ill, as adult children can take care of them and provide the necessary support.

However, on the other hand, there are significant disadvantages to this arrangement. Firstly, living with siblings for an extended period of time can lead to conflicts and arguments. Family issues have been proven to be a leading cause of divorce in some countries. Additionally, when individuals continue to live with their parents, they may become overly dependent and lack the skills necessary to live independently. This can hinder personal growth and self-reliance, preventing individuals from taking on responsibilities and developing a sense of autonomy. Furthermore, not being seen as dependable or responsible can damage an individual's reputation within their community.

In conclusion, while there are advantages to living with parents and siblings until old age, the disadvantages outweigh them. The conflicts that can arise from living with siblings and the hindrance to personal growth and independence make it less favorable. It is important for individuals to learn to live independently, take on responsibilities, and contribute to society.
