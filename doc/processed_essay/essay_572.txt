Revised:

Nowadays, there is a debate regarding whether or not school children should be given homework by their teachers. Some argue that homework is crucial for a child's education, while others believe that children should be allowed time to play outdoor games and spend quality time with their families. In this essay, I will discuss both views before presenting my own opinion.

On the one hand, homework undoubtedly plays an important role in a child's academic development. By completing homework assignments, students have the opportunity to review and reinforce what they have learned in the classroom. This helps them to solidify their understanding of various subjects. Additionally, homework allows parents to be more involved in their child's education, as they can assist them in completing their assignments and track their progress. For example, if a certain chapter is covered at school and students are given related questions to answer as homework, they can revise the material and parents can actively participate in this process. As a result, children become more engaged and committed to their academic performance.

On the other hand, there are valid arguments in favor of minimizing homework for the well-being of children. It is crucial for parents and teachers to encourage youngsters to participate in physical activities and engage in extracurricular pursuits. This is because physical fitness is a crucial aspect of overall well-being, and it helps to promote mental strength. By allowing children more free time after school, they have the opportunity to relax and engage in activities that promote their overall growth and development. For instance, instead of burdening children with excessive homework, we should allocate quality time to spend with them at home, enabling them to unwind and foster healthy relationships with their family members.

In conclusion, while there are merits to both sides of the argument, I believe that it is important to strike a balance between homework and other aspects of a child's life. Homework serves as a valuable tool for reinforcing learning and involving parents in their child's education. However, it is equally important to allow children time to participate in physical activities and spend quality time with their families. Therefore, schools should ensure that they do not overload students with excessive homework, but rather focus on supporting their overall development, including values such as integrity and kindness. After all, schools are not only responsible for academics but for nurturing well-rounded individuals.
