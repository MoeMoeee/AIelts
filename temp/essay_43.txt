Revised:
In the 21st century, assessing the impact of computers on young people is undoubtedly challenging. While some argue that computers not only hinder learning productivity but also have adverse effects on a child's physical and psychological development, I am convinced that the effectiveness of computers depends on how children utilize them.

On the one hand, if children are taught to use computer functions properly, it can undeniably offer benefits in the learning process. Firstly, due to advancements in computer technology, individuals from all backgrounds now have access to traditional education without the constraints of physical factors like geographical distance, which was previously challenging, if not impossible. Additionally, learning through computers allows for flexible study hours, unlike traditional courses with limited time. People of all ages can cover learning materials at their own pace, which enhances their passion for learning.

On the other hand, the negative consequences of excessive reliance on computers are evident in children's physical and mental well-being. Firstly, prolonged computer use can lead to physical health issues such as obesity, as it promotes a sedentary lifestyle where children spend excessive time staring at screens. This can result in vision problems and back pain if not addressed. Moreover, uncontrolled computer use significantly impacts children's social lives. Studies have shown that excessive computer usage can lead to social isolation and a lack of real interaction and community engagement.

Considering the aforementioned characteristics, it can be concluded that the influence of computers in modern society largely depends on individual approaches, which can bring both positive and negative aspects to children's development. Therefore, moderation is key.
