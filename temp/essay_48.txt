Revised:

Here is your edited essay according to IELTS structure:

Modern society is driven by technology, with computers seamlessly integrated into our daily lives. Young children, in particular, utilize computers in various ways. While there are potential negative effects, I believe that the positive impacts outweigh the negative ones on the younger generation.

To begin with, computers have proven to be instrumental in aiding children's studies. Through the internet, they can access a vast range of information related to their subjects. Consequently, they are exposed to different ideas and perspectives, enhancing their overall knowledge.

Moreover, computers contribute to the development of essential skills. Social networking sites like Facebook and Twitter enable children to interact with their friends and relatives, honing their communication abilities. Additionally, computer literacy has become a valuable asset in various careers. With computer skills, youngsters become more employable, eliminating the need for extensive training by organizations seeking suitable candidates.

However, one cannot overlook the adverse effects of excessive computer use. Prolonged periods spent in front of a computer screen can lead to various health problems, particularly issues with eyesight. Furthermore, it can lead to obesity among children due to a sedentary lifestyle. Additionally, excessive computer use can create a rift between children and their parents as they prioritize screen time over face-to-face communication.

In conclusion, while computers present both advantages and disadvantages, the positive aspects greatly outweigh the negative ones for young children. Parents must establish a balanced approach by setting limits on computer usage and restricting access to certain websites. By doing so, children can reap the benefits of computer technology while minimizing any associated drawbacks.
