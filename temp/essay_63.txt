Revised:

Is is believed by a number of people that in metropolitan cities, public transportation must be provided without any charges. However, despite the positive aspects of this amenity, there are some drawbacks. Both of the sides will be discussed in the forthcoming paragraphs.

On the one hand, offering free public transportation can bring a number of benefits, especially in terms of financial savings. For individuals, not having to pay for daily commute fare would provide a significant monetary relief. Additionally, on a larger scale, making public transport free would have a considerable impact on the environment. With the availability of free transportation, people would be more inclined to use public transport instead of their own vehicles. Consequently, this would help in addressing the problem of air pollution caused by the emission of hazardous fumes from automobiles. Moreover, reduced private vehicle usage would lead to decreased traffic congestion. Hence, there are immense advantages to be gained from providing free public transportation.

However, on the other hand, offering free travel in public vehicles can have detrimental effects on the functioning of governmental policies. For instance, in a recent publication, it was stated that in developed countries, income from public transportation accounts for a significant proportion of the GDP, ranging from 6:1 to 12:1. Therefore, it is obvious that making public transportation free would result in this substantial revenue stream being lost. Consequently, additional financial burdens would be placed on authorities, as they would have to cover maintenance costs, investments, and salaries from alternative sources. Thus, it is not a wise decision to make public transportation free of charge.

In conclusion, while many people advocate for the provision of free urban transportation to facilitate journeys for city dwellers, its implementation would not be economically viable. Although there are several benefits associated with this suggestion, the major drawback is the negative impact it would have on the financial sustainability of governmental bodies.
