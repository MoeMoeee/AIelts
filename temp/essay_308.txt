Topic:

"More and more students are choosing to study at colleges and universities in foreign countries. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this."

Essay:

"A growing number of students place a premium on pursuing their higher education abroad. Although attending foreign universities than their own countries has many benefits, the drawbacks involved are  important to be discussed.

On the plus side, it appears that studying abroad proves beneficial. The first benefit is concerned with more opportunities in the target country. Simply put, young people continue their studies in universities where studying is supposed to be more facilitated in comparison with their home country. This in turn paves the way for either academic or professional progress, affecting manifold aspects of their life. An obvious example of this situation is an avid student from a developing country looking for well-equipped laboratories in order to find a cure for hard-curable diseases such as cancer, by which the whole world would no longer be threatened. Students’ networks, moreover, could be enhanced as they collaborate with international peers and academics, thereby boosting the chance of employability in the upcoming opportunities.

Having said that, the downside of receiving overseas education should not be underestimated. To begin with, if their ideology or race is improperly introduced to the world, non-native students are likely prone to discrimination. As a direct result, all their attempt for integrating into society would fail and they are left with nothing but a traumatic experience. Take a young girl from an Islamic country for example; what may come under fire when she enters the campus with her Muslim-themed appearance is the idea of being a terrorist, permeating other classmates’ minds by the power of media. Another evident disadvantageous side is the high costs of the living in foreign countries, especially progressive ones, such as America. Newcomers should burden the cost of maybe prohibitive tuition fees or accommodation, which were up to their parents heretofore.

On balance, the tendency of studying overseas would be both rewarding and problematic. While it may guarantee their future remarkable achievements and provide them with more opportunities, students are highly likely to suffer from harassment as well as financial difficulties."


Revised:

A growing number of students are choosing to pursue their higher education abroad. While studying at foreign universities offers many advantages, it is equally important to consider the drawbacks involved.

On the positive side, studying abroad provides numerous benefits. Firstly, students have access to better educational opportunities in their chosen country. For instance, in countries like the United States or the United Kingdom, universities are well-equipped with state-of-the-art facilities and offer a wide range of courses. This enhances the academic and professional growth of students and has a positive impact on various aspects of their lives. For instance, an ambitious student from a developing nation may require advanced laboratories to conduct research on diseases like cancer, with the potential to benefit the world. Additionally, students have the opportunity to build strong networks and collaborate with international peers and academics, thus increasing their chances of employability in future opportunities.

However, it is crucial to acknowledge the drawbacks of studying abroad. One major disadvantage is the possibility of facing discrimination based on one's ideology or race. Non-native students are often susceptible to prejudice if their cultural or religious background is not properly understood. This can lead to difficulties in integrating into the host society and result in a traumatic experience. For example, a young Muslim girl from an Islamic country could face stereotypes and prejudices when she enters a campus due to media-driven misconceptions associating her appearance with terrorism. Furthermore, the cost of living in foreign countries, particularly in progressive nations such as the United States, can be prohibitively high. Students and their families often have to bear the burden of expensive tuition fees and accommodation expenses, which can pose financial challenges.

In conclusion, while studying abroad offers rewarding experiences and future opportunities, it also comes with potential difficulties. Students are likely to benefit from global exposure and improved learning environments, but they may also encounter discrimination and financial obstacles. It is important for students to carefully weigh the advantages and disadvantages before deciding to study in a foreign country.
