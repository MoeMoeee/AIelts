Here is the revised essay:

In recent years, there has been a growing trend of people opting for food from other countries rather than consuming local cuisine. This shift can be attributed to various factors such as the widespread access to the internet and social media, as well as an increasingly affluent society. This essay will discuss the driving forces behind this trend and evaluate whether it is a positive or negative development.

One of the main reasons for this trend is the easy access to the internet and social media platforms, which expose individuals to different cultures and traditions, including their respective cuisines. Young people, in particular, are often attracted to and curious about exploring various ethnic foods and practices. For example, popular food bloggers like Irfan from Chennai have garnered millions of followers on their vlogs, where they travel to different countries to provide insights into various food items and cuisines. Moreover, the availability of cutting-edge technology and readily accessible information on websites fuels the interest in exploring and tasting international foods.

Additionally, the increasing affluence of society plays a significant role in driving this trend. With greater disposable income, individuals are more inclined to explore and indulge in international cuisines, which are often perceived as exotic and novel compared to the ubiquitous local food. Singapore, known as a food paradise in Asia, benefits from an influx of tourists who are drawn to its wide variety of restaurants. High-class restaurants and five-star hotels in the city offer a diverse range of international buffets and local delicacies from around the world to cater to the desires of eager food enthusiasts. This also contributes to the cultivation of bonds and friendships between different cultures, as Singapore is a melting pot of diversity.

In conclusion, the preference for both local and international cuisines provides benefits to individuals and countries alike. Embracing diverse ethnic groups and local cultures allows for a more sustainable future in our interconnected world. However, it is important to strike a balance and not completely disregard local cuisine in favor of international options. The exploration of international food should be seen as an opportunity to embrace new flavors and experiences, while still appreciating and supporting local food traditions.
