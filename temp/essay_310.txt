Topic:

"Some people think that products should be made to last as long as possible, while others think making the products cheap is more important.

Discuss both views and express your own opinion."

Essay:

"It is asserted that the durability of products is of great importance. However, cost-effectiveness is a key factor that appears to have a number of proponents. I advocate the former group.

On the one hand, some advocate the idea of more reasonably-priced products. To begin with, having a voracious appetite for shopping affordable goods, people encourage a consumer-driven manufacturing process by which production lines would be expanded. Under this circumstance, more job opportunities could be made and the wheels of industry will be kept turning. An obvious example of this is China, known for its mass production. Not only has it recruited many locals, but also an array of jobs have been developed around the world. Furthermore, supporters of affordable goods claim that class disparity could be less apparent. Simply put, all walks of life would be able to accommodate their needs even the proletariat whose desires have been suppressed.

However, the longevity of goods takes priority in other people’s opinion. The first compelling reason is that a culture of consumerism would no longer be promoted. In other words, when products last for a long time, people, irrespective of their purchasing power, try not to give in to the urge to go shopping. This is clearly delineated in a society where the trend-led approach to purchasing fashion clothing is rejected and replaced by slow fashion, focusing on conscious buying and thoughtful purchases. Moreover, there is a strong correlation between built-to-last items and their positive impacts on the environment. To put it another way, the more sustainable material is used, the less environmentally damaging waste, such as plastic, would be dumped, leading to securing the future of our planet.

To conclude, I would tend to side with those supporting longevity when it comes to purchasing. It seems to be unreasonable to put the environment in jeopardy by generating more waste and seems more logical to prevent the mass discarding of goods and consumerist culture."


Revised:

"It is argued by some that the durability of products is crucial, while others prioritize the affordability of products. I personally believe that products should be made to last as long as possible.

On the one hand, proponents of cheap products argue that affordability promotes consumer-driven manufacturing and creates job opportunities. As an example, China is known for its mass production, which has not only employed many locals but has also created jobs worldwide. Moreover, those in favor of cheap products claim that it reduces class disparities, as people from all walks of life can meet their needs without breaking the bank.

On the other hand, there are strong arguments for prioritizing the longevity of products. Firstly, a focus on durability discourages a culture of consumerism. When products last longer, individuals are less likely to engage in excessive shopping, regardless of their purchasing power. This can be seen in the growing trend of "slow fashion," where consumers prioritize conscious and thoughtful purchases instead of following temporary fashion trends. Secondly, durable products have a positive impact on the environment. By using sustainable materials, such as reducing plastic waste, we can protect our planet and secure a sustainable future.

In conclusion, I believe that products should be made to last as long as possible. It is unreasonable to generate more waste and contribute to environmental damage, when we can prevent the mass discarding of goods and promote a more conscious consumer culture."
