Revised:

Education is often regarded as a crucial factor for achieving success in business. However, some individuals argue that higher education is not necessary to excel in the field of business. In my opinion, I partially agree with this viewpoint, but I believe that obtaining a tertiary education can provide valuable knowledge and skills that can significantly contribute to the success in business endeavors.

Firstly, it is undeniable that business has become a popular and flourishing career choice in today's society. Many people aspire to have their own businesses due to the convenience and flexibility it offers. Even graduates who are dissatisfied with their qualifications often change their career direction to business administration. This is evidenced by the increasing number of individuals enrolling in Master of Business Administration (MBA) programs offered by universities. For example, a study conducted in Germany revealed a significant surge in enrollment rates in MBA programs over the past five years.

On the other hand, some people argue that higher education is not essential for success in the field of marketing. They claim that specific career areas, such as education and healthcare, may require formal education, but marketing does not necessarily benefit from it. Additionally, advancements in technology have made it easier for individuals to succeed in their business careers without a formal education. Compared to two decades ago when advertising opportunities were limited to television and radio, social media now offers a greater chance to flourish in personal marketing endeavors.

Personally, I believe that education plays a vital role in any field, from engineering to sculpting. It provides essential training and knowledge that professionals need to excel in their chosen occupation. Drawing from my own experience, I initially worked as a teacher and had a small jewelry business. However, I struggled to achieve the desired income. This led me to pursue an MBA, and through this program, I acquired valuable insights and skills that paved the way for my success as a businesswoman.

In conclusion, while it is possible to succeed in the field of business without a tertiary education, I believe that obtaining higher education can provide individuals with valuable knowledge and skills that contribute to their success. The evolution of technology has undoubtedly opened up new avenues for personal marketing, but it does not diminish the importance of education. Universities, training programs, and practical experience can all serve as valuable resources in ensuring the development of professional businessmen and women.
