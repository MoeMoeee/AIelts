But I believe that other adepts like soldiers, should get more wages as they safeguard their nation twenty-four seven and sometimes, in unfortunate conditions, sacrifice their lives for saving their citizens. Therefore, giving more salary to popular players than other industrious humans is unjustified. 


In conclusion, although sports personalities encounter numerous difficulties, just for the pride of their country, I reaffirm that division of pay should be equal among persons pertaining to any profession, considering their hard work."

Revised:

Earning a huge amount of money by famous sports personalities, compared to other professional workers, is deemed inappropriate as it goes against the principle of equal opportunities for all individuals, despite the undeniable importance of sport in our lives. I completely agree with this notion.

There are several reasons that support my viewpoint, with the most prominent being the contributions of other professions to society. While it is true that sports professionals entertain and inspire people, it is the work of other skilled individuals that truly shape and serve the citizens of a nation. Take doctors, for example, who treat a diverse range of patients in various challenging situations. They not only provide essential healthcare services but also contribute to the economy through their taxes. On the other hand, athletes engage in sports primarily to earn wealth for themselves. In summary, professionals from other industries, as true facilitators, provide more tangible benefits to society in multiple ways.

Nevertheless, some individuals argue that the high income earned by renowned sports persons is justifiable due to the physical risks they face in order to bring glory to their country. They argue that these athletes deserve substantial earnings as compensation for their efforts, particularly in the event of career-ending injuries that could limit their future earning potential. However, I believe that other professionals, such as soldiers, deserve greater financial rewards as they protect their nation around the clock and often make ultimate sacrifices, even giving up their lives for the safety of their fellow citizens. Therefore, the unequal distribution of wealth, with popular athletes earning more than hardworking individuals from other industries, is unjustified.

In conclusion, while sports personalities do face numerous challenges in their pursuit of national pride, I strongly believe in equal distribution of income across all professions, taking into account the hard work and contributions of individuals in diverse fields.
