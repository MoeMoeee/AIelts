Topic:

"Some people think that it is the responsibility of governments to take care of the environment, while others believe that it is the responsibility of the citizens. 

Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"As  this is the era of global warming, environmental care is of utmost importance. Today, through this platform, I am given a chance of expressing my thoughts on who should be responsible to look after the environment. There are two schools of thought, one says that the government should be active in taking measures to secure it. On the other hand, the second is linked to the people. According to this,  people should individually be working. 

For the first point, firstly, the official environmental office should keep  issuing relevant measures. Secondly, they should control the smoke coming out of diesel cars. For this, the people running such vehicles should be banned and charged a heavy amount. Thirdly,  fireworks usage should be stopped. Moreover, the top management should be taking care of extra usage of machinery that exhibits smoke and increases air pollution. In addition, dust and maintenance while raining should also be given a considerable amount of attention. 

On the contrary, the second way is associated with every resident. It is their responsibility to do whatever is required in their capacity. Individuals should avoid smoking because it affects seasons badly. Moreover, they should also take care of extra utilization of air-conditioners and vehicles. They may also go to nearby places by walking or cycling. 

I believe that it is not possible for one group to clear the environment. It is always a joint effort of both parties that lead to the best outcomes for a nation to protect itself."


Revised:

As the issue of environmental conservation becomes increasingly important in the era of global warming, the question of who is responsible for taking care of the environment is a topic of much debate. Some argue that it is the government's duty to implement policies and regulations to protect the environment, while others believe that individuals should take personal responsibility for their actions. In this essay, I will discuss both points of view and provide my own opinion.

Those who support the notion that governments should take care of the environment argue that there are several steps that the government can take to ensure its protection. Firstly, government agencies should regularly issue relevant environmental measures and regulations to address issues such as air and water pollution, deforestation, and waste management. These regulations should be strictly enforced with penalties for non-compliance. Secondly, the government should take measures to reduce air pollution by controlling the emissions from vehicles, particularly diesel cars. This can be done by imposing heavy fines or banning the use of such vehicles altogether. Additionally, the government should take steps to minimize air pollution caused by industrial activities, such as regulating the use of machinery that emits polluting gases. Lastly, the government should promote the conservation of natural resources by discouraging activities such as fireworks usage, which can contribute to air pollution.

On the other hand, proponents of the individual responsibility view argue that each citizen should take personal responsibility for protecting the environment. Individuals can contribute to environmental care by adopting environmentally friendly habits in their daily lives. For instance, individuals can reduce air pollution by avoiding smoking, minimizing the use of air conditioners, and using public transportation or walking instead of using private vehicles whenever possible. Additionally, individuals can contribute to reducing waste and conserving natural resources by practicing recycling, reusing, and reducing consumption of single-use items. These small actions when multiplied by millions of individuals can have a significant impact on the environment.

In conclusion, the responsibility for environmental care should be shared between the government and citizens. While the government should take the lead in implementing policies and regulations, individuals should also take personal responsibility by adopting environmentally friendly habits and practices. Only through joint efforts can we successfully protect the environment and create a sustainable future for all.
