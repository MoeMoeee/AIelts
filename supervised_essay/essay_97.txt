Topic:

"The speeding up of life in many areas such as travel and communication has negative effects on society at all levels – individual, national, and global. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, people can travel and communicate more efficiently than before due to the advancements in science and technology. While many people agree with the idea that the speeding up of life has negative effects on the community, I personally believe that its advantages far outweigh its disadvantages.

First of all, at the individual levels, people tend to travel every year. They have to spend a large amount of money on different aspects such as accommodations, plane tickets or foods. At the national and international level, what often being criticized is the environmental pollution. For example, airplane and cars today, which help speed up travelling, emit more CO2, which could destroy the ozone layer and lead to the greenhouse effect.

On the other hand, people will have more choices and more opportunities to travel. This also lead to many potential jobs in tourism. Moreover, due to better communication, people are connected with their near and dear ones and distance are no longer a barrier. At the national level, it improves individuals’ efficiency and help to increase a country’s GDP. The rich nations are opening Multi National Companies in developing countries and providing job opportunities to millions. This is narrowing the gap between rich and poor. Thus, the economies of the poor countries are improving. At global level, it helps to improve international exchange and communication among nations. Because of this fast era of today, nations are developing doing foreign trade with each other. Moreover, through social media they solve global problems together such as poverty, disease or environment pollution.

In conclusion, I believe that the speeding up of life has both negative as well as positive effects. However, the positive effects are much more than negative influences"


Revised:

It is widely acknowledged that many working individuals lead sedentary lifestyles that result in various health problems. While some argue that advancements in technology and transportation have contributed to this trend, I believe that these developments have more advantages than disadvantages.

On an individual level, people are able to travel more easily and frequently than ever before. However, this often requires a significant financial investment, and can also contribute to environmental issues such as pollution. For example, the emissions from airplanes and cars used in travel can harm the ozone layer and contribute to global warming.

Despite these challenges, the rise of efficient travel and communication also provides numerous benefits. For one, people are able to explore a wider range of destinations and increase their exposure to different cultures. Additionally, improved communication technology has allowed individuals to maintain connections with loved ones, regardless of geographic distance. At a national level, these advancements can also boost productivity and contribute to a country's GDP. For instance, multinational companies can create job opportunities in developing nations, helping to reduce economic inequalities.

Moreover, the enhanced ease of travel and communication has also enabled greater collaboration and problem-solving on an international scale. Individuals and nations are now able to share ideas, best practices, and solutions across borders and time zones through social media and online platforms. This can benefit global communities by addressing issues such as poverty, disease, and environmental degradation.

In conclusion, while the rapid pace of modern life has resulted in some negative consequences, the benefits of improved technology and communication outweigh the drawbacks. By embracing these advancements responsibly, individuals and societies can navigate the challenges of the modern world while working towards a better future.
