Revised:

There is an ongoing debate as to whether or not advertising is crucial for individuals. While some people argue that it is necessary for purchasing advanced products and taking advantage of special discounts, others believe that advertising is so common that people no longer pay attention to it. This essay will discuss both views and argue in favor of the former.

On the one hand, if advertising of products is not held, the masses will not be able to acquire knowledge of the latest products available in the supermarkets. Advertisements help people stay informed about new items coming to the market. Moreover, they provide detailed information on how appliances work and offer deals on various items, which ultimately encourage people to make purchases. For instance, during Boxing Day in Canada, many outlets post deals on special brands, resulting in increased shopping activities at the end of the year.

On the other hand, people lead busy lives and may not have enough time to pay attention to the advertisements on television or social media platforms. In the current era, where prices for every item are high, people prioritize purchasing only those things that are necessary and are likely to last for a long time. Consequently, many individuals tend to skip these advertisements as they consider them to be a waste of time. For example, a YouTube director predicted that 99% of people prefer to skip advertisements at the start of a new video because they deem them useless.

In conclusion, although advertisements are so common that people tend to ignore them in order to save time, they play a significant role in helping individuals save money by enabling them to take advantage of deals. Additionally, advertisements provide detailed information on products, which can be beneficial in making informed purchasing decisions. Therefore, while there may be some truth to the argument that people no longer pay attention to advertisements, the benefits they provide outweigh this claim.
