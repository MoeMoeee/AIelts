Revised:

Nowadays, the business sector has become increasingly competitive, requiring workers to possess a range of skills to succeed. In my opinion, while good qualifications are important, having social skills is more vital in this century. In this essay, I will explain the benefits of being socially adept in business competition and provide a logical conclusion.

To begin with, in recent decades, many companies have experienced unusual circumstances. Job seekers often neglect the importance of networking when making crucial decisions. These candidates believe that having a wide network can greatly enhance their chances of securing a job. For instance, in countries with weak economies such as Turkey, Bulgaria, and Macedonia, finding employment can be extremely challenging without strong connections, even for individuals with a high level of education. Thus, the ability to establish and maintain social connections can be seen as a crucial skill, particularly in these less affluent countries.

On the other hand, research has shown that individuals with introverted tendencies and good qualifications often struggle to find work. Their lack of communication skills hinders their ability to showcase their talents in their respective fields. As a result, their self-confidence may be undermined, and they may become more socially withdrawn over time. Business is a highly competitive environment, and individuals who struggle with social anxiety find it increasingly difficult to navigate these demanding waters.

In conclusion, while both qualifications and social skills are important in the competitive job market, the advantage of having strong social connections outweighs the benefits of being highly educated. It is my hope that governments address these disparities in the future and implement equal standards in assessing individuals. By doing so, we can create a more level playing field for all job seekers.
