Revised:

Here is the edited essay:

It is a contentious issue whether reducing the speed of vehicles can effectively reduce accidents on the road. While some individuals advocate for limiting the speed of automobiles, others argue that alternative measures can be taken to tackle this problem. In my opinion, various measures can be taken to address this issue, and this essay will explore these measures in detail.

On the one hand, advocates for limiting speed contend that speeding is one of the main causes of accidents. Young drivers, in particular, often seek thrill and show off their driving skills to others. This recklessness can result in unavoidable accidents. For example, a survey on car accidents conducted in Mumbai revealed that most accidents were caused by overspeeding. Thus, proponents of reducing speed limits argue that this would discourage drivers from driving at high speeds and deter accidents.

On the other hand, others believe that merely reducing the speed limit will not solve the problem of road accidents, and the government needs to take various measures to alleviate the issue. Drunk driving is one of the leading causes of accidents, as drivers under the influence of alcohol have poor judgment and reaction times. Additionally, accidents commonly occur due to inexperienced drivers. For instance, the famous Bollywood celebrity Salman Khan was involved in a drunk driving case in the early days of his career. To address these issues, the government can enforce stricter rules for issuing driver's licenses and impose heavy penalties on drunk drivers.

In conclusion, reducing speed limits alone may not effectively reduce the number of accidents on the road. Instead, I believe that the government should implement stricter rules and regulations to address the issues of drunk driving and inexperienced drivers. By doing so, we can significantly reduce the number of accidents on the road, creating a safer driving environment for everyone.
