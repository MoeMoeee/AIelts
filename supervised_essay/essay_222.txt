Revised:

Some individuals opine that individuals under the age of 18 who commit serious crimes, such as robbery or violent attacks, should be punished in the same way as adults. However, I strongly disagree with this concept. This essay will discuss the differences in opinion and provide reasons why I believe that young offenders should be treated differently.

Firstly, proponents of this viewpoint argue that serious crimes should not go unpunished as it could lead to an increase in crime rates. They believe that young individuals mostly follow the influence of others and are often unaware of the consequences of their actions. To illustrate, consider a recent incident where a group of 16-year-old boys were involved in a high-speed accident resulting in the death of a mother and her one-year-old child. The driver of the vehicle was sentenced to 17 years in prison. From this perspective, punishing young offenders equally sends a strong message that crime will not be tolerated and helps prevent its spread.

However, others disagree with treating young offenders as adults, highlighting that they are the future of our society. They argue that imposing harsh punishments would not only destroy their innocence but also hinder their development and potential. Everyone deserves a chance to learn and grow from their mistakes. It is important to note that most mistakes committed by young individuals are not premeditated but rather occur coincidentally. For example, a survey conducted among 90% of doctors reveals that the human soul is inherently innocent until faced with certain circumstances that threaten it. Therefore, providing young offenders with a chance to rehabilitate themselves is crucial for their personal growth and the betterment of society.

In conclusion, while it is essential to recognize and address the severity of crimes committed by young people, treating them in the same way as adults is not the most effective approach. By considering the unique circumstances of young offenders, we can adopt a more rehabilitative and supportive approach that allows them to learn from their mistakes and become better individuals. This would not only protect their future but also contribute to a society that is focused on guiding and nurturing its youth.
