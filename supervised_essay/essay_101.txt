Revised:

In the era of globalization, some countries have a larger population of young adults than older people. This situation can have potential advantages, such as increased manpower and economic output. However, it also has some disadvantages, such as a lack of proper guidance and experience. In this essay, I will argue that the advantages outweigh the disadvantages.

On the one hand, having a higher number of younger people can lead to economic growth and prosperity. Firstly, the young people can enter the workforce and contribute to the national economy. Secondly, with fewer elderly people, there is less pressure on the healthcare system. For instance, in some European countries, healthcare spending is high due to a greater proportion of people over the age of 60. Finally, there would be less spending on retirement benefits, resulting in a large pool of money that the government could allocate to other productive initiatives.

On the other hand, there may be some potential disadvantages to having a large number of young people. For instance, lacking seniors' guidance, young people might make hasty decisions that could have negative impacts on society. However, with the availability of online forums and technological advances, young people can still seek advice and guidance from experienced individuals.

In conclusion, countries with larger populations of young people stand to benefit from increased economic growth and reduced burdens on healthcare and pension systems. The potential disadvantage of a lack of guidance and supervision from older generations can be mitigated through online resources and technology. Therefore, I believe that the advantages of this situation outweigh the disadvantages.
