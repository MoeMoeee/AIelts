Topic:

"Most employees do job interviews before offering position to a person. Do you think this is the best way to do it or are there better alternatives? What is the best method of choosing employer in your opinion?"

Essay:

"Today, many people have to go through interviews in order to get selected for a position. In my opinion, I strongly agree with this method of appointing employees, as this gives the leverage to employers to choose the best candidates and also, job seekers get to know better about his/her employer during the whole interview process.

To commence with, many organisations today have a thorough selection process in order to find the best out of all that suits their requirements. The job interview is one of the steps that is included in this process and it plays an important role to examine the candidate's efficiency. To cite an example, for many software engineers, Google is one of their dream companies, and to get selected, some might have to take 6 to 8 rounds for a senior role. This method has proven successful in finding the best gems and that is the reason, Google is one of the most advanced and highly used search engines.

Furthermore, getting selected via interviews is a transparent method of getting a job. There were days when people used to get opportunities based on their contacts and references. In addition, job seekers can choose their employers effectively beforehand as they have plenty of resources where they can check about the experiences of past employees and verify organisation ratings. Moreover, during the whole interview process, they will get in contact with senior colleagues which will enable the employees to get to know their employer better.

To sum up, I firmly believe that many companies follow the right process of hiring employees through job interviews. I believe that the aforementioned points are strongly supporting my viewpoints."


Revised:

It has become common practice for companies to conduct job interviews as part of their employee selection process. While some argue that there may be better alternatives, I believe that interviews are an effective way of selecting the best candidate as they allow employers to assess a candidate's suitability for the role and give job seekers the opportunity to learn more about their potential employer.

Job interviews are a crucial step for many organizations to ensure that they select the most qualified and competent candidates that align with their requirements. For example, companies such as Google have a rigorous selection process for senior positions, which involves numerous rounds of interviews. The success of this process speaks for itself as Google is known for its high-quality employees and efficient work environment.

Job interviews have the advantage of being a transparent method of hiring as candidates are selected based on their merit and capabilities. This is an improvement from the past where nepotism and favoritism were prevalent, and job opportunities were based on connections and references. Additionally, job seekers can research and learn more about their potential employer through available resources such as online reviews and employee feedback. During the interview process, candidates can interact with senior colleagues and gain deeper insight into the organizational culture and values.

In conclusion, I strongly endorse the use of job interviews as a suitable method of selecting employees. It allows companies to find the best fit for the job, while candidates can objectively evaluate their potential employer. Ultimately, this improves the hiring process and contributes to the success of a workplace.
