"It is often argued that men should be the breadwinner who should go out to work and earn money for all the incomes of the families and women should stay home and look after the whole family. However, I fundamentally disagree with the given notion.

 To begin with, before the second world war, the labour force was dominated by  males. Revised:

It is a common belief that men should be the primary wage earners who go out to work, while women's role is confined to being homemakers. However, I strongly disagree with this notion.

Firstly, it is important to acknowledge that before the Second World War, men dominated the labor force, and women had limited opportunities in the job market. However, during and after the war, the need for a large workforce arose as men were serving in the military. This led to the recognition of women's capabilities, and they were actively recruited, particularly in the United States. It was during this time that female workers demonstrated their ability to perform the same jobs as men.

Furthermore, women have advanced significantly over the past few decades and have been able to reach higher positions. The belief that women are physically weaker than men, preventing them from engaging in physically demanding jobs, is outdated and inaccurate. Tasks such as driving, loading, and unloading goods were traditionally seen as male occupations, but women can now perform these tasks effortlessly and effectively. Similarly, in the past, female involvement in activities like wrestling was uncommon, but today, women have proven themselves capable of meeting the necessary criteria.

In conclusion, in today's world, where gender roles are continually evolving, it is essential to recognize that both men and women are equally capable of fulfilling various roles. Providing equal opportunities in the workforce can enable individuals to showcase their competence and contribute to society.
