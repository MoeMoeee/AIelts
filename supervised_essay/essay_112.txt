In today's world, there is an ongoing debate regarding the usefulness of libraries and whether they are a waste of resources and money. Some suggest that computers should replace libraries, while others disagree. In this essay, I will discuss both sides of the argument and provide my opinion.

Computers are electronic devices that are equipped with various software programs, including social media, email, job searching websites, and educational resources like Microsoft Office and painting software. They are easily accessible and provide a fast and interactive learning experience. Therefore, some individuals contend that libraries, which required extensive funding for their establishment and maintenance, are no longer a necessity.

On the other hand, there are those who believe that libraries continue to be an essential resource for learning. Libraries provide a peaceful environment where individuals can access a vast collection of both fiction and non-fiction books. They offer a quiet and serene environment that allows individuals to concentrate and absorb new knowledge in a leisurely manner. Furthermore, not everyone has access to computers, especially in developing countries, and so libraries provide a more accessible option for individuals who do not have access to technological equipment.

In my opinion, both libraries and computers are essential resources for learning, and both play significant roles in a person's educational journey. Libraries provide a peaceful atmosphere, and the tangible presence of books is an irreplaceable experience that no computer screen can replicate. Computers, on the other hand, provide quick access to information, interactive learning, and opportunities for communication and productivity.

In conclusion, both libraries and computers have their advantages and are necessary for individuals' educational advancement. While some may view them as opposing resources, they can work collaboratively to supplement and complement each other.
