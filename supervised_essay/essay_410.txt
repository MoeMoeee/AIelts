In recent years, there has been a growing emphasis on the importance of technology. While some argue that it is unnecessary to include computer skills as part of a child's education, I strongly believe that having these skills will provide children with a competitive advantage.

On the one hand, we are currently living in a technology-driven era where computers play a crucial role in our daily lives. On the other hand, acquiring computer skills can significantly improve a child's job prospects. In our country, many people underestimate the potential of the artificial intelligence (AI) sector, resulting in a lack of interest in learning computer skills. However, there is a rising demand for AI professionals, with foreign companies even offering high salaries to individuals with expertise in this field. By equipping children with computer skills, they will have a better chance of securing high-paying jobs in the future. Furthermore, as automation becomes more prevalent, individuals with strong technology skills will be better equipped to adapt to changing job markets. In South Korea, for example, approximately 15% of accountants are already being replaced by robots, and this number is expected to rise steadily.

In conclusion, while some may argue that introducing computer skills into a child's education can be burdensome, I firmly believe that it is essential for their future success. As technology continues to advance, being proficient in computer skills will provide children with immense opportunities and enable them to navigate the evolving job market with ease. Therefore, incorporating computer skills as an essential part of education will equip children with the tools they need for a fruitful and fulfilling career.
