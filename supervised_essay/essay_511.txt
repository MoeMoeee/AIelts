Revised:

It is believed that the high sales of popular consumer goods are driven by advertising rather than the real needs of the society where these products are sold. I strongly agree with this statement, as I believe that advertising has the ability to create curiosity and influence consumer behavior, despite the lack of real necessity. In this essay, I will explore this concept further and provide examples to support my opinion.

Firstly, advertisements have the power to captivate consumers by creating an interactive interface in their minds. When advertisements are engaging and relevant, they create a desire for the product being promoted. For instance, in my hometown, there is a special sweet called Khasta that is commonly consumed. Traditionally, people use wooden utensils to shape this sweet as it is a time-consuming process. However, a well-known kitchen utensil manufacturer introduced a steel device that could shape the sweet, claiming to be more efficient and convenient. Despite the lack of demand for this new product, the manufacturer launched an advertising campaign after three years of its initial launch. As a result, sales tripled the following year. However, consumers soon realized that the taste, texture, and durability of the sweet were compromised. After numerous complaints, the local government investigated the matter and discovered that the wooden equipment provided a unique flavor to the sweet, which was lacking in the steel device. This experience illustrates how advertising can influence sales initially, but fail to sustain demand in the long term.

It is important to note that the longevity of demand for a product heavily depends on the consumers themselves. The power of advertisement may lead to an increase in sales initially, but it is ultimately the consumer's experience and satisfaction that determines whether the demand continues. Therefore, it is crucial for companies to conduct thorough research on the preferences and practices of the local population before introducing a new product into the market.

In conclusion, advertising plays a significant role in increasing sales for consumer goods, even in cases where there may not be a real need in the society. However, the sustained demand for a product is contingent upon the consumer's satisfaction with the product. Thus, it is essential for companies to conduct proper research on the preferences and practices of the target market before launching a new product.
