Revised:

In today's world, the number of obese individuals is rapidly increasing globally. While there is ongoing debate, the main causes are attributed to sedentary lifestyles and unhealthy dietary choices. This essay will examine these causes and their effects.

Firstly, an unhealthy lifestyle and a lack of nutritious diet have emerged as the two primary reasons for obesity in the 21st century. For example, a study conducted by the American Obesity Society revealed that the fast food industry has experienced a significant surge since the early 2000s. This is evident as people with hectic daily routines tend to opt for quick and easy meals, which often consist of fast foods and microwavable diets high in fat and sugar. Furthermore, individuals are not engaging in regular physical activities, exacerbating the problem. Consequently, the combination of a sedentary lifestyle and the consumption of processed food contribute to weight gain and obesity.

The effects of obesity are extensive, with a major consequence being serious health issues. For instance, in the United Kingdom, numerous scientific studies have shown that over 70 percent of obese individuals are at high risk of developing diseases such as heart failure and diabetes, which are among the leading causes of death. Consequently, substantial amounts of money are spent annually on diabetes and heart failure medications. Therefore, it is crucial to minimize these effects in order to improve the quality of life for future generations.

In conclusion, raising awareness about healthy food choices and promoting physical exercise are essential strategies to combat obesity. It is expected that schools and parents will play a significant role in encouraging children to adopt nourishing diets and embrace a healthy lifestyle. By doing so, we can create a healthier society and prevent the alarming rise of obesity.
