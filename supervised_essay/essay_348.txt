Topic:

"The older people who need employment have to compete with younger ones. What problems can this create? What are some solutions?"

Essay:

"In recent years, competition is increasing between young people and the old who are searching for same job. It causes some negative consequences and steps should be taken to tackle this problem.

There are some problems occur when the young and old are struggling to get the job. First of all, elderly people are not very adaptive and creative as youngers are and they do not very energetic to do work like younger. They may find some difficulties with usage of technology and to fulfill the requirements of company which could affect on their health and they might suffer both physically and psychologically. Older people have more difficulties to find a job even when they have same qualifications as younger and have experience also. While, some employers prefer older people with higher working experience rather than younger who have just graduated and having no work experience and good communication skills.

To solve this problem government should take some initiative in effective way. Government should encourages self-employment for fresh graduates so they can create job opportunities themselves. Then the retirement age should be decreased which reduce the pressure of competition the young have to face. Another possible way is to allocate more money to pension system because who find their pension sufficient for a living will not have to find a work. Moreover, younger people and government should support older person to use new technologies so they can do work well.

In conclusion, some problems occur in competition of youth and old to get job like technology and experience related but it can be solved by taking some measures like decrease retirement age and increase pension."


Revised:

In recent years, there has been an increasing competition between younger and older individuals vying for the same jobs. This situation raises several problems that need urgent attention and calls for effective solutions.

One significant issue that arises when the older and younger generations compete for employment is the differences in adaptability and creativity. Elderly people are generally less flexible and innovative compared to their younger counterparts. They may struggle with the use of modern technology and meeting the evolving requirements of companies. These challenges can have detrimental effects on their physical and psychological well-being. Moreover, older individuals often face more difficulties in finding employment, even when they possess similar qualifications and work experience as their younger peers. In some cases, employers may prefer older workers with higher levels of experience and stronger communication skills.

To address these problems, it is essential for the government to take proactive measures. Firstly, the government should encourage self-employment among fresh graduates, enabling them to create their own job opportunities. Additionally, reducing the retirement age would alleviate the pressure of competition faced by the younger generation. This would allow older individuals to exit the workforce earlier, making way for younger job seekers. Moreover, allocating more funds to the pension system would provide adequate financial support for retirees. As a result, those who have sufficient pension benefits would not feel compelled to seek employment. Lastly, younger individuals and the government should actively support older people in acquiring proficiency in using new technologies, as this would enhance their job performance and increase their employability.

In conclusion, the competition between the youth and the elderly for employment presents various challenges related to technology and experience. However, these problems can be tackled through measures such as decreasing the retirement age and increasing pension benefits. It is crucial for the government to take the lead in implementing these solutions in order to create a more equitable job market for all generations.
