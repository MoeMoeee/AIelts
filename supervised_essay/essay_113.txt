Revised:

The increasing pressure on school and university students is a subject of concern as it imposes a heavy workload on them at a young age. While this trend has both positive and negative impacts, it is essential to strike a balance between the two, with parents and teachers playing a pivotal role. In this essay, I will discuss the issue in more detail.

On the one hand, the pressure on students is rising due to several reasons. Firstly, we live in an era where competition is intense, and getting high grades is crucial to gaining entry into top colleges and universities. Moreover, tertiary education is expensive, and many students have to work part-time jobs to afford tuition fees, adding to their already hectic schedules. In some instances, parents may force their children to pursue subjects that hold little interest for them, thereby contributing to their stress levels.

On the other hand, it can be argued that this situation is a positive development since hard work from an early age prepares students to tackle life's challenges maturely, avoiding involvement in crime or violence. Additionally, it enables them to develop their knowledge and personality, resulting in a more satisfying and fulfilling life in the future.

Despite these potential advantages, there are also negative implications of the increasing pressure students face. For example, they may struggle to balance their studies with leisure time, leading to boredom and burnout. Furthermore, if students are forced into courses they do not wish to pursue, it can lead to feelings of failure or being unable to cope, which may result in suicidal tendencies.

In conclusion, while education is a crucial aspect of one's life, excessive pressure on young students can have adverse effects on their mental and emotional wellbeing. Thus, it is essential for parents and educators to find ways to help students manage their workload and keep things in balance.
