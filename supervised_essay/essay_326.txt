Revised:

Nowadays, an increasing number of individuals are experiencing health issues that cannot be effectively treated with modern medicines due to their adoption of a sedentary lifestyle. Some argue that a return to traditional medicines should be promoted as a remedy for these health problems. This essay agrees with this notion and will provide two reasons to support this perspective.

First and foremost, traditional medicine, in some cases, has shown remarkable success in treating fatal diseases that modern medicine fails to address. For instance, consider a person diagnosed with a severe lung disease. Despite being given a mere four months to live by doctors, he refused to accept his fate and explored alternative treatments. Through the use of Kazakh national medicine, which involves drinking kumis and spending time in the steppe, he surprisingly managed to completely cure his illness. This compelling example underscores the potential efficacy of traditional medicine in successfully combating dire health conditions that modern medicine is unable to address.

Furthermore, traditional medicine typically consists of natural remedies derived from plants, with the exception of certain practices like Chinese traditional medicine involving acupuncture. As a result, it tends to have fewer adverse effects on the human body. In contrast, modern medicine can sometimes worsen a patient's condition if not administered properly. This highlights the advantages of alternative medicine, which prioritizes one's well-being through the use of natural ingredients.

In conclusion, traditional medicine has proven effective in instances where modern medicine has proven powerless. Additionally, alternative medicine offers numerous benefits to the human body due to its reliance on natural ingredients. As a result, the encouragement and utilization of traditional medicine should not be overlooked.
