It is widely believed that advertising methods utilised by certain corporations are unethical and unacceptable in today's society. This essay concurs with the view and aims to discuss the reasons for this belief, and provide relevant examples.

One of the most unacceptable advertising strategies is the deliberate misrepresentation of products. Advertisers often use slogans that suggest a product will only be available for a limited time to create a sense of urgency, encouraging consumers to purchase without proper consideration. For instance, television channel 'Shop CJ' constantly displays a time limit for all their products, pressuring viewers to buy them quickly.

Another widely used advertising technique on the internet is the frequent pop-up of websites. This is generally irritating for users, who are bombarded with multiple pop-up ads before they can access the information they require. For example, 'Techmahindra.com' greets users with a series of pop-up windows for every click. Another advertising strategy is the use of famous personalities to promote products and encourage people to make a purchase. A well-known example of this is Varun Dhawan, who endorses fairness creams that often have adverse health effects.

In conclusion, it is clear that many companies utilise advertising methods that are not appreciated by citizens. These methods often pressure individuals to buy products or inundate them with advertisements, leading to a negative impact on society.
