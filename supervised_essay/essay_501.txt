Revised:

It is predicted that in the future, cash payments may no longer be accepted in shops and all transactions will have to be made using cards or mobile phone applications. This essay will discuss the likelihood of this change and explore why some individuals may be reluctant to give up using cash.

With the advancement of technology, various aspects of our lives have undergone significant transformations. One such aspect is the way we shop and make payments. A new method of payment, known as cashless payment, using cards or mobile phone applications, is expected to emerge. This method is designed to provide people with greater convenience and efficiency when making purchases.

However, there are certain individuals, particularly sellers, who may oppose this shift in payment methods. This is primarily because the government has imposed regulations requiring stores to pay extra charges to the banks for cashless transactions. Consequently, this additional burden may have a negative impact on the revenue of these establishments.

In conclusion, the transition from cash to cashless payments is likely to occur in the future as technology continues to develop. Nevertheless, some individuals, particularly sellers, may resist this change due to the additional charges they incur.
