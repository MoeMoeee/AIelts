Revised:

In today's society, advertising has become pervasive everywhere. Advertisements are found on TV shows, magazines, and even huge LED boards on street corners. However, the methods used to create advertisements are sometimes unethical and unsuitable for current social standards. In this essay, I will discuss the extent to which I agree that some advertising methods are unethical.

Firstly, advertisements can have a negative impact on consumer behavior. For example, some families may not need a particular product advertised on TV, but the housewife may think that it is a good idea to have it, and decide to purchase it immediately. Similarly, young children may see toy advertisements on TV, and because they are not mature enough to understand the family's financial situation, they may demand the toy, and their parents may be obliged to purchase it. The advertising industry is aware of the vulnerability of these two groups, and they exploit it for their own benefit.

Secondly, advertisements use various psychological tactics to manipulate the consumer's weakness. For example, an advertisement with a physically fit person using a particular product may seem innocent, but it could have a negative impact on the self-esteem of youth who are struggling with body image issues. Another example could be the use of persuasive language on the cigarette boxes, such as "the ideal of a manhood." It is designed to project an image that smoking is associated with being masculine. However, smoking is hazardous to human health and causes severe damage to society.

In conclusion, I strongly agree that the advertising industry utilizes unethical and unacceptable methods to increase their sales, which can have a detrimental impact on society. As a result, governments worldwide must take action by implementing measures to regulate the advertising industry. By doing so, the authorities can check and control advertisements before broadcasting them to the general public.
