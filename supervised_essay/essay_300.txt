Topic:

"Many people send and receive emails in business and in their personal life. Do the advantages of using the email outweigh the disadvantages?"

Essay:

"A large number of people utilize the email service to carry out their professional tasks and for attending to their personal affairs. While there are some drawbacks associated with using this means of communication, I believe the main benefits are more substantial.

On the one hand, a potential disadvantage may be misapprehension resulting from an email exchange. Since emotions and tone of speech are, at times, difficult to convey properly through the written form, the content of an email, too, might upset or aggravate someone. For instance, a manager’s announcement could sound forceful when organizing a meeting with the staff through email and frustrate the employees. Another negative is receiving unwanted electronic mail known as spam. These messages contain harmful pieces of virus. I, personally, become rather alarmed when my inbox is cluttered by spam.

On the other hand, a primary advantage of using the email service is fast communication. Whereas in the past, hand-written letters used to take a long time to be delivered, the email technology has made it possible to send and receive mail almost instantly. In business, where people tend to make work-related arrangements on a daily basis, using this service is extremely useful. A further benefit is that the email is accessible in most places around the globe. If one wishes to contact their friends or relatives overseas, there is a good chance that they will be able to connect to the Internet and send an email.

On balance, it is true that using emails to exchange information would seem disadvantageous under certain circumstances. However, in my view, its positive effects on the speed of communication as well as the accessibility of this service override the downsides."


Revised:

A large number of people utilize email services for their professional and personal communication needs. While there are some drawbacks associated with this means of communication, I believe that the advantages outweigh the disadvantages.

On the one hand, one of the potential disadvantages of using email is the possibility of misapprehension. It can be difficult to accurately convey emotions and tone of speech through written words, resulting in misinterpretation and potentially upsetting or aggravating someone. For example, a manager's announcement organizing a meeting via email might unintentionally come across as forceful or frustrating to the employees. Additionally, the issue of receiving unwanted emails, commonly known as spam, poses a nuisance. These messages often contain harmful viruses and can clutter one's inbox, raising alarms and causing inconvenience.

On the other hand, the primary advantage of email communication is its speed and efficiency. Unlike traditional letters that took a considerable amount of time to be delivered, email enables users to send and receive messages almost instantly. This is particularly useful in professional settings where work-related arrangements need to be made quickly and efficiently on a daily basis. Furthermore, email is accessible in most parts of the world, allowing individuals to connect with friends and family overseas without hassle.

In conclusion, while there are certain drawbacks to email communication, such as the potential for miscommunication and the inconvenience of spam, the positive effects of this technology in terms of speed and accessibility outweigh these downsides. Therefore, I believe that the advantages of using email outweigh the disadvantages.
