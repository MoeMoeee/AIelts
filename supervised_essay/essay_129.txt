Revised:

Obesity is a significant problem facing developed countries, especially among children between the ages of 4-14. This essay aims to discuss some of the main reasons for this and its impact.

One of the leading causes of childhood obesity is the shift in dietary habits, particularly in countries like the United States, London, and some Asian territories. The consumption of unhealthy products such as packaged foods, fast-food, carbonated drinks, and desserts with high sugar levels has increased over the years. In addition, the abundance of money coupled with a lack of proper time has contributed to this problem. Parents, who work hard to provide for their families, have the luxury of affording excess food. The trend of grocery shopping in high-end markets like Walmart also leads to the purchase of unnecessary edible items that eventually increase intake resulting in corpulence.

The impact on children who are overweight is significant. Apart from the difficulties faced in physical activities, they may experience serious health issues such as high cholesterol levels, heart disease, hormonal changes, PCOD in women, skin complications, hair loss, and mental health issues like depression. However, with proper diet, physical exercise, and medication, these conditions can be put under control with professional help.

In conclusion, while acknowledging the problem of childhood obesity, it is essential to understand the negative effects on children's lives. Parents should encourage proper diets, adequate exercise, and professional guidance to ensure the well-being of their children. Improving this by taking baby steps will lead to successful outcomes in all aspects of children's lives.
