"Some people feel that manufacturers and supermarkets have the responsibility to reduce the amount of packaging of goods. Others argue that customers should avoid buying goods with a lot of packaging. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Some people feel that manufacturers and supermarkets have the responsibility to reduce the amount of packaging of goods. Others argue that customers should avoid buying goods with a lot of packaging. I think that the responsibility is on both the buyer and the seller as they both deserve a win-win situation.

On one hand, The consumer wants to get the goods well preserved and with the least packaging possible as it will help him get to the action faster, And also it is safer this way environmentally, For instance, There are so many goods that don't need this much packaging which consumes more time to open and more energy to waste as an example the decorated boxes which consume more materials just for the appearance to be more appealing. The buyer has to be smarter and more practical to avoid this conflict as at the end of the day the consumer only needs the item which is more useful not the more decorated.

On the other hand, The manufacturers have a point and this decoration will help them in getting more money as the buyer most of the time tends to buy good-looking items regardless of what is the item but still, The seller has this part of responsibility where he has to be more conservative and using less packaging as much as he can.

In conclusion, The buyer and the consumer have the same responsibility not only towards themselves but also towards the environment as the buyer has to be more practical and so does the seller."


Revised:

Some people believe that manufacturers and supermarkets should bear the responsibility of reducing the amount of packaging for goods, while others argue that customers should avoid purchasing products with excessive packaging. In my opinion, both buyers and sellers hold a shared responsibility in order to achieve a mutually beneficial outcome.

On the one hand, consumers desire goods that are effectively packaged, preserving the quality of the products while minimizing unnecessary packaging. This not only enables users to access the items more efficiently, but it also contributes to environmental sustainability. For instance, certain products are excessively packaged solely for aesthetic purposes, such as the use of decorative boxes. This practice not only wastes materials but also requires additional energy to dispose of. Consumers should, therefore, make more informed choices by prioritizing functionality over aesthetics.

On the other hand, manufacturers may argue that attractive packaging assists in selling more products, with customers often gravitating towards visually appealing items, regardless of their actual content. Nevertheless, it is crucial for sellers to adopt a more conservative approach and reduce excessive packaging wherever possible.

In conclusion, both consumers and manufacturers share the responsibility for reducing packaging waste. Not only is this important for individual consumers, but it is also an obligation towards the environment. By being more pragmatic in their purchasing decisions, consumers can contribute to a more sustainable future. At the same time, manufacturers should make efforts to minimize unnecessary packaging, recognizing the significance of environmental conservation.
