Topic:

"In some cities public parks and open spaces are being changed  into gardens where local residents can grow their own fruits and vegetables. Do the advantages outweigh disadvantages?"

Essay:

"These days in several cities public spaces such as parks are being converted into gardens in order to support local dwellers to plant fruit and vegetables. This trend has merits and demerits as well.

To begin with, the benefits of this change are varied. Firstly, local people can achieve enormous provisions by gardening. The most important thing is that during harvesting citizens are able to get reliable products. As a consequence, they can consume healthy fruits and vegetables, which are rich in vitamins and minerals as well. ,Also it can fill the people's deficit for healthy fruit. Furthermore,  gardening is a good motivation, which leads to a healthy lifestyle. In truth, gardens need regular care by individuals. This, in turn, makes people do regular physical work. Additionally, they might avoid obesity and remedy other health issues. In a sense, there will be a more healthy and hardworking population. 

On the contrary, there are also some serious drawbacks to this phenomenon. It is true that there are not enough green areas for other local residents in some regions. Hence, a plethora of people might stay without gardens. According to scientists' words, walking through parks can relieve people's stress. However, due to the ,gardens there will be a lack of spaces for relaxation. Lastly, giving open fields for gardening may affect other spheres of the economy.  To put it simply, an agricultural country without industry is not able to develop in all respects. 

In conclusion, I am convinced that changing some public places into gardens and guiding them for local dwellers is not a positive development."


Revised:

These days, in several cities, public spaces such as parks are being converted into gardens to support local residents in growing their own fruits and vegetables. This trend has both advantages and disadvantages.

On the one hand, there are several benefits to this change. Firstly, gardening allows local people to have a sustainable source of fresh produce. When citizens are able to harvest their own fruits and vegetables, they can be assured of the reliability and quality of the products they consume. This means they can enjoy healthy and nutritious food, which is rich in vitamins and minerals. Additionally, gardening can promote a healthier lifestyle, as it requires regular care and physical activity. By engaging in regular gardening activities, individuals can avoid obesity and improve their overall well-being. This can lead to a more health-conscious and productive population.

On the other hand, there are certain drawbacks to this phenomenon. One major concern is the reduction of green spaces available for other local residents. In some areas, the conversion of parks into gardens means that there are limited spaces for people to relax and unwind. Scientists have proven that spending time in parks can help reduce stress levels, but with the absence of these green spaces, individuals may find it difficult to find a place of solace in their communities. Moreover, giving up open fields for gardening purposes may have a negative impact on other sectors of the economy. In countries heavily reliant on agriculture, prioritizing gardens over industrial development can hinder overall progress and growth.

In conclusion, while the transformation of public spaces into gardens for local residents has its merits, I believe that the disadvantages outweigh the advantages. The reduction of green areas for relaxation and potential hindrance to economic development make this change less favorable. It is important for city planners and policy-makers to strike a balance between supporting urban agriculture and preserving public spaces for the well-being of the entire community.
