Essay:

"To live securely and safely, we all need a home. It is our basic need. Some minds claim that a place for living for people who are unable to afford a house should be offered by the government, however, others claim that it should not be provided by the government. I firmly agree with the former notion because of many reasons.

The first and foremost is that for the protection of the individuals in the country, it is the duty of the authority to provide a free place for the impoverished to live in which shows the positive attitude of management for the people. To illustrate, there are countries such as Canada, India and many more that are providing free-of-cost houses for underprivileged families. Secondly, caring for local people is the duty of authority and by doing this, people will believe that the government is taking care of its residents.

Moving further, the count of death and disease will be reduced when all people have homes as it protects us from extreme weather conditions such as heat, snow storms, cold, rain and many more. For example, people who don't have a home because of money,  have to live on streets or roads which will prone them to get diseases or a reason for death as well. Furthermore, others will also follow this plan so their residents don't have any trouble living in the country.

To sum up, there is no doubt free housing will affect the budget of government but owing to a number of reasons to provide homes without money to impoverished nationals such as the safety and security of the population, the positive attitude of the country towards its citizens and the harmony of the nation."

Revised:

To ensure the wellbeing and security of individuals, having access to housing is crucial. Some argue that the government should provide free housing for those who cannot afford it, while others disagree. I strongly agree with the former viewpoint for several reasons.

First and foremost, it is the responsibility of the government to provide shelter for the underprivileged. By offering free housing, the authorities demonstrate their commitment to caring for the welfare of their citizens. Countries such as Canada and India have already implemented programs that provide free housing to impoverished families. This not only ensures that everyone has a place to live, but also fosters a sense of trust and support between the government and its people.

In addition, providing free housing can significantly reduce the rates of illness and mortality in a society. Extreme weather conditions like heatwaves, cold spells, and heavy rainfall pose a significant threat to those without a proper shelter. Individuals who are unable to afford housing often end up living on the streets, exposing them to various health risks and even the possibility of death. By offering free housing, the government can protect the most vulnerable members of society and ensure their safety and wellbeing.

Moreover, providing free housing sets a positive example for other nations to follow. When a government prioritizes the needs of its citizens and ensures their access to basic necessities, it inspires other countries to do the same. This can lead to a global movement towards providing free housing for all, promoting equality and social justice at an international level.

In conclusion, while it is true that providing free housing may impact the government's budget, the numerous benefits it brings outweigh the financial considerations. The safety and security of the population, the positive attitude of the government towards its citizens, and the potential for inspiring change on a global scale are all compelling reasons to support the provision of free housing for those in need.
