Topic:

"In today's world of advanced science and technology, we still greatly value our artists such as musicians, painters and writers. What can the arts tell us about life that science and technology cannot?"

Essay:

"Although the world has seen a considerable growth of science and technology, art still holds a certain importance in the cultural and spiritual life of people because of the benefits which humans have received from the artist such as musicians, painters and writers not from scientists. In my opinion, there are many valuable things that almost all people can study based on these arts.

Admittedly, nowadays, humans have many kinds of entertainment activities which help the spiritual enhancement of life. These activities are not indispensable for balancing life and work. It is because of its benefits that it is easy to find short-term art classes widely held in many centres as a method of relaxation, emotional expression and creativity. This is extremely necessary in daily life while science and technology seem to ignore them.

Besides, art also creates the human value through their work that no technology can be transmitted. Take a writer for an example, they have strong power to spread meaningful stories and positive energy to communities that can contribute, maintain and develop the good culture and tradition of each nation. Moreover, In medicine, doctors used music as a tool to ease the pain of the treatment regimens, especially for patients who meet mental problems. This not only brings many benefits but also reduces recovery time. 

In conclusion, science and technology bring much knowledge about modern things but art helps people deeply understand and know what they want to be.Therefore, both of them should be maintained and developed equally to ensure no loss of traditional and cultural value of the nation."


Revised:

Although the world has witnessed tremendous growth in science and technology, art continues to hold significant importance in the cultural and spiritual realm of people. This is due to the unique and irreplaceable contributions made by artists such as musicians, painters, and writers. In my view, art offers valuable insights and perspectives on life that cannot be captured by science and technology.

Firstly, art plays a crucial role in providing individuals with various forms of entertainment that enhance their spiritual well-being. These activities, although not essential for survival, serve as a means of achieving a work-life balance. This is evidenced by the widespread availability of short-term art classes aimed at promoting relaxation, emotional expression, and creativity. Such activities are considered necessary for individuals in their daily lives, despite science and technology often overlooking their importance.

Moreover, art provides a platform for the expression of human values that cannot be conveyed through technology alone. Consider the impact of writers on society, for instance. They possess the ability to spread meaningful stories and positive energy, thereby contributing to the preservation and development of a nation's culture and traditions. Additionally, music has been employed as a therapeutic tool in the field of medicine, to alleviate the emotional pain experienced by patients undergoing treatment. This not only brings numerous benefits but also reduces recovery time.

In conclusion, while science and technology contribute vast amounts of knowledge about modern advancements, art enables individuals to deeply understand and pursue what truly matters. Therefore, both must be equally nurtured and developed to ensure the preservation of a nation's traditional and cultural values.
