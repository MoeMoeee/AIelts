One of the main reasons why government intervention is necessary is because the internet provides an easy way for criminals to commit cybercrime. This includes fraudulent websites that are designed to steal personal information, which can result in the loss of money and other assets. For example, individuals can be easily tricked into clicking links that lead to websites that look legitimate but are actually fake. Consequently, the government should regulate such games and limit their access to ensure that young people are protected. Lastly, cyberbullying has become rampant on the internet. In order to combat this problem, the government can introduce strict laws to prosecute cyberbullies and hold them accountable for their actions.

In conclusion, although the internet has made our lives easier, it has also brought new challenges and increased the potential for various illegal activities. Therefore, there is a strong case for the government's intervention to regulate online activities, protect users, and prevent cybercrime.
