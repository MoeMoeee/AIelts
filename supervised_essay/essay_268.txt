Revised:

Nowadays, taking a gap year between finishing school and starting university has become increasingly popular. This essay will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this practice and provide a logical conclusion.

There are two main advantages of taking a gap year: improved academic performance and personal growth. Firstly, during this break, students can take a break from the academic rigour and engage in different activities they are interested in. This allows them to return to their studies with renewed energy and focus. Secondly, a gap year enhances personal growth. For instance, students who participate in various fields can build confidence, gain experience in different activities, learn from their mistakes, and ultimately prepare themselves for higher studies.

However, taking a year off also has some major disadvantages. One of these is the increased risk of involvement in illegal activities. When students do not find appropriate work during this time, they may resort to selling drugs, trafficking, or committing crimes. This is particularly true for those who do not have a strong financial background and cannot afford travel expenses, internships, or other opportunities. Additionally, long holidays can make students lazy and tired. They may lack a sense of maturity and struggle to find suitable jobs, face breakups with their partners, experience family pressure, and encounter other hindrances to personal growth.

In conclusion, taking a gap year has certain negative effects, such as the potential for unlawful actions and losing enthusiasm. However, it also has an extremely positive influence on students' academic achievement and self-actualization.
