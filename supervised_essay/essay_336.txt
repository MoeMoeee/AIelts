To address this problem, the government should implement an effective and efficient public transportation system. Another significant problem faced by city dwellers is the high cost of living. The demand for housing in big cities often outweighs the supply, leading to skyrocketing prices. This makes it increasingly difficult for individuals, particularly those with lower incomes, to afford decent housing. To tackle this issue, the government should focus on increasing the supply of affordable housing. This could be done by implementing policies that promote the construction of affordable housing units and providing subsidies or financial assistance to individuals or families in need. Additionally, the government could also explore the possibility of developing satellite towns or expanding existing urban areas to create more affordable housing options.

In conclusion, living in big cities can be challenging due to problems such as traffic congestion and high living costs.
