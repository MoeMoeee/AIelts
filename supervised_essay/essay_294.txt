"It has been suggested that primary school children should learn how to grow vegetables and care for animals. This essay will discuss whether the advantages of this outweigh the disadvantages.

Moreover, the use of pesticides can contaminate the soil, affecting the overall health of plants and the bacteria present in it. This can pose risks to children's health, as exemplified by cases of diarrhea after engaging in planting activities.

In conclusion, although there are some drawbacks to primary school children learning how to cultivate plants and care for animals, the benefits outweigh the disadvantages. Teaching children about organic foods can improve their dietary choices and promote healthier lifestyles. Additionally, it can foster a sense of responsibility and empathy towards animals. However, it is crucial to ensure the safety and well-being of children by implementing proper guidelines and precautions in agricultural activities."
