"The government is responsible for protecting a nation's cultural identity. Thus, some people believe new buildings should be built in traditional styles. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?"

Essay:

"Culture is the core foundation of any country. Ancient buildings and monuments are strong symbols which indicate the richness of the culture. Hence, some believe that the new constructions should be alligned in a manner which preserve the true identity of the culture. In my view, I am partially agreed with the afforementioned statment and I will further illustrate my ideology in the next few paragraphs. 

The proposed practice is already inplace in several countires. The most popular example is Italy where the new constructions are throughly governed with several laws specially in the areas with the ancient values. The positive trend is most of the other countries are also adhereing to the same practice and government and law makers are in a process of imposing new rules. The preservation of cultural values would highly important to the countries which are relying largely on tourism income. 

In the contrary, the world is constantly enriching with new and effective engineering and architectural concepts. Thus new constructions could be planned to attain great level of effectiveness and space management. Moreover, it would be ideal to use the latest technologies and designs in new constructions rather than adhereing to old designs. The said notions is also very crucial aspect to be looked at considering the significance of space management to cater to the demands of the growing population. 

In conclusion, it is agreed that preserving the ancient values when constructing the new buildings are imparative. However, the said practice should be limited to the areas which has significant ancient values while in other cases to use the most updated technology and designs."

Revised:

Culture is an essential part of every nation's identity, and preserving it is vital. Some people suggest that new buildings should be constructed in traditional styles. This essay will explore this suggestion and examine whether I agree or disagree with it.

The conservation of cultural values is a priority for many nations and should be reflected in any new building construction. Italy is an outstanding example of a country that preserves its ancient values by imposing laws that govern the construction of new buildings in areas of historical or cultural significance. This practice is spreading globally, with governments and law-makers introducing new regulations. This approach is particularly important for countries that rely heavily on tourism income.

However, we cannot ignore the current technological advancements in architecture and engineering that could be used to design functional and space-efficient buildings. Therefore, when planning new buildings, it would be beneficial to incorporate modern and innovative concepts rather than solely relying on traditional designs and methods. Especially in areas of high population growth, using cutting-edge technologies and designs are essential.

In conclusion, while it is important to preserve the integrity of cultural landmarks while constructing new buildings, implementation of traditional styles should be limited to specific areas with cultural significance. Furthermore, the integration of innovative technologies and designs in new building constructions should be encouraged to meet the modern-day demand. By doing so, we can strike a balance between preserving cultural heritage and meeting the contemporary construction requirements.
