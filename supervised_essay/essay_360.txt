Revised:

In many countries, schools are facing severe issues related to student behavior. This essay will discuss the causes of this problem and suggest potential solutions.

There are various reasons why student misbehavior is on the rise. Firstly, overcrowded classrooms contribute to this issue. With a large number of students in a single class, teachers find it challenging to maintain discipline. As an example, some schools in Saudi Arabia have classrooms where teachers have to handle over 50 students. This makes it nearly impossible for teachers to effectively manage such a high number of students. Secondly, the prevalence of violence on social media has a significant impact on student behavior. Children are exposed to violent images and videos on platforms like Facebook and Twitter. Consequently, they may imitate what they see online and exhibit similar behavior in school.

To address these problems, several measures can be taken. One solution is to allocate more funds to the education sector to build additional schools. This step would help reduce the student-teacher ratio, enabling teachers to handle classes more effectively. By creating smaller, manageable class sizes, teachers can devote more attention to individual students and maintain discipline more easily. Furthermore, stricter regulations should be enforced to prohibit violent images on social media. Implementing stringent laws against such content would discourage its circulation and protect children from harmful influences.

In conclusion, the rise of student misbehavior in schools can be attributed to overcrowded classrooms and exposure to violence on social media. However, there are solutions available. Governments should invest more in the education sector to build additional schools, allowing for smaller class sizes and improved discipline. Moreover, measures should be taken to prevent the circulation of violent images on social media platforms. By addressing these issues, schools can create a more conducive environment for learning and cultivate positive behavior among students.
