Topic:

"Nowadays, not enough students choose science subjects in university in many countries. What are the reasons for this problem? What are the effects on society?"

Essay:

"One of the heated issues is there are less students following science-related subjects in university. Many people think that this situation cannot cause any negative effects. However, I do believe that the lack of science students might cause severe consequences on society. In this essay, I will give my own opinion on the reasons and causes of the shortage of science students.

There is no doubt that the number of students choosing science has decreased dramatically in recent years. There are many reasons for this. For one thing, many undergraduates found science-related subjects difficult and not attractive enough. For another, science subjects taught in the current educational program are so impractical that undergraduates cannot apply what they learn in real life situations. The most familiar example of this is that Vietnamese students have to learn a lot of unrealistic knowledge that cannot help them much in their work. On the other hand, many students having a great passion for science may not have enough money to pay because of the high  educational fee in university. It seems quite clear that these factors have a significant contribution to the decrease of science students.

It is well known that the shortage of undergraduates learning science may cause extreme effects on society. As many students found science unattractive, students can easily get bored of science. The science subjects boredom is the main reason for the lack of science students which can lead to the shortage of science workers in the future. Take Vietnam for an example, if this situation keeps continuing, Vietnamese patients will have struggled with the decrease in the number of doctors in 50 years’ time. To address this problem, the Vietnamese government has worked out many measures to attract more students to choose to study science at university. In a nutshell, this problem must be tackled quickly. 

In conclusion, the decline in science-related  subjects students can cause terrible effects. Both governments and their citizens have to take action to address this constructively."


Revised:

One of the pressing issues in many countries is the declining number of students choosing science subjects in university. While some argue that this situation might not have negative implications, I firmly believe that the shortage of science students can have severe consequences on society. This essay will discuss the reasons for this problem as well as its effects on society in order to shed light on the issue.

There are undoubtedly multiple factors contributing to the decline in science students. Firstly, many undergraduates perceive science-related subjects as difficult and unappealing. In addition, the current educational curriculum often lacks practical applications of scientific knowledge, making it challenging for students to see the relevance of what they are learning in real-life situations. For instance, in some countries, students are required to learn excessive amounts of theoretical knowledge that may not be applicable to their future careers. Moreover, financial constraints also play a significant role, as some students with a keen interest in science might not have the means to afford the high educational fees required for science degrees. It is evident that these factors collectively contribute to the decrease in science students.

The shortage of students in science subjects can have serious ramifications for society. As many students find science unattractive, they easily lose interest and become disengaged from the subject. This disinterest directly translates into a shortage of qualified professionals in scientific fields, which can have long-term effects on the workforce and the progress of society. To illustrate, if this trend persists, countries like Vietnam may face a shortage of doctors in the future, leading to significant challenges in providing healthcare services for their citizens. Recognizing this issue, governments must take proactive measures to attract more students to pursue science-related subjects at university.

In conclusion, the decreasing number of students choosing science subjects in university can have far-reaching consequences. It is crucial for both governments and citizens to address this issue promptly and effectively. By addressing the reasons behind this decline and implementing measures to make science subjects more appealing and practical, we can ensure a steady supply of skilled scientific professionals in the future, thus benefiting society as a whole.
