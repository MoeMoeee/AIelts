Revised:

In recent times, there has been a growing preference for exploring international cuisines and foods from other cultures instead of consuming local dishes. This trend can be attributed to the widespread availability of the internet and social media, as well as the increasing affluence of society. In this essay, I will delve into the factors driving this trend and explore whether it is a positive or negative development.

One driving force behind this trend is the easy access to the internet and social media, which expose individuals to various cultures and traditions, including their cuisines. Particularly, young people are attracted and curious about exploring different ethnic foods and culinary practices. For example, food blogger Irfan from Chennai has amassed millions of followers on his vlog videos, where he travels to different countries to share information and knowledge about various food items and cuisines. Such trends not only benefit the tourism industry by attracting millions of visitors, but they also facilitate cross-cultural appreciation and understanding. Moreover, the prevalence of cutting-edge technology and readily available information on websites further fuels the interest of many to explore and taste international cuisines.

Another factor contributing to the popularity of international cuisines is the increasing affluence of society. With higher disposable incomes, people are more inclined to indulge in exotic food experiences rather than sticking to familiar local dishes that are available everywhere. Take Singapore, for instance, often praised as a food paradise in Asia. The city-state attracts a large number of tourists due to its plethora of restaurants, including high-class establishments and five-star hotels that offer a wide range of international buffets and an array of local food items from around the world. The melting pot of diverse cultures in Singapore also fosters social bonding and friendship among individuals from different backgrounds.

In conclusion, both the support for local food and the exploration of international cuisine bring benefits to individuals and countries. By embracing different ethnic groups and their cuisines, we can build a more inclusive and sustainable future in this interconnected world. However, it is important to strike a balance between the two, ensuring that the promotion of international cuisines does not overshadow the preservation and appreciation of local food culture.
