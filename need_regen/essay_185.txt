Revised:

The world has witnessed a significant increase in leisure, business, and commercial flights in recent years. This essay will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of such flights, as well as whether flight should be further regulated.

One of the main advantages of air travel is the boost it provides to global trade and economy. It facilitates the development of international trade relations and allows businesses to expand their operations across borders. Additionally, flying saves a considerable amount of time. In today's fast-paced world, individuals are often swamped with numerous responsibilities. By choosing air travel, they can cover long distances in much less time, thereby increasing productivity. For example, a businessperson can attend meetings in different countries within a matter of hours, resulting in more efficient collaboration and decision-making.

However, it is important to acknowledge the disadvantages of increased air travel. Firstly, flying is an expensive mode of transportation. The high cost of manufacturing and maintaining aircraft makes it inaccessible for many people. This creates a divide between the wealthy, who can afford air travel, and the less affluent, who are limited to alternative modes of transport. Another drawback is the negative impact of flights on the environment. Airplanes emit greenhouse gases, contributing to climate change and air pollution. Therefore, the indiscriminate expansion of flights could exacerbate these environmental issues.

In conclusion, the rise in global air travel has both advantages and disadvantages. It stimulates economic growth and saves time for individuals, but it can also be costly and environmentally damaging. To ensure sustainable development, flight schedules should be regulated to minimize environmental harm and increase accessibility for all.
