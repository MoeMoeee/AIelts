Topic: 
"The growth of online shopping will one day lead to all shops in towns and cities closing. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:
"Humanity has always been experimenting with different technologies to make sales perform better. In recent years, online shopping websites and apps have become quite popular, which makes some individuals think that they can close down offline shops. In my opinion this claim is overstated and it is not going to happen in the near future. However, online services may be more preferable for certain people.

On the one hand, offline shops afford people the opportunity to make more deliberate and weighed decisions on items to buy. This is especially the case for clothes, as it is much more convenient to try on them immediately in a fitting room. Another significant benefit of offline shops is that a person can ask for a consultation from a seller. This is crucial for older individuals, who need extra guidance and assistance during their shopping. For instance, my grandparents are not advanced users of the internet, therefore they always choose to see products they want to buy with their own eyes.

On the other hand, online services have developed substantially over the recent years. They are constantly looking for new opportunities to make their apps and websites convenient to as many users as possible. The reason for that is the advanced improvement of internet technologies, which allows them to experiment with different styles of user interface to find the ones that perform better for everyone.

In conclusion, the majority of individuals use online services because of their convenience. However, there will always be people who find them difficult to use or simply enjoy the offline shopping experience. This is the reason why offline shops are going to coexist with online apps and websites making shopping available for all the people."

Revised:
The rapid growth of online shopping has sparked a debate regarding the future of traditional brick-and-mortar stores. While some argue that the rise of online shopping will eventually lead to the closure of physical shops, I believe that this claim is exaggerated and that offline shops will continue to coexist with their online counterparts. In this essay, I will present arguments supporting this view and highlight the benefits of both online and offline shopping experiences.

Offline shops offer unique advantages that cater to certain consumer preferences. For instance, when it comes to purchasing clothing, trying on items in a fitting room allows customers to make more informed decisions. This tactile experience cannot be replicated by online shopping alone. Additionally, the presence of knowledgeable sales staff provides valuable assistance and guidance, particularly for older individuals who may require extra support during their shopping process. Personally, I have observed how my grandparents, who are not well-versed in using the internet, prefer to physically examine products before making a purchase.

On the other hand, online shopping has evolved significantly in recent years, striving to enhance user convenience. E-commerce platforms and websites continually invest in improving their apps and interfaces, making them user-friendly and accessible to a wider range of consumers. The constant advancements in internet technology have facilitated experimentation with different interface styles to enhance the overall shopping experience. Thus, online shopping has become increasingly attractive for individuals seeking convenience and a wide variety of options.

In conclusion, while it is true that online shopping has gained popularity due to its convenience, it is unlikely to completely replace traditional brick-and-mortar stores. Offline shops offer distinct advantages, such as the ability to try on items and seek personalized assistance from sales staff. Moreover, there will always be individuals who either find online shopping challenging or simply enjoy the tangible experience of offline shopping. Therefore, both online and offline shops will coexist, ensuring that shopping remains accessible and enjoyable for all consumers.
