This essay will discuss how stiff competition and lax testing systems are the main cause of this problem, and the most suitable solutions are imposing heavier punishments on violators and revamping testing facilities.
The main cause of this problem is the fierce competition that exists in any sports. In other words, most many professional athletes feel that they have to take substances like steroids to give themselves an advantage over other strong opponents. Another reason is the lack of strictness in testing procedures. Many athletes who take advantage of banned substances can still get off scot-free due to the holes in testing systems. For example, a high-profile mix martial artist named Jon John who is notorious for using PED described how easy it was to get away with cheating in an interview in 2015.
A viable solution is to heavily punish lawbreakers. If sports clubs and establishments raise the fine for using banned substances, many athletes will think twice before making attempt to cheat. Another the way to deal with this issue is to upgrade testing amenities. This will eradicate any holes existing in the system and ensure that the test result is highly accurate. For instance, after the UFC had made major investments to provide their staff with the latest testing equipment, many fighters in their organization got caught.
In conclusion, strong competition and ineffective testing systems are the main cause of this problem, and the most suitable solutions are enforcing harsher punishments on violators and reforming testing facilities."

Revised:
In many professional sports, the use of banned substances by athletes to enhance their performance has become increasingly common. This essay will discuss the causes of this phenomenon, including intense competition and inadequate testing systems. It will also propose two potential solutions: imposing stricter penalties on offenders and improving testing facilities.
The primary cause of this issue is the intense competition prevalent in professional sports. Athletes often feel compelled to resort to substances such as steroids in order to gain an edge over their formidable opponents. Another contributing factor is the lack of rigidity in the testing procedures. Many athletes who use banned substances manage to evade detection due to weaknesses in the testing systems. For instance, Jon Jones, a well-known mixed martial artist, admitted in a 2015 interview how easy it was to escape punishment for using performance-enhancing drugs (PEDs).
One effective solution is to implement severe punishments for those who violate the rules. By increasing fines and penalties for using banned substances, sports organizations can deter athletes from attempting to cheat. Additionally, it is crucial to enhance the testing facilities. This will help eliminate any loopholes in the system and ensure the accuracy of test results. For example, the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) significantly invested in state-of-the-art testing equipment, resulting in the identification of numerous fighters who were using banned substances.
In conclusion, the prevalence of banned substance abuse in professional sports is primarily caused by intense competition and inadequate testing systems. To address this issue, it is recommended to impose stricter penalties on offenders and improve the quality of testing facilities.
