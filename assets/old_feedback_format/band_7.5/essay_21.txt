Revised:
The topic of the necessity of freedom of speech in a free society is widely debated today. Personally, I firmly believe that the liberty to express oneself is essential for a community. This is because freedom of speech fosters societal progress and the absence of this fundamental right undermines the justice system.

To begin with, it is widely acknowledged that when every citizen has the autonomy to voice their opinions, it contributes to the advancement of the government. This means that if individuals have the right to speak about community issues, it positively impacts the country's development. In contrast, if citizens are denied this right, the government will lack awareness of prevailing problems, hindering progress. A prime example of this is the comparison between the United States and the former Soviet Union (USSR). The United States, where citizens enjoy full freedom of speech, continues to lead as one of the most advanced countries in the world. Conversely, the USSR, which suppressed freedom of speech, collapsed years ago.

Another crucial aspect to consider is that a fair justice system cannot exist if the state suppresses the voices of its people. When individuals are unable to advocate for themselves, authorities are deprived of valuable perspectives. A striking example is North Korea, which starkly contrasts with other modern countries by depriving its citizens of the right to speak freely. Consequently, the community suffers from injustices, as individuals may be punished for actions that are acceptable in other nations.

In conclusion, it is evident that the right to voice one's opinions is vital for the development of a government and the establishment of a just society. By allowing freedom of speech, governments can foster progress, while the absence of this right undermines societal justice.
