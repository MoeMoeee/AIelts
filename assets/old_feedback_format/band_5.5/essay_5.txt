Revised:
In recent times, there has been a debate about whether students in universities should be allowed to choose their courses or only study subjects that will be useful in the future, such as science and technology-related courses. While some argue that students should be restricted to studying only courses that are beneficial in the long term, others maintain that they should have the right to choose their courses. In this essay, I will analyze both views and present my opinion in favor of allowing students to choose the courses they like.
On the one hand, taking science and technology-related courses can bring many benefits as the world is constantly evolving with the use of technology, especially in the healthcare industry. Most of the equipment used in hospitals is implemented through technology, which helps to make work easier and reduces the rate at which patients die. However, I believe that students should be given the opportunity to study courses they like because not everyone has the ability to cope with science and technology-related courses, and studying their dream courses will benefit both them and the community, just like those who studied science and technology-related courses.
On the other hand, when students are allowed to choose the courses they like, they can be better prepared for the career they want to pursue. More time will be spent studying what they are interested in, and they will learn with more enthusiasm as they are studying what they like.
In my opinion, courses studied in tertiary institutions aside from science and technology-related courses are also well recognized because of their impact on society. For example, lawyers and judges play a crucial role in delivering justice, which has helped society at large by creating a favorable atmosphere for us all to live in. When students are given the opportunity to study the courses they like, it broadens their range of interests and helps them develop important characteristics such as self-confidence and high self-esteem because they were not forced to study courses they did not like.
In conclusion, I believe that students should have the right to choose the courses they like in university as it will allow them to develop their skills, interests, and passions, which in turn will benefit both themselves and society.
