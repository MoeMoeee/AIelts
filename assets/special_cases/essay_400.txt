Revised:

The given table presents data on the percentage of mobile phone owners utilizing various features on their phones in Vietnam in the years 2005, 2008, and 2010.

Overall, it is evident that there was a decrease in the majority of features during the specified period, except for the ability to take photos. Making calls remained the most commonly used feature among cellphone users.

In 2005, the proportion of individuals using their phones to take photos saw a slight increase from 65% to 70% in 2008, and subsequently grew to 77% by the end of the period. Conversely, the percentage of phone users recording audio experienced a sharp decline from 78% in 2005 to 63% in 2010.

Making calls was the most popular feature throughout the three years, with 100% of phone users employing this function in 2005 and 2008. However, it slightly decreased to 97% in 2010. Moreover, the second most frequently used function was sending and receiving text messages, which accounted for 82% in 2005. However, this figure gradually declined to 68% by 2010.

In summary, the majority of features on mobile phones witnessed a decline in usage over the period, except for the feature of taking photos. Making calls continued to be the most preferred function among phone users in Vietnam.
