The given flow chart illustrates the process of recycling aluminium cans. 

To begin with, the first step involves the collection of aluminium cans in designated containers specifically for reusable items. These containers are then transported to recycling facilities where the process of waste recovery commences. Next, the accumulated jars undergo cleaning and sorting procedures to eliminate any waste materials. 

Following this, the cans are passed through specialized machines that shred them into small pieces. Once in pieces, they are compressed to form metal blocks. 

Subsequently, these blocks are placed inside large ovens and heated to a specific temperature for melting. Once softened, the aluminium is passed between two significant rollers, which flatten it to a thickness ranging from 2.5 mm to 6 mm, depending on its intended application. 

Ultimately, the flattened metal is transported to specific factories that produce beverages. Within these facilities, the aluminium canisters are filled with drinks and subsequently distributed to various shops and local markets for consumers to purchase. Remarkably, in the UK, a noteworthy 74% of distributed aluminium cans are reused. 

Overall, the process of recycling aluminium cans involves several steps such as collection, cleaning, shredding, compressing, melting, flattening, and reusing. The aforementioned statistics regarding the reuse of aluminium cans in the UK reveal the effectiveness of the recycling process in this particular country.
