Revised:

The two pie charts provide information about the primary exercise objectives and locations of a selected group of adults aged between 20 and 50 years. Overall, it can be observed that the percentage of individuals prioritizing different exercise objectives varies, with work achievement being the highest priority for both age groups, accounting for 32% and 31% respectively.

To begin with, when analyzing the exercise objectives of adults aged between 20 and 50, the charts reveal that work achievement is the most significant priority for both age groups. Around 32% of individuals aged 20-50 consider achieving work-related goals as their primary exercise objective. This indicates that a substantial proportion of adults in this age group prioritize career advancement and professional success as a means of finding happiness.

Interestingly, the second most common exercise objective for both age groups is losing weight, accounting for 25% and 26% of adults aged 20-50 respectively. This suggests that a significant number of individuals within this age range consider physical fitness and weight management as essential for their overall happiness.

Furthermore, the charts highlight some differences in exercise objectives among the two age groups. For individuals aged 20-30, maintaining mental health is the third most common exercise objective, representing 20% of the responses. On the other hand, adults aged 40-50 prioritize enhancing physical strength as the third most important exercise objective, accounting for 18%. This indicates a shift in priorities as individuals progress through different life stages.

Regarding exercise locations, both age groups prefer to exercise in parks or other outdoor spaces. In particular, approximately 50% of individuals aged 20-50 choose parks as their preferred exercise location. This suggests that being surrounded by nature and benefiting from fresh air while engaging in physical activity is highly valued among adults within this age range.

In conclusion, the charts illustrate the primary exercise objectives and locations of adults aged between 20 and 50. While work achievement and weight loss are the most common exercise objectives for both age groups, there are also some variations, such as the emphasis on mental health for younger adults and physical strength for older adults. Additionally, it is evident that exercising in natural environments, such as parks, is preferred by individuals in both age groups.
