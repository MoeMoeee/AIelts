Here is the edited essay with an improved structure:

"The given pie charts provide information on how dangerous waste products are managed in three different countries, namely the Republic of Korea, Sweden, and the United Kingdom.

Overall, the Republic of Korea primarily relies on recycling as the main method of dealing with pollution, while Sweden and the United Kingdom predominantly resort to burying waste.

According to the illustration, in the Republic of Korea, recycling accounts for the largest proportion of handling dangerous waste products, at 69%. Burial and incineration are also utilized, with figures of 22% and 9% respectively. Conversely, in Sweden, only a quarter of the waste (25%) is recycled, while underground techniques are employed in 55% of cases, and incineration accounts for 20%.

Meanwhile, in the United Kingdom, the majority of dangerous waste (82%) is buried using underground techniques. Furthermore, the use of chemical treatment and dumping at sea methods both represent 8% of waste disposal methods. Incineration is the least common method employed, accounting for only 2% of pollution management in the UK."
