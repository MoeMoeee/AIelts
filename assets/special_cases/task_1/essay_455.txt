Revised:

Overall, it is evident that the process of making a cup of tea can be divided into two distinct stages: those involving the kettle and those involving the cup. Additionally, the process does not require any specialized equipment and can be completed within approximately 10 minutes.

The initial stages of preparing tea involve the use of the kettle. Firstly, water is drawn from a tap and poured into the kettle. Subsequently, the kettle is plugged in and the water is heated until it reaches boiling point, which typically takes around 5 minutes.

The subsequent stages take place in the cup. While waiting for the kettle to boil, a teabag is placed in the cup. Once the water is boiled, it is then poured into the cup, directly onto the teabag. The tea is left to steep for approximately 5 minutes before the teabag is removed.

Finally, to enhance the taste, sugar or milk can be added to the tea according to individual preferences. Once the desired additions have been made, the tea is ready to be consumed.

In conclusion, the process of making a cup of tea is straightforward and consists of two main stages: the utilization of the kettle and the actions performed in the cup. With minimal equipment required and a relatively short time frame, tea can quickly be prepared and enjoyed. (165 words)
