Revised:

"The diagram provided illustrates the process of brick manufacturing in the construction industry.

Firstly, the clay is extracted with the assistance of a digger. Following this, the clay is cleaned of any debris and dirt using a metal mesh and a roller. Once cleaned, the clay is then mixed with sand and water in a mixer. This mixture is subsequently transferred to a mold or nippers to give the desired shape of the brick. 

After molding, the bricks are dried in an oven for a duration of 24 to 48 hours. Once dried, they are fired in the oven twice. Initially, they are subjected to a moderate temperature of 200°C - 980°C, and then they undergo a second firing at a much higher temperature of 870°C - 1300°C. Subsequently, the bricks are placed in a cooling chamber, where they remain for a period of 48 to 72 hours. 

Once completely cooled, the bricks are packaged and transported for delivery.

Overall, the process involves multiple stages, including clay extraction, cleaning, mixing, molding, drying, firing, cooling, and packaging, to manufacture bricks for use in the building industry."
