The process starts when adult salmon lay their eggs, which are then incubated. Subsequently, the eggs hatch, leading to the emergence of young salmon. During the initial stages of their development, these young salmon spend their time growing in freshwater areas. As they progress, they move to the next stage, swimming in the estuary. Furthermore, the diagram illustrates that the salmon then venture into the ocean. 

Following the completion of the first six stages, the salmon enter a phase where they relocate to rearing territory. Finally, once they have reached a sufficient size and have completed the maturation process, the salmon embark on a migration towards their spawning areas. Here, they reproduce, laying eggs, and continue the cycle by traveling between the river and the ocean.

In conclusion, the diagram demonstrates the life cycle of a salmon, revealing seven distinct stages from egg incubation to adult fish migration. The remarkable aspect of this life cycle is the movement of salmon through three different locations.
