Topic:

"The graph shows the amount of money spent on books in Germany, France, Italy and Austria between 1995 and 2005."

Essay:

"The graph gives information how much money in Germany, France, Italy and Austria spent on books during the period from 1995 to 2005. The unit are measured in million dollars.

Overall, it can be seen that there was a rise in the quantity of money spent on books in four different countries, Germany had the highest figure throughout the period, while Austria witnessed the most dramatic increase in latter half of the following period.

Looking at the figures in further detail, people in Germany seemed to be keen on reading books, which showed the number of money spent on books in Germany started at 80 million, after which it experienced a slight rise to 90 million in 1999, then reached a peak at nearly 100 million in 2005. By contrast, 30 million people spent on books in Austria in 1995, stood at the lowest, slightly rose to 40 million in 1999 and a final increase sharply to over 70 million in 2005.

Turning to the remaining statics, the figure for France was at 55 million at first, followed by a subsequent climb to 70 million in 2001 and peak at over 70 million in 2005. Similar changes as Germany can be seen in the figure for Italy, which started at 50 million in 1995 then witnessed a slight rise to 55 million before ending the period at just over 60 million."

Similarly, Italy experienced a similar pattern of book spending, starting at 50 million in 1995 and slightly increasing to 55 million before reaching just over 60 million in 2005.

In summary, the graph illustrates the upward trend in book expenses in Germany, France, Italy, and Austria from 1995 to 2005. Germany consistently had the highest spending on books, while Austria observed a remarkable increase in book expenditures during the latter part of the period.
