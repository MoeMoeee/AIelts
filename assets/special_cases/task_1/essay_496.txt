Topic:

"The diagram below shows how glass is recycled."

Essay:

"The diagram illustrates the process of making a glass usable again.

Overall, the process consists of six stages, beginning with putting it into a recycle bin and ending up with delivery to the supermarket.

First of all, used or empty glass bottles are kept separately in a green recycle bin where the signature of the recycling is displayed. After that, with the use of a vehicle the collected materials are taken to the treatment plant and stored there. Next, the collected glasses  are washed using a sterilising machine to ensure that the harmful bacteria and germs are totally eliminated.

In the next stage, the sterilised bottles are crushed and melted with the crushing and melting machine where they are converted into liquid. Following this, the liquids are poured into the moulding machine where they are designed to provide an accurate shape and size of the bottle.

Finally, they are made ready to be delivered to the supermarkets."

Subsequently, these collected materials are transported to a treatment facility by means of a vehicle and stored there temporarily. Following this, the collected glass bottles undergo thorough washing in a sterilizing machine to ensure the complete eradication of harmful bacteria and germs.

Moving on to the next phase, the sterilized glass bottles are crushed and melted in a crushing and melting machine, resulting in the conversion of glass into a liquid state. This liquid glass is then poured into a molding machine, which imparts precise shapes and sizes to the bottles.

Finally, the glass bottles are prepared for shipment and are dispatched to supermarkets for further use.

In conclusion, the process of recycling glass commences with the collection of glass bottles, carries through various stages like cleansing, crushing, and molding, and concludes with the delivery of the recycled glass to supermarkets.
