Furthermore, subjects such as music, art, and drama play a pivotal role in enhancing students' self-confidence. Through engaging in creative lessons, students acquire the skills to perform in front of audiences, granting them opportunities to interact and collaborate with their peers. Consequently, they become aware of their true potential and exhibit greater confidence in their abilities. For example, psychologists often recommend drama classes to individuals grappling with social anxiety, as these classes facilitate the development of self-assurance.
In conclusion, this essay wholeheartedly supports the idea that music, art, and drama carry equal weightage to other subjects in primary education. These subjects empower children to uncover their latent talents at an early stage, while also bolstering their self-assurance. By affording young learners the chance to explore their creativity and develop their personalities, schools can contribute significantly to the holistic development of their students. Thus, recognizing the value of arts-related subjects in primary education is essential.
