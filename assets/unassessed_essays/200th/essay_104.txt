Topic:

"Many animals are being hunted to the brink of extinction in order to fulfill the demand and greed of mankind for decorative purposes as well as for other reasons, such as traditional medicine.
Why does this happen?
Will this improve or get worse in the future"

Essay:

"Being under long-term repercussion of human, the ecological balance has become vulnerable and some species are endangered. Frequently, an overhunting phenomenon is caused by a lack of regulation; nevertheless, hopefully, a conscious part of modern society will tackle this issue.
A commonly held belief is that if an exploitation of animals tends to transform to a burgeoning industry with a significant market value, it has to be controlled by governments to prevent devastating circumstances, such as extinctions of species. Obviously, poaching or illegal animal trade must be punished, in view of the fact that some individuals are going to lose an empathy and humanity under pressure of a huge amount of money. For instance, gray whales had been virtually eradicated by a commercial whaling, until worldwide protection organizations had not signed a moratorium on this activity.
Although current situation seems to be disastrous, several experts suppose that the future of Mother Nature could be an optimistic. The more resources on the social media show documentary films or a terrifying statistic about the animal kingdom destroying, the more people are involved in an essential process of a nature preservation. World Wildlife Fund is a prime example of an organization, which leads to create an awareness about taxing fauna’s issues and definitely has become a base of positive changes.
In conclusion, animals are treating by the irresponsible human’s behavior. Being a dominant species on our planet, unfortunately, not all of individuals realize a value of a creature’s life without strict laws. Yet I strongly believe that progressive society is able to turn over this trend, and the situation will get better."

Introduction:
It is a well-known fact that many animals are hunted to the brink of extinction to meet the insatiable demand and greed of mankind. The reasons for this are mainly due to decorative purposes and traditional medicine. In this essay, we shall discuss the underlying causes of this issue and also predict whether the situation will improve, remain the same, or worsen in future.

Body Paragraph 1:
The overhunting and exploitation of animals are often attributed to a lack of regulation. This has led to the death and extinction of some animals. However, governments can play a crucial role in preventing such circumstances by imposing strict laws and severe penalties. For instance, the commercial whaling of gray whales led to their virtual eradication. The situation improved only after international protection organizations enforced a moratorium on whaling activities.

Body Paragraph 2:
Although the current situation seems to be dire, there is hope for the future. The prevalence of social media provides an excellent opportunity for experts and organizations to create awareness about environment preservation. World Wildlife Fund is a prime example of an organization that has succeeded in creating awareness about the dire need to protect endangered species. As more people become aware of the importance of protecting nature, the situation may gradually improve.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, it is clear that the irresponsible behavior of humans has endangered the lives of many animals. Despite this dire situation, it is possible to turn the tide. Governments can play an active role in regulating the hunting and trade of animals. Additionally, awareness can be created through social media, leading to a more positive attitude towards preserving endangered species. It is, therefore, possible that the situation will improve in the future if we work towards creating a sustainable environment for all living organisms.
