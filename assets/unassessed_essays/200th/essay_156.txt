Revised:

The impact of international marketing has always been a topic of debate in various countries. While some individuals view it as an intrusive and unwanted idea, others consider it a necessary and economical means of gaining knowledge, exchanging cultures and languages. This essay will explore both perspectives before presenting my opinion.

On one hand, detractors of international marketing argue that the increasing promotion of multinational companies has negatively impacted local businesses and economies. Small-scale businesses have lost their charm due to the arrival of big corporations, which attract a larger customer base. This has created a sense of dependency on foreign trade and foreign investment. For instance, transnational companies like Amazon have abruptly put many local bookstores out of business. Also, according to a recent survey, local food items struggle to keep up with the global products, which makes the cost of living rise. Furthermore, the government’s lack of consideration for public opinion before implementing various free trade policies also adds to the problem.

On the other hand, some people believe that the advantages of international marketing far outweigh any drawbacks. They see international marketing as an opportunity to learn and grow from each other's cultures and exchange of technology as well. In addition, it is believed that the global market can provide employment opportunities for graduates, and consequently, increase the country's economic conditions. For example, the arrival of McDonald's and KFC proved beneficial for the country since it provided a platform for young people to work and earn a living while studying, which is not possible with local businesses.

In conclusion, it is true that international marketing has indisputably brought both advantages and disadvantages to nations. However, we need to have a sense of responsibility and consideration to balance both sectors. Governments should keep in mind the interests of local businesses, and should consider public opinion before implementing any free trade policies. Thus, I maintain that, with proper regulations and limitations, international marketing can be an additional means for a country to progress in a positive direction, both economically and socially.
