Topic:

"In spite of the advances made in agriculture, many people around the world still go hungry. Why is this the case? What can be done about this problem?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, in agricultural sector, they have made a great advancement but many people around the world still go hungry. This essay will shed some light on the causes and what solutions can be developed to remove this problem.

First of all, the government is not paying attention to agricultural sector. They ponder that it is not important for them. In spite of having many technologies which can make more harvest, the regime is not using it. May be, they are making low budget on agricultural department. That's why it takes more time to grow rice or any other food. Moreover, the farmers are not getting enough price for their hard-work. And because of that they are unable to use the technology of agriculture. So, the price of food getting higher. One good illustration is that Bangladesh is a country where poor people go hungry. Because in Bangladesh, the price of food is very high and they don't have sufficient money to buy food.

To solve this problem, the administration should give more effort for the development of agricultural sector. They should increase the number of technology which can make good crops in short time. At the same time, the government should give enough money to the farmers so that they can produce more crops. the price of food should be low so that every people can buy food for their living. Then there will be no people who will go hungry for a single day.

To sum up, it is essential to give more importance on agriculture because without food no individual can live in this world. It will be impossible for mankind to prevent extinction without food."

Revised:

Despite the advances made in the field of agriculture in recent times, a significant number of people worldwide are still suffering from hunger. In this essay, we will explore the causes of this problem and suggest potential solutions.

The inadequate attention given to the agricultural sector by governments is one of the primary reasons behind this problem. In many cases, governments do not consider this sector a priority. They often allocate insufficient funds to the agricultural department, limiting the use of advanced technologies that could help produce more crops in less time. As a result, farmers are unable to leverage the full potential of technological advancements, and the price of food keeps soaring. Bangladesh is a case in point, as it is a country where a large number of people are unable to afford food due to high prices.

To address this pressing concern, governments should consider investing heavily in the agricultural sector. They should create favorable policies that help farmers access advanced agricultural technologies by offering subsidies and incentives. Additionally, the government should ensure that farmers receive adequate compensation for their hard work. When farmers can reap the fruits of their labor, it is reasonable to expect that they will be motivated to enhance the quality and quantity of their harvests. This, in turn, would help minimize the price of food items, making it more affordable for everyone.

In conclusion, it is fundamental to recognize the significance of agriculture because food is a basic necessity for human survival. Ignoring this sector's development would pose a looming threat to humanity worldwide. Governments must prioritize agriculture and adopt policies that incentivize farmers to use cutting-edge technology to increase their yields while reducing the cost of food products.
