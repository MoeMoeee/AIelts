Topic:

"Some people think environmental problems are too big for individuals to solve. Others, however, think that those problems can be solved unless individuals take some action. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"The issues related to the environment such as global warming, climate change and pollution are getting more and more severe, which has become too challenging for each citizen to handle. However, others believe that people need to take responsibility for protecting and preserving their surrounding environment, so the huge mentioned problems can be mitigated. In this essay, both views will be discussed before giving my perspective.  

It is justifiable why the idea that individuals cannot deal with  huge environmental problems is in favour. The first reason is that humans cannot control or change unprecedented harsh natural phenomena, namely floods, drought, tsunamis, and other weather patterns. The only thing they can do is to adapt and have preparation to minimize the damage caused by these problems. Besides, it should be the government’s responsibility to introduce laws imposing on their citizens on protecting the environment. The explanation is no one can change a situation without  collective power. In other words, people are willing to work together only if there is a proper guide and encouragement from their authorities. For example, how a normal city dweller stops an industrial factory from performing their working activities without releasing carbon dioxide into the air. Hence, what is needed is  regulation from the authorities to control the factory’s harmful activities to the environment. 

On the other side, there are a variety of reasons why people advocate solving  environmental problems individually. Admittedly, it is humans’ daily activities that lead to all types of pollution. To be more specific, it has been proved that the amount of CO2 emitted from private cars and motorbikes pollutes the air and the act of throwing garbage and plastic bags into the rivers pollutes water. In the same way, the increasing use of electric household appliances, especially air conditioners, contributes to intensifying the greenhouse effect which causes global warming. Therefore, if each person can adopt a green lifestyle, for example, saying no to plastic bags, using public transportation to commute or cutting down on their use of electric appliances at home, the environmental issues can be eliminated significantly. 

In conclusion, I hold the same view that environmental sustainability depends greatly on each citizen’s discipline and responsibility. However, to guarantee individuals act in protecting the Earth to work effectively, it is  governments around the world that should enact proper environment-protecting laws for their people to oblige."


Revised:

There is a growing concern about environmental issues such as pollution, climate change, and global warming. While some people argue that these problems are too complex for individuals to handle, others believe that people need to take responsibility for mitigating environmental problems. In this essay, I will discuss both views before presenting my own opinion.

On the one hand, those who believe that individuals cannot solve environmental problems argue that these issues are beyond human control. For instance, natural disasters like droughts, tsunamis, and floods are unforeseeable, and people can only take measures to minimize their damage. Moreover, it is primarily governments that should be responsible for implementing environmental regulations and enforcing laws. That is because environmental problems cannot be solved without collective power. A regular citizen cannot stop an industrial factory from emitting harmful gases into the air, whereas the government has the authority to regulate factories' activities. Therefore, to tackle environmental issues, governmental initiatives are necessary.

On the other hand, proponents of individual actions for the environment assert that humans are the primary cause of pollution and environmental degradation. Private cars and motorbikes emit carbon monoxide into the air, and improper waste disposal pollutes the water. Moreover, the use of electric appliances and air conditioners contributes to global warming. Therefore, if individuals adopt sustainable approaches in their daily lives, such as using public transport, reducing plastic usage, and conserving energy consumption, significant progress can be made in tackling environmental issues.

In my opinion, the responsibility of environmental protection should be shared between governments and individuals. While people should be encouraged to adopt environmentally friendly practices in their daily lives, governments should enact robust environmental laws and regulations to promote sustainable actions. Moreover, governments should provide incentives and education initiatives to encourage individuals to adopt sustainable lifestyles.
