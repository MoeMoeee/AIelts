Revised:

Nowadays, people often travel from one country to another for holidays or vacations, which is commonly referred to as global tourism. While global tourism brings various economic benefits, it also presents some concerns regarding its impact on local communities and the environment. This essay will explore the potential advantages and disadvantages of international tourism.

On the one hand, global tourism has several benefits to multiple parties. Firstly, the government receives revenue from taxes paid by visitors, which can strengthen the country's economy. Additionally, local businesses such as accommodation providers, transportation services, and restaurants can earn a profit from visitors, contributing to the local economy.

On the other hand, international tourism may bring some negative consequences, especially in nature tourism. The impact of human activities on the natural habitat could lead to environmental degradation and even the endangerment of animal species. Hence, governments should implement preventative measures to ensure that undisciplined tourists do not engage in practices that could harm the environment. For example, every tourist could be required to be accompanied by a trained local guide.

In conclusion, global tourism brings a myriad of advantages to a country and local populations, but at the same time could also bring challenges like environmental degradation. Therefore, it is crucial for governments to establish comprehensive regulations to ensure the sustainable development of the tourism industry that does not negatively impact local communities or the environment.
