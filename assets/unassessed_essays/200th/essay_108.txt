While some people believe that tourists should be responsible for social and environmental issues, others think that they do not need to do that. For example, museums should address environmental crises which are generated by their visitors.

Firstly, in many places especially entertaining constructions, tourists there cause a lot of social and environmental problems. A good example is conflicts between local people and visitors due to a difference in cultural background. A great solution is that travelers should research thoroughly about codes of conducts in the areas they have a tendency to explore. Secondly, although there are a number of labours collecting garbage which is discharged by visitors, it is not effective if tourists do not improve their awareness to protect the environment.
In conclusion, some individuals argue that travellers do not pay more attention to protecting the environment and reducing social issues whereas I support the opinion that they need to cooperate with service providers to be responsible for these kinds of problems."

Revised:

Over recent years, there has been an increase in the number of tourists worldwide, leading to debates about their social and environmental responsibilities. While some individuals argue that tourists have no obligation to bear these responsibilities, I strongly agree with the view that travellers ought to play a role in addressing these issues.

On the one hand, it is important to acknowledge that tourists cannot be held solely responsible for social and environmental problems. They often encounter various social and environmental burdens in their daily lives and look forward to their holidays as a means of relaxation and rejuvenation. Additionally, service providers bear a significant responsibility for addressing societal and environmental challenges. For example, museums, tourism agencies, and governments must develop policies and plans that promote sustainable tourism practices to minimize impacts on the environment. Other stakeholders, such as local communities, have a vested interest in managing issues such as over-tourism and cultural clashes.

On the other hand, I maintain that tourists have a role to play in reducing social and ecological problems. Firstly, irresponsible behaviour by tourists can have significant negative social and environmental impacts on destinations. For example, conflicts may arise between tourists and locals due to cultural differences and excessive use of resources can result in damage to natural habitats. Therefore, travellers should make a concerted effort to adhere to local norms and customs and show respect for communities. Secondly, tourists can proactively contribute to the sustainability of destinations through responsible actions, including minimizing their ecological footprint by reducing waste and conserving natural resources.

In conclusion, I affirm that tourists should work cooperatively with service providers and other stakeholders to enhance their social and environmental responsibility. While it is true that tourists cannot be held solely responsible for social and environmental problems, their cooperation is essential in reducing the impacts of tourism and contributing to sustainable development.
