Topic:

"Many people believe that big businesses in addition to generating profits should be involved in and responsible for the social life of the community. To what extent do you agree or disagree? Give your opinion."

Essay:

"Large companies have always attempted to come into money; however, the issue of businesses' social compulsory responsibilities is a controversial issue among people. Personally, I entirely agree with the viewpoint that businesses have to do more for the community than make money, and I will explain why in this essay.

If large companies should take participate in social life in society, by fair and impartial looking, it could have some two-way profits for both businesses and the communities. This is the fundamental idea which its reasons are categorized into three main parts. First, Since people might not apprehend that all large companies are run with the sole purpose of marketing as much advantage as possible for their own, businesses could gain people's trust and foster them to invest their money in their projects. As an example, companies could replace their prioritization from economic growth with gaining people's satisfaction. 

Second, marketing development is the other advantageous consequence. It is notable, for instance, that it could offer a chance for discovering young people's talents who could not be a going concern on their own. Since businesses have to emphasize innovation and creativity in order to survive in a competitive world, they could hire talented young people, which is profitable for both sides. Third, it is generally accepted that thriving the community is imperative for a successful business. In other words, it seems that most of the prosperous activities flourish in a bustling atmosphere. So, if large companies are involved in society, they could indirectly create effective circumstances for their companies' future of business.

In summary, it might be said that expecting social obligations for large businesses in society is unacceptable because it is the government's responsibility for social supports, and companies should not take charge of it. However, it seems that the effects of this approach appear to be overwhelmingly positive, and its two-way beneficial impacts of the large companies and the communities are undeniable."


Revised:

It is widely believed that big businesses should not only focus on generating profits but also be responsible for the social well-being of the community. While this is a contentious issue, I firmly agree with this viewpoint. In this essay, I will explain why businesses should take more responsibility for society.

To begin with, engaging in social activities is a win-win situation for both businesses and the community. One of the primary benefits is building trust and loyalty among people. People do not always understand that the sole aim of companies is to maximize their profit. However, if companies prioritize the satisfaction of the community, they can gain people's trust and encourage them to invest in their projects. This, in turn, could foster long-term relationships and enhance the company's reputation.

Moreover, companies can use social activities as a means of promoting their brand. One way of achieving this is to search for talented young people who may have ideas but lack the resources to bring them to fruition. By providing them with employment opportunities, companies can promote innovation and creativity and discover talent that could help them in the long run. This approach could also potentially help companies stand out in a highly competitive market and attract customers who share similar values.

Finally, it is important to note that thriving communities often attract successful businesses. Therefore, businesses should work towards fostering community development to create the right atmosphere for their operations. This could involve investing in infrastructure development, education, healthcare, or other social issues. By doing so, they could indirectly boost future business prospects.

In conclusion, while some argue that companies should not be responsible for social support and obligations, the benefits of their involvement in society are too significant to ignore. When businesses prioritize social welfare, they could foster trust with the community, promote their brand, and create thriving environments for their operations.
