Revised:

It is widely acknowledged that the tourism industry, particularly in relation to flights and accommodation, contributes to a range of problems, including pollution, traffic congestion, and water pollution. In light of this, there is a debate as to whether governments should enforce additional taxes on flights or accommodations as a means to restrict the negative impacts of the tourism industry. This essay will argue that imposing extra taxes may not be the most effective solution and could potentially exacerbate existing issues.

It is undeniable that imposing extra taxes on flights and accommodations may dissuade potential tourists from traveling. Many individuals, particularly those with limited financial resources, save money meticulously to explore new countries. Any increase in ticket prices or accommodation costs could render their travel plans financially unattainable. For instance, I recall a situation where a friend of mine, who was not financially well-off, had to pay exorbitant prices for his trip, despite the soaring costs. Consequently, imposing additional taxes may not deter tourists, as their desire to explore new destinations would outweigh the increased expenses.

While extra taxes on flights and accommodations might discourage some individuals from traveling, it is doubtful that this measure would effectively address the pollution issues associated with tourism. Pollution is not solely caused by tourism; local residents also contribute to environmental degradation by discarding garbage irresponsibly. Additionally, many countries rely heavily on tourism for economic sustenance. If people were to stop traveling to these nations, it could lead to a host of economic problems. Rather than imposing extra taxes, it would be more prudent for governments to focus on developing and implementing sustainable tourism practices to alleviate the negative impacts on the environment.

In conclusion, the imposition of extra taxes on flights or accommodations as a means to restrict the problems associated with the tourism industry might not be the best solution. It could hinder the opportunities for individuals with limited financial resources to embark on travel experiences and may not effectively mitigate pollution issues. Governments should instead focus on implementing sustainable tourism practices and working towards minimizing the negative impacts of the industry. This would ensure that tourism can continue to thrive while safeguarding the environment for future generations.
