Topic:

"Some people are fond of buying new gadgets, phones, or laptops. Is this a good thing or a bad thing? Discuss your opinion"

Essay:

"Owning latest electronic devices has become a trend. It is observed that some people like to purchase latest electronics devices, mobile phone or laptops. In my opinion, this is detrimental to the individual as it might impact their financial stability and personal life.

Firstly, any electronic device comes with a hefty price tag. The frequency with which companies launch mobile phones and laptops is frantic. Every single day a new phone or a gadget is launched in some part of the world. People who are fond of buying new products end up selling older products so that they can fund the new purchase. However, as the resale price of these goods is low, they recover only a fraction of the original cost and hence end up putting in more money for purchasing the device. Spending too much money on new gadgets can put individuals under tremendous financial distress.

Secondly, the compulsive urge to buy the latest version of gadgets is not good for the environment. When people update the devices frequently, a lot of older devices will end up in landfills. From there they will pollute the land and water. They could even emit harmful radiations.

Thirdly, most electronic gadgets have different operating procedures. Hence it becomes essential for the buyer to spend a significant amount of time to familiarize themselves with the new device. This wastes precious time that could be used for other useful purposes.

In conclusion, I believe the practice / habit of buying latest devices, mobile phones or laptops has a detrimental effect on the personal life and financial condition of an individual."


Revised:

Here is the edited essay:

Owning the latest electronic devices has become a trend. It is observed that some people like to purchase the latest electronic devices, mobile phones, or laptops. In my opinion, this trend has both positive and negative impacts on individuals.

Firstly, any electronic device comes with a hefty price tag. The frequency with which companies launch mobile phones and laptops is frantic, and people who are fond of buying new products end up selling older products so that they can fund the new purchase. However, as the resale price of these goods is low, they recover only a fraction of the original cost and hence end up putting in more money for purchasing the device. Consequently, spending too much money on new gadgets can put individuals under tremendous financial distress.

Secondly, the compulsive urge to buy the latest version of gadgets is not good for the environment. When people replace their devices frequently, a lot of older devices will end up in landfills, which can cause severe environmental damage. Furthermore, older devices can emit harmful radiation, which poses a significant threat to human health.

Thirdly, most electronic gadgets have different operating procedures, and it becomes essential for the buyer to spend a significant amount of time familiarizing themselves with the new device. This wastes precious time that could be used for other useful purposes.

However, the love for gadgets has its merits as well. Owning the latest electronic devices enhances personal productivity, especially for students or those working remotely. Mobile phones, for instance, enable people to stay connected to their families and friends, as well as facilitate emergency communications.

In conclusion, I believe that the trend of buying the latest devices, mobile phones, or laptops has both advantages and disadvantages, but the disadvantages outweigh the benefits. To strike a balance, individuals should be cautious about their purchasing habits, considering their financial situations, the environmental impact, and the potential time-wasting factor.
