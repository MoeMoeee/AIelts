Revised:

Nowadays, individuals participate in various sports depending on their time, finances, or interests. There are popular sports such as running, cycling, jogging, and swimming, which everyone has likely engaged in at least once in their life. In addition, there are uncommon physical activities, such as big wave surfing, bull riding, base jumping, or boxing, which are considered dangerous due to their high injury rates. Some people believe that these sports should be prohibited, while others maintain that individuals should be free to choose their preferred sport activities. This essay will discuss both perspectives and offer my opinion.

Those in favor of banning dangerous sports argue that they pose a significant threat to an individual's welfare. They fear that their loved ones, whether it be their children, parents, or spouse, may become injured, resulting in high medical costs and lengthy recovery times.

However, most individuals, including myself, disagree with this notion, believing that participating in these sports is a way to fully embrace life. Engaging in such activities provides a surge of adrenaline, allowing individuals to break away from their mundane routines. These activities add value to our lives and act as a blessing, reminding us to appreciate our loved ones and cherish every day spent with them. Furthermore, these sports can help release our negative thoughts and emotions, promoting positive mental health. As long as safety precautions are followed and individuals participate with professionals, it is possible to minimize potential risks.

In conclusion, those who choose to participate in these risky activities should proceed with caution, ensuring they have properly evaluated the protocol and taken the necessary time to learn about their chosen sport. With adequate knowledge, appropriate protective measures, and professional guidance, it is possible to minimize associated risks. Therefore, individuals should have the freedom to choose their preferred sport activities.
