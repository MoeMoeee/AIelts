Essay:

"In modern society, some people believe that the government should financially support the retired, ageing population while others think they should be able to be financially independent. I believe that the government should definitely support retired workers, especially the ones who have had a larger impact on the country’s well-being.

There is no denying that most people have a rough idea on when they would like to retire so planning in advance and having the ability to save money for their future is a big reason why the government shouldn’t pay. Therefore, it would make sense for the government to refuse financial support in this case. As a result, they would need extra support financially.

Now to why the government should pay, there are multiple reasons. One of them being that many individuals pay taxes and have jobs that they can only sustain during their younger years. For example, soldiers working in the army retire younger and find themselves jobless after retirement. Not to mention how much they sacrifice for their country, only to be left with nothing as soon as they finish their service. As far as taxes are concerned, most of the money that the public spend, many of them never see the impact it has on the country. 

In conclusion, the government should aim to implement schemes that give back to some of the most valuable members of our society. Without them, the country would struggle to function effectively. To give them nothing in return is a disgrace to the government."

Revised:

In modern society, there is a divide in opinion regarding whether the government should provide financial support to retired people or if individuals should take care of their own expenses. In my view, I believe that the government should indeed support retired workers, particularly those who have made significant contributions to the nation's well-being.

It is true that many people have a general idea of when they would like to retire, and therefore they have the ability to plan ahead and save for their future financial needs. In such cases, it would be reasonable for the government to refrain from providing financial assistance. However, there are many jobs that pay minimum wage, especially those directly associated with the government, which do not offer enough income to allow individuals to save for retirement. Therefore, these individuals would require additional financial support.

On the other hand, there are several reasons why the government should assume the responsibility of financially supporting retired individuals. Firstly, many people pay taxes and have jobs that can only be sustained during their younger years. For instance, soldiers serving in the military retire at a relatively young age and often find themselves unemployed after retirement. They have made immense sacrifices for their country, only to be left with nothing once their service ends. Moreover, a significant portion of the public's expenses through taxes does not directly benefit them; therefore, it is only fair for the government to prioritize the welfare of retired citizens.

In conclusion, the government should strive to implement programs that provide assistance to some of the most esteemed members of our society. Without their contributions, the country would struggle to operate effectively. Neglecting to offer them financial support is a discredit to the government.
