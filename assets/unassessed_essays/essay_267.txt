Topic:

"Some people buy their children a large number of toys to play with.what are the advantages and disadvantages of having large number of toys."

Essay:

"Toys are essential tools that aid in a child's development in different aspects. A number of parents buy their children many toys to discover. In this essay, I will tackle the different pros and cons of this matter. 

To begin with, there are a lot of material things  that are beneficial in a kid's journey. Musical instruments, rattles, stuffed toys, and bicycles are just some of the toys that can be found in the mall which are very attractive to the youngsters. As a mother, I am guilty of buying almost anything I think my son needs and also those he's been wanting to have. Because of that, I can observe that he has developed different skills such as learning how to ride the bike, or how he finds music interesting, and even playing pretend using dolls. 

On the other hand, because he has a lot of toys to choose from, some toys are being neglected. A child can only focus on what he finds enjoyable at the moment and forgets to play with the others. As a result, it will just be placed in the stock room and sometimes be forgotten. As an example, my husband and I bought our offspring a  basketball ring because he was so  into basketball at that time, but eventually got tired of it and puzzles that shifted his attention. 

To end, whilst toys are essential things needed for a child's development, the number should also be kept to a minimum. Aside from it can save space in your place, you can also prevent from spending too much and you can be assured that they are going to play with it."


Revised:

Toys play a crucial role in a child's overall development. Some parents opt to provide their children with a large array of toys for them to engage with. This essay will explore the advantages and disadvantages associated with having a large number of toys.

Firstly, a wide variety of toys can be beneficial for a child's learning journey. Musical instruments, rattles, stuffed animals, and bicycles are just a few examples of the toys available in stores, capturing the interest of young minds. As a mother, I am guilty of purchasing toys that I believe my son needs or those he desires. Consequently, I have observed the development of various skills in him, such as riding a bicycle, a fascination with music, and even engaging in imaginative play with dolls.

However, an excess of toys can lead to neglect of some items. A child's attention may be solely focused on the toys that they find enjoyable at that moment, causing them to neglect other toys. Consequently, these neglected toys end up being stored away and forgotten. For instance, my husband and I bought our child a basketball hoop because he was passionate about basketball. However, he eventually lost interest and became engrossed in puzzles, diverting his attention away from the basketball hoop.

In conclusion, while toys are important for a child's development, it is advisable to limit the number of toys provided. This not only helps to save space within the home, but also prevents excessive spending. By limiting the number of toys, parents can ensure that their children spend quality time engaging with each toy and fully benefit from the overall learning experience.
