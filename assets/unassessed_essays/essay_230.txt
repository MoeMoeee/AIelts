Topic:

"What are the pros and cons for children of watching television?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, most people have televisions at home as they have become an important part of our lives like smartphones or tablets. As a result, a great number of children are fond of watching cartoons or family films on TV. There are both advantages and disadvantages to this. In this ,essay I am going to examine how kids benefit from televisions and what drawbacks there are.

First of all, watching TV can be beneficial for children's development as there are millions of programmes with educational elements. Besides specific programmes with the only aim to teach a kid something about the natural world or literacy, there are family films. Not only do they entertain children but also introduce them to the basics of  life such as kindness, tolerance and love. 

Another advantage for kids watching TV is that they can obtain patience. The ability to sit still is very useful since it can come in handy at school, college or even work. What is more, when youngsters watch movies or animated movies, they learn to focus all their attention on the screen which can help them to develop concentration.

However, according to  scientific ,research it is proven that watching television is harmful to the eyes. Consequently, spending a lot of time in front of a TV screen can lead to visual impairment, which is a common problem among young people. 

Furthermore, television can be addictive ,especially for little boys and girls. A lot of parents use cartoons as a way to calm their youngsters, which can result in a child's obsession with TV. In this case, a youngster becomes more interested in the imaginary world, shown in films and animated movies than in reality. Finally, a child can find it hard to socialize in the community.

To sum up, watching TV can be both advantageous and disadvantageous for kids, as they can learn something new but it can result in worsening of their eyesight. Following this, the pros overweight the cons since parents can control the amount of time their infants spend in front of the screen in order to prevent negative effects."


Revised:

Nowadays, televisions have become an integral part of our lives like smartphones or tablets, and most people have them at home. As a result, a great number of children are fond of watching cartoons or family films on TV. There are both advantages and disadvantages to this. In this essay, I am going to examine how kids benefit from television and what drawbacks there are.

First and foremost, watching TV can be beneficial for children's development, as there are millions of programs with educational elements. Apart from specific programs designed to teach a child something about the natural world or literacy, there are also family films. These programs not only entertain children but also introduce them to the basics of life such as kindness, tolerance, and love.

Another advantage of children watching TV is that it can help them develop patience. The ability to sit still is very useful, as it can come in handy at school, college, or even work. Furthermore, when youngsters watch movies or animated films, they learn to focus all their attention on the screen, which can help them develop concentration skills.

However, scientific research has proven that watching television is harmful to the eyes. Consequently, spending a lot of time in front of a TV screen can lead to visual impairment, which is increasingly common among young people.

Furthermore, television can be addictive, especially for young boys and girls. Many parents use cartoons as a way to calm their children down, which can result in a child's obsession with TV. In such cases, children become more interested in the imaginary world depicted in films and animated movies than in reality. This can lead to difficulties in socializing with others in the community.

In conclusion, watching TV can be both advantageous and disadvantageous for children. While they can learn something new, it can also result in worsening of their eyesight. However, the benefits of television outweigh the drawbacks, as parents can control the amount of time their children spend in front of the screen to prevent any negative effects.
