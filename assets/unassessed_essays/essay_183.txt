Topic:

"Some people think governments are responsible for peoples health, others believe its an indiviuduals responsibility. 
Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Healthcare remains a very crucial part of out basic amenities. The topic of whether or not people or the government should be held accountable for issues partaining to health,  remains very controversial. In my opinion, the government should be solely in charge of the health of its citizens at all times. 

On one hand, health care as well as the funding for the research into cures and vaccines are expensive. Private firms and singular entities cannot bear the burden alone. Furthermore, the cost of hospital admissions, investigations and medications would cripple the finances of the individual without governemt aid and policies. All fingers are not equal, therefore, the government plays a huge role by providing subsidized care to patients in its publicly funded facilities. For example, data from a survey carried out by the World Health Organization , in Nigeria , in 2013, revealed that many Nigerians depend entirely on the government to access excellent medical care. 

On the other hand, people are the masters of their bodies. Most diseases in the world today are preventable, that is, they are mostly caused by poor lifestyle choices. Individuals should incocate the habit of proper diet, exercise and regular medical checkups to keep fit and also  to detect illnesses at the early stages. 

In conclusion, while some are of the opinion that governments should be responsible for health, others are of the school of thought that it rests on the individual. I believe that it is the  responsibility of governments to cater for the health of all its nationals."


Revised:

The issue of who is responsible for healthcare remains contentious, with some arguing that individuals should take care of their own health, while others believe that it is the government's responsibility. In my view, the government should have the primary responsibility for ensuring the health of its citizens.

One reason why the government should take the lead on healthcare is that it is expensive to fund the research and treatment of diseases. Private entities cannot bear the burden alone. Moreover, healthcare costs such as hospital admissions, investigations and medications can be too much for individuals to manage on their own. With publicly funded health facilities, the government can provide subsidized care to patients. For instance, in Nigeria in 2013, a survey by the World Health Organization found that many Nigerians rely on the government for access to quality medical care.

Even though governments have a key role to play in healthcare, individuals have an important part to play in taking care of their health. Many illnesses today can be prevented by adopting healthy lifestyles, such as regular exercise, healthy diet and check-ups. By putting effort into staying healthy, individuals can reduce the burden on the government's healthcare system.

In conclusion, while there are differing opinions about whether individuals or governments should be responsible for healthcare, I believe that it is primarily the government's duty to ensure the wellbeing of its people. Nevertheless, individuals should also take the initiative to maintain good health in order to reduce pressure on the government's resources.
