Revised:

Nowadays, various methods are used in educational institutions to facilitate teaching, including books, films, and computers. While these modern techniques, such as videos and computer-based learning, have proven to be effective, I do not agree with completely replacing books with them. In this essay, I will elaborate on my viewpoint with relevant examples.

Firstly, it is undeniable that these technological advancements bring benefits to both students and teachers, making the education process more flexible. For instance, tutors could use videos or short movies to provide practical examples related to the subjects they are teaching. This can enhance learners' understanding of complex curriculums or difficult subjects, such as geography and geology. Therefore, these methods have gained overwhelming endorsement, particularly in developed countries like America and Europe.

However, books hold a traditional and indispensable role in education. They have been used as the primary tool for teaching for ages. For example, in developing countries where technology can be expensive or not readily available, books are the only accessible and affordable resource for teaching. Thus, we cannot deny their significant importance. Additionally, books can captivate young learners with colorful photos and stories, encouraging their interest and engagement. Individuals who have a reading hobby tend to find books more attractive than videos and films.

In conclusion, while it may be true that using books in teaching can at times seem tedious and time-consuming, they cannot be completely replaced by other modern techniques. Moreover, films, TV, and computers are not accessible in many places around the world. Therefore, a balanced approach that incorporates both books and technological advancements should be adopted to ensure effective and inclusive education globally.
