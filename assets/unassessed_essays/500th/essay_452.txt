On the one hand, it is argued that the best way to stay fit is by joining a gym. The main reason for this is that gyms offer a wide range of tools and equipment for training. Each equipment serves a specific purpose and can be tailored to suit individual fitness goals. For example, by paying for a gym membership, one can have access to various machines and weights to work on different muscle groups. Additionally, gyms often have trained instructors who can guide individuals on their fitness journey, ensuring that they exercise correctly and effectively.

On the other hand, many believe that doing everyday activities such as walking and climbing stairs is sufficient to stay fit. This viewpoint is based on the idea that these activities do not require any additional costs as they can be easily incorporated into one's daily routine. Moreover, engaging in regular physical activities like walking not only helps maintain physical fitness but also improves mental well-being. It is also argued that leading an active lifestyle by performing everyday activities can have a positive impact on overall health, as it allows individuals to burn calories and maintain a healthy weight.

In conclusion, both joining a gym and engaging in everyday activities have their benefits when it comes to staying fit. Joining a gym provides access to specialized equipment and professional guidance, while doing everyday activities is a cost-effective way to maintain physical and mental fitness. Personally, I tend to believe that joining a gym is the best way to stay fit because it offers a wide range of lessons and the presence of trained instructors who can provide personalized guidance. However, it is important to choose an approach that suits one's lifestyle, preferences, and budget.
