Revised:

Advertisements for snacks and toys have a profound influence on children and their parents. Some argue that these advertisements should be banned in order to protect children. In my opinion, I agree that advertising to children should be prohibited due to the potential harmful effects it can have on them.

Firstly, children are easily influenced by advertisements and often pester their parents to purchase the products they see. Advertisements often promote unnecessary and consumeristic products that children do not actually need. This can lead to family disputes and strain the relationship between parents and children. For example, the Coca-Cola company targets children with its advertisements without mentioning the negative impact of soft drinks on their physical and mental health. Therefore, teaching children how to critically evaluate advertisements is crucial in order to protect them from making impulsive and unnecessary purchases.

Furthermore, young children struggle to differentiate between actual TV programming and commercials. As a result, they view advertising as part of their entertainment and rely on it heavily for information. Using popular cartoon characters and film heroes to endorse products gives children the false impression that everyone has access to material possessions. This can perpetuate an unrealistic sense of entitlement and fuel the desire for material goods. For instance, advertising fast food to children can have negative long-term consequences for their health. Some countries have already implemented bans on advertisements targeting young children, recognizing the potential harm it can cause.

In conclusion, the impact of advertisements on children and their parents cannot be overlooked. Banning advertising to children would not only benefit parents, but also protect the mental and physical health of children. Teaching children to critically analyze advertisements is essential to empower them to make informed choices. By implementing such measures, we can create a safer and healthier environment for our children.
