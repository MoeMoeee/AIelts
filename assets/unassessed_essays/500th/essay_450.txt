On the one hand, the prohibition of private vehicles in certain city areas undoubtedly has numerous advantages for the environment. For instance, in Germany, people rely on public transportation to commute to work or school, minimizing the negative impact on the environment. Additionally, various awareness campaigns and advertisements in the country discourage activities such as smoking in forests, ensuring a cleaner and healthier environment.

On the other hand, it may pose challenges for individuals who have familial responsibilities and work long hours. For instance, parents often rely on private vehicles to safely transport their children to school or other educational institutions. Moreover, professionals with demanding schedules and multiple business engagements throughout the city may find it difficult to reach their destinations on time due to the limited schedules of public transportation services.

In conclusion, I strongly advocate for the implementation of restrictions on private transportation in other cities, as it offers significant benefits in protecting the environment and improving the quality of life for residents. While there may be some difficulties for certain individuals, such as parents and professionals, alternative solutions can be explored to address their specific transportation needs. Overall, the advantages of this measure outweigh the challenges it may pose, making it a necessary step towards a sustainable and cleaner urban environment.
