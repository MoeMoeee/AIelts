Revised:

In many countries, experience is valued more than knowledge in the workplace. Some argue that this is unfair to skilled individuals who may have innovative ideas. This essay will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of valuing experience over knowledge.

On one hand, in some countries such as India, companies prefer to hire individuals who have at least two years of experience and offer them high salaries. However, this can result in skilled individuals losing out on certain job opportunities regardless of their potential. Additionally, experienced individuals may use traditional methods instead of embracing new and innovative approaches. According to a survey conducted by The Indian University, the economy has suffered as a result of this practice.

On the other hand, countries like Canada offer recent graduates opportunities to showcase their skills, which leads to a higher level of innovation and economic growth. In 2019, Canada offered over 85,000 permanent residencies to immigrants with technical skills.

In conclusion, valuing experience over knowledge has its advantages and disadvantages. While experienced individuals may bring a level of expertise to the workplace, this practice can limit opportunities for skilled individuals with innovative ideas. Governments should consider giving younger individuals a chance to demonstrate their skills and improve methods in a creative way."
