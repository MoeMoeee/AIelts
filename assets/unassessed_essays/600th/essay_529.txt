Revised:

In today's society, there is a divergence of opinion regarding whether it is preferable to lead a life of routine or embrace change. While some individuals feel comfortable with stability and avoid any alterations, others argue that change brings about positive outcomes. This essay will discuss both perspectives and provide my own opinion with the support of relevant examples.

On one hand, many people prefer to avoid changes as they find comfort in maintaining a stable and predictable lifestyle. For instance, individuals who choose to pursue a specific career path without seeking promotions or advancements demonstrate a preference for a routine life. Additionally, some individuals fear the uncertainties that come with change and are unwilling to take risks, such as the potential of losing their job or facing criticism from colleagues. This aversion to change is especially evident in Japanese society, which places great emphasis on cultural beliefs and traditions that discourage individuals from seeking promotions, as it is considered disrespectful.

On the other hand, proponents of change argue that it plays a vital role in personal growth and has numerous positive effects. They claim that by embracing change, individuals are able to reach their full potential and achieve their goals. Furthermore, scientific studies have shown that individuals who welcome change are more likely to maintain good mental health. This is because when people engage in new activities or expand their knowledge in unfamiliar areas, their brains are stimulated to adapt and overcome challenges. Consequently, change is closely linked to personal development and mental well-being.

In conclusion, while there are differing opinions on the value of routine versus change, it is evident that change has a significant role in personal development and mental health. However, some societies, rooted in culture and tradition, are hesitant to embrace change. From my own perspective, being open to change is of utmost importance in the contemporary world.
