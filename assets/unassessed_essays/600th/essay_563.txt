Newspapers, for example, have a long history of journalistic standards and fact-checking, which can provide readers with a sense of trust and reliability.

This digital divide can hinder individuals from accessing online reading material, making printed media a more accessible option. In addition, some readers still prefer the convenience and portability of physical books and newspapers, especially in situations where internet access is limited or impractical.

On the other hand, online platforms offer numerous benefits to users and are likely to dominate as a medium in the future. Firstly, digital content is easily accessible and can be accessed from anywhere with an internet connection. This convenience allows readers to stay up to date with the latest news and access a wide range of literature without the need to carry around heavy physical copies. Additionally, the digital format also allows for interactive features such as hyperlinks, multimedia content, and search functionality, which enhance the reading experience and provide additional sources of information.

In conclusion, while there are valid reasons why printed newspapers and books will still have appeal in the future, I believe that online platforms will dominate as the preferred medium for reading.
