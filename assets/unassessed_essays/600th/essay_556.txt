Topic:

"Nowadays many people travel to foreign countries for pleasure. Some believe this travel has a negative impact on the countries travelled to.To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, there is an increasing number of tourists who visit foreign countries to get  relaxation. However, it is argued that travelling has negative influences on that particular area. I completely agree with this point of view because it may lead to crime and pollution of that destination.

The large number of tourists may cause a crime. It is undeniable that travellers bring  benefits to some particular countries. Since sightseers spend a considerable amount of money at the time of travelling, it could lead to an increase in the local businesses` income. But despite this fact, due to the throng of visitors in the bustling areas, there is a high chance for pickpockets to steal tourists` belongings elusively. This should be controlled with the help of the military or artificial intelligence otherwise their businesses will break down soon.

In addition, such a large volume of trippers can cause environmental problems to fragile habitats and historical sites. As holidaymakers may generate a massive amount of rubbish during the trip, disposal of this produced waste can be a challenging task for locals. Moreover, the mismanagement of litter can have a negative impact on the biosphere of that state. To illustrate,  Thailand has failed to mitigate water pollution and protect the natural environment due to the soaring amount of plastic rubbish left after the visit of tourists. 

In conclusion, as far as I am concerned, I totally agree that travellers from overseas are causing  damage to the destination travelled to. For one thing, an increasing number of visitors can create a crime, for another the environment may be altered and polluted by human beings."


Revised:

Nowadays, an increasing number of people are traveling to foreign countries for leisure and relaxation. However, some individuals argue that this trend has a negative impact on the countries that are visited. I completely agree with this perspective as traveling can lead to an increase in crime rates and environmental pollution in the destination countries.

To begin with, the influx of tourists can contribute to a rise in criminal activities. While it is true that travelers bring economic benefits to certain countries by spending their money, the crowded tourist areas become prime targets for pickpockets and thieves. Without proper measures in place, such as the involvement of military or the use of advanced technologies like artificial intelligence, businesses in these areas will suffer, and the local economy will be negatively affected.

Furthermore, the large number of tourists can pose significant environmental challenges for delicate ecosystems and historical sites. As tourists generate a substantial amount of waste during their trips, the disposal of this waste becomes a daunting task for locals. If not managed properly, the improper disposal of litter can have a detrimental impact on the natural environment. For instance, Thailand has struggled to control water pollution and protect its natural resources due to the increasing amount of plastic waste left by tourists.

In conclusion, I firmly believe that traveling to foreign countries for pleasure can have detrimental effects on the destinations visited. The surge in tourism can lead to an increase in crime rates and environmental pollution. To mitigate these negative impacts, it is crucial for both the tourists and the local authorities to work together in implementing effective measures to ensure the sustainable and responsible management of travel activities. Only then can we truly enjoy the benefits of traveling while minimizing its negative effects.
