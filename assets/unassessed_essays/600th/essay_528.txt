Revised:

In recent years, there has been a rapid growth in globalization and the number of multinational companies. Some people argue that these developments have negative repercussions for the environment. I entirely agree with this viewpoint due to the increasing amount of gas emissions and the decreasing green spaces, both of which will be discussed in this essay.

Firstly, the expansion of globalization has made it easier for people to travel anywhere and anytime, but it has also had a detrimental impact on the environment due to the subsequent increase in gas emissions. Nowadays, most people prefer to commute by using their private vehicles or airplanes when traveling abroad. However, the use of such modes of transportation results in significant gas emissions, thereby contributing to the deterioration of the environment. For example, executives of large multinational corporations, who have offices in various countries, often commute using airplanes and private cars for business meetings. These modes of transportation emit large quantities of greenhouse gases, which in turn has an adverse effect on the environment.

Moreover, the expansion of multinational companies also poses a threat to the environment, particularly in terms of decreasing green spaces. When multinational companies establish new businesses or factories in different locations, it directly impacts the green ecosystem, leading to a decrease in forest and woodland areas. This disturbs the natural balance of the environment. For instance, Nestle, a multinational company, has numerous branch offices and factories across various countries, occupying substantial amounts of land. Consequently, green spaces are diminishing as this company continues to expand its operations globally.

In conclusion, I believe that globalization and the rising number of multinational companies have a negative impact on the environment due to the increased gas emissions and the reduction of green spaces. It is crucial that measures are taken to mitigate these effects and promote a more sustainable approach to economic development. This could involve promoting alternative modes of transportation or encouraging companies to adopt environmentally friendly practices. Only by doing this can we ensure the preservation of our environment for future generations.
