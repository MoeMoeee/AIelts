Essay:

"Some family heads argue that young children should participate in social settings rather than engage in individual activities. This essay will discuss both views and why I think it is essential to mingle with others.



On the one hand, parents choose to let their children carry out free time activities on their own to develop independence. When children work independently, they acquire skills that will be useful for their future such as being able to solve problems. Furthermore, not having to spend time with a lot of people allows them to avoid disagreements with friends. Children find it easier to play online games than to go out to the park. Therefore, parents desire their children to stay home and do what they love on their own.



On the other hand, research indicates that collaboration between young children enhances social and confidence skills. First, as young youths do things with their mates, they develop good communication skills by initiating and contributing to conversations. Hence, in future when they are young adults, they are prepared to face the challenges that come with working with different people. For example, as they participate in group settings, they become capable communicators and learn from other people's perspectives. Secondly, spending time with peers' supports the development of confidence. This is achieved when youths share ideas and extend on them, as a result, they build on their self-esteem knowing that their thoughts are valued. Therefore,  it is important that children should get out of their comfort zone and meet people to strengthen life's essential skills such as social and confidence skills.



In summary, some people think children should spend time on their own, while others think they should socialise. I think the latter is essential because it helps with skills for social life and confidence."


Revised:

Some people argue that parents should encourage their children to participate in organized group activities during their free time, while others believe that it is important for children to learn how to occupy themselves independently. This essay will discuss both views and present my own opinion.

On the one hand, parents who support independent activities believe that children can develop important skills, such as problem-solving and decision-making, when they engage in activities on their own. This allows them to become more independent and self-reliant, which will benefit them in the long run. Furthermore, by not having to interact with other people in a group, children can avoid conflicts or disagreements that may arise within a group setting. For example, some children may find it easier to play online games or engage in solitary hobbies, such as reading or painting, rather than being involved in social activities outside their home. Therefore, parents encourage their children to pursue their personal interests and hobbies independently.

On the other hand, research has shown that participating in group activities can enhance a child's social and communication skills. When children engage in activities with their peers, they are required to communicate, cooperate, and collaborate, which helps them in developing these essential skills. Working in teams or groups enables children to learn how to effectively communicate their ideas and opinions, listen to others, and find common ground. This prepares them for future challenges that they may encounter when working with diverse groups of people. Additionally, spending time with peers helps build confidence as children learn to share their thoughts and ideas with others, contribute to discussions, and feel valued and supported by their peers. Therefore, it is important for children to step out of their comfort zones and engage in group activities to develop vital life skills, such as social and communication skills.

In conclusion, while some individuals believe that children should spend their free time engaging in independent activities, I firmly believe that it is crucial for children to participate in organized group activities. These activities provide children with opportunities to develop social and communication skills that are essential for their personal and professional lives. By engaging in group activities, children can learn to work in teams, communicate effectively, and build their self-confidence. Therefore, parents should encourage their children to participate in organized group activities in order to foster their social development.
