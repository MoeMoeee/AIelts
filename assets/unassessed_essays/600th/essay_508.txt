Topic:

"Some people think that cultural traditions may be destroyed when they are used as money-making traditions aimed at tourists, others, however, believe that it is the only way to save these traditions. 



Discuss both views and give your opinion"

Essay:

"The tourism industry is an essential part of Vietnam's total GDP. To develop it further, travel agents have utilized all their means to create new experiences. But in the process, there have been some hot debates about whether or not commercializing cultural traditions is a sensible decision. In my opinion, both views have their legitimate reasons.



On the one hand, the continuous expansion of tourism has several consequences. The first negative effect is that heritage sites and monuments are jam-packed with tourists, causing damage to them. For instance, the influx of tourists in the ancient town of Hoi An carved their names on the walls, which vandalized the historical landmark. Moreover, the uncontrolled development of tourism can deteriorate the environment and threaten wildlife. A simple example is Nha Trang beach with garbage floating all over its coast, causing loss of scenery, water pollution, and harm to marine life.



On the other hand, some argue that putting a price on cultural tours is the only method to keep them from fading away. First, revenue from sightseeing, art performances, and other tourist activities is used largely for restoration efforts. Without enough financial support, various attraction sites may fall into disrepair. Another reason is that tourism helps promote cultural exchange. Tourists can visit many historical significance places and talk to local people to gain an in-depth understanding about the area's culture. This encourages the revival of traditions and elevates cultural values.



To sum up, it is beyond doubt that developing tourism brings both positive and negative impacts. Governments and authorities should not lose sight of this fact before deciding on expanding the tourism industry."


Revised:

The tourism industry plays a vital role in contributing to Vietnam's total GDP. In order to further develop this industry, travel agents have resorted to commercializing cultural traditions to create new experiences for tourists. However, this has sparked a heated debate as to whether this commercialization is beneficial or detrimental to these traditions. In my opinion, both perspectives have their valid reasons.

On the one hand, the continuous expansion of tourism has brought about several consequences. One negative effect is the overcrowding of heritage sites and monuments, leading to damage. For instance, the influx of tourists in the ancient town of Hoi An has resulted in some visitors carving their names on the walls, which has vandalized this historical landmark. Moreover, uncontrolled tourism development can have a negative impact on the environment and wildlife. This can be observed at Nha Trang beach, where garbage is scattered all over the coast, leading to the loss of natural beauty, water pollution, and harm to marine life.

On the other hand, proponents argue that commercializing cultural traditions is necessary to ensure their survival. First and foremost, revenue generated from organized cultural tours, art performances, and other tourist activities is often utilized for the restoration and preservation of cultural heritage. Without this financial support, many attraction sites may fall into disrepair. Additionally, tourism provides an opportunity for cultural exchange. Tourists have the chance to visit historically significant places and engage in conversations with local people, thereby gaining a deeper understanding of the local culture. This interaction encourages the revival of traditions and enhances cultural values.

In conclusion, it is undeniable that the development of tourism has both positive and negative impacts. Governments and authorities should carefully assess these impacts before deciding to expand the tourism industry. Measures should be taken to mitigate the negative effects, such as managing visitor numbers and implementing strict regulations to protect cultural heritage and the environment. By doing so, we can strike a balance between preserving cultural traditions and promoting sustainable tourism in the long run.
