Revised:

Giving a speech in front of a large audience is an important skill in today's society. Despite many people feeling nervous or afraid to speak in public, the ability to communicate effectively in public is crucial for both personal development and societal progress. In my opinion, it is essential for children to be taught public speaking in schools. 

To begin with, in a competitive society where good communication is valued more than knowledge alone, the majority of people place great emphasis on their ability to speak well in public. This skill is becoming increasingly important in all professions, and mastering it can provide individuals with a wide range of opportunities in the future. For instance, influential figures like Steve Jobs and Mark Zuckerberg not only excel in public speaking but also possess charisma, which helps them captivate their audience.

Moreover, in certain developed countries, schools have already incorporated public speaking into their curriculum. Studies have shown that students who receive training in public speaking tend to have a significant impact on their careers and are more likely to achieve success. Unfortunately, only a minority of schools worldwide have embraced this approach, and the results have been exceptional. If all nations were to implement public speaking education, it would have a profound effect on the overall quality of life in these regions.

In conclusion, it is crucial for individuals to acquire the skill of public speaking in order to enhance their lives and achieve personal growth. By stepping out of our comfort zones and honing our communication skills, we can look forward to a brighter future. Therefore, children at school should definitely be taught public speaking as part of their education. After all, effective communication is an integral part of life.
