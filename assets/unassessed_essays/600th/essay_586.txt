Topic:

"Some people believe that zoos are inhumane and should be closed down. Others argue that zoos play an important role in wildlife conservation. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Nowadays, we have zoos all around the world to preserve endangered animals and to the purpose of introducing these animals to people. While some people think that zoos are necessary to protect wildlife animals others believe that this lifestyle is not acceptable for animals and should not be allowed. I will discuss both viewpoints and elaborate the reasons why I side with the latter opinion defending that putting animals in a small place for a lifetime is unacceptable. 

On the one hand, zoos might play an important role in protecting endangered species by providing them with essential conditions to survive. Since the biggest issue in the wild is difficulty to find food and water sources, zoos can help the animals by providing these basic needs. Furthermore, these controlled places might give a chance for treatment to animals with serious injuries and help to increase their lifetime. More than that, with controlled breeding next generations could be produced and animal genes can be preserved.

On the other hand, removing animals from their natural habitats can lead to a lot of problems such as an imbalance in nature. For instance, removing one animal from the food chain can affect all other species in the food pyramid. Therefore, as all the natural balance would be affected negatively changing the natural system should not be that easy. Besides all of these, putting an animal in a very small living place can cause a lot of behavioural disorders also. Consequently, these disorders will lead to other mental and physical conditions.

In conclusion, there are some believers that menageries are necessary to keep wild animals alive and protect their generations for long years whereas others argue that zoos are inhumane places for animals as they are way smaller than animals need and change balance of the nature. I believe animal welfare is more important than everything and the protection of endangered species can be done without changing their natural habitats. I opine zoos have gone out of their purpose and have become touristic places only. Therefore, as a veterinarian, I believe as these conditions cause more problems than help, zoos are unacceptable places for animals."


Revised:

Nowadays, zoos are prevalent worldwide, serving as a means of preserving endangered animals and introducing them to the public. While some argue that zoos are necessary for protecting wildlife, others hold the view that this confinement is inhumane and should not be allowed. In this essay, I will discuss both perspectives and explain why I support the latter opinion, considering that confining animals in small spaces for their entire lives is unacceptable.

On one hand, zoos can play a pivotal role in safeguarding endangered species by creating conducive environments for their survival. In the wild, animals face challenges in finding food and water sources, but zoos can address these needs. Additionally, zoos can provide medical treatment and increase the lifespan of animals with severe injuries. Moreover, controlled breeding in zoos can contribute to the production of future generations and the preservation of animal genes.

On the other hand, the removal of animals from their natural habitats can lead to numerous problems, including disruptions in the ecological balance. For instance, eliminating one animal from the food chain can have a negative impact on other species within the food pyramid. Therefore, altering the natural system should not be taken lightly, as it can disrupt the entire ecosystem. Furthermore, confining animals to small living spaces can result in various behavioral disorders, leading to additional mental and physical health conditions.

In conclusion, while there are proponents who argue that zoos are necessary for the survival and protection of endangered species, others contend that zoos are inhumane, as they provide animals with spaces much smaller than what they require and disrupt the balance of nature. I believe that animal welfare should take precedence over other considerations, and the conservation of endangered species can be achieved without altering their natural habitats. As a veterinarian, I consider these conditions in zoos to be more detrimental than helpful, making zoos unacceptable environments for animals.
