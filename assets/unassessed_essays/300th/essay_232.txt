In today's era of developing medicine, an increasing number of individuals are opting for alternative medicines and treatments instead of visiting their regular doctor. This essay explores whether this trend is positive or negative.

The field of medicine is constantly advancing, with doctors and professors constantly striving to find quicker and more effective ways of treating various diseases. Consequently, people are naturally inclined to seek these modern methods to cure their health problems. This is a reasonable approach because there are instances where conventional doctors are unable to provide the necessary help, leaving individuals with no choice but to explore alternative treatments. Take the example of the renowned Medical Center in Uzbekistan, which has gained recognition across Central Asia for treating diseases that regular doctors failed to address. In 2017, doctors at this center successfully aided a boy who was unable to speak through their unique methods. Thus, it is evident that alternative and unconventional treatments can sometimes prove to be highly beneficial in combating illnesses.

Furthermore, there are cases where regular doctors lack sufficient expertise and knowledge about particular health issues. In countries like Kazakhstan, the quality of education, including in medical schools, may be subpar. This fact is widely known among the populace, leading many individuals to prioritize alternative medicines over conventional ones. However, this development can serve as an incentive for regular doctors to improve their competency and gain the trust of the people.

In conclusion, people have the right to decide whether to try alternative treatments or stick to conventional doctors. Personally, I believe that individuals should strive to find the treatment that suits them best, even if it entails exploring unconventional methods. Therefore, I consider the growing preference for alternative medicines and treatments to be a positive development in the field of healthcare.
