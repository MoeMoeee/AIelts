Revised:

Few people think that juveniles should be punished the same as adults for committing serious crimes such as robbery and murder. I disagree with this statement because I believe everyone deserves a second chance. Additionally, if young offenders are placed in the same correctional facilities as adult criminals, they may be negatively influenced and become more prone to engaging in criminal activities.

Firstly, it is important to note that young people often commit crimes due to a desire for recognition or because of their innocence and lack of understanding. Therefore, they should be given the opportunity to rectify their mistakes and get their lives back on track. Being at a young age means they have a whole life ahead of them, and given the chance, they can change their behavior and become productive members of society. For instance, many correctional facilities provide prisoners with the opportunity to learn vocational skills and produce goods that can be sold in the market. This not only helps them acquire useful skills but also helps in their reintegration into society, as people are more likely to accept individuals who are contributing positively.

Secondly, not all young offenders are serious criminals. By treating them in the same manner as adult offenders, they may become isolated from the outside world and more likely to engage in criminal behavior. This would be detrimental to society, as they would be exposed to other criminals in prisons and learn from them. Furthermore, they may form new connections and affiliations with other criminals, which would further corrupt their minds and lead them astray. To illustrate, a recent survey revealed that 70% of young children who were rejected by society and entered the world of crime ended up becoming serious criminals because they associated with negative influences.

In conclusion, while punishment is essential in order to instill fear and discourage individuals from engaging in criminal activities, it is not the ultimate solution. If society truly wishes to bring about positive change, it needs to embrace and help young offenders improve instead of resorting to punishment. Providing support and guidance can go a long way towards rehabilitating them and preventing future crimes.
