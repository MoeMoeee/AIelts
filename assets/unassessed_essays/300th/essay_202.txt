In today's world, one of the most controversial issues is whether the national borders will remain between countries or not in the future. On the one hand, proponents of the view that national borders will cease to exist argue that globalization and technological advancements have made the world a global village. They argue that in the future, people will be able to move freely across borders, and the concept of "nation" as a political and geographic entity will become a thing of the past. They also argue that such freedom of movement between countries will positively impact economies, promote cultural exchange, and increase tolerance.

On the other hand, opponents of the idea argue that the nation-state system is fundamental to maintaining law and order, security, and sovereignty. They argue that without borders, criminals and terrorists would easily cross borders and cause mayhem. Moreover, they believe that different countries possess different laws and moral standards, and the absence of borders would lead to cultural clashes and conflicts.

In my opinion, national borders will continue to exist in the future because they are necessary to maintain security and law and order. Moreover, cultural diversity is one of the most significant strengths of the world, and it will remain so only if countries can preserve their unique identities by maintaining borders.

In conclusion, the debate over the existence of borders between nations is a complex one. While some argue that they will disappear in the future due to globalization and technological advancements, others believe that they will remain necessary for security and sovereignty.
