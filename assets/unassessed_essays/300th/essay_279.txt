Topic:

"In some cultures, children are often told that they can achieve anything if they try hard enough.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of giving children this message?"

Essay:

"For a long time, society views hard work as a prime determination in achieving success.  In this following essay, I will discuss thoroughly the positive and negative impacts and provide some evidence of this behaviour towards children.

First and foremost, promoting hard work can be advantageous for young people in some ways. Maximizing effort is essential to sharpening persistent and resilient skills. With these skills, children are able to adapt to difficult situations and find the solution to solve the issues that may occur. For instance, in order to achieve straight A’s, students must prioritize and be willing to commit to homework, long-hour mentoring sessions, and practice with various kinds of tests with different levels of difficulty. However, hard work must be done with consistency. Hence, by combining these two attitudes, children will perform much better and leading to higher chances of success.

On the other hand, hard work may bring an unpleasant impact on children. A long-haul discipline without any rest may cause burnout and disrupt the mental health condition. For example, if we force children to study without any rest or day off, success is not something that is close to achieving, instead they will get stressed which even leads to depression. To ease some pressures, it is important for children to have some personal traits, such as holidays or going out with friends. Furthermore, aside from taking some time off, setting goals and purposes may also help young people to map out their effort and know what to achieve. 

To conclude, indeed hard work motivates children in achieving their goals and dreams. However, it is also important to note, a hard effort may bring pressure which leads to harmful impacts. Hence, setting clear goals and having some time off may balance the negative impact of hard work."


Revised:

For a long time, society has viewed hard work as a key factor in achieving success. In this essay, I will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of instilling this belief in children and provide evidence to support my arguments.

Promoting the value of hard work can be advantageous for young people in several ways. Firstly, it helps to cultivate persistent and resilient skills. By exerting effort, children develop the ability to adapt to difficult situations and find solutions to any issues that may arise. For example, in order to achieve top grades, students must prioritize their studies and be committed to completing homework, attending long-hour mentoring sessions, and practicing with various types of tests at different levels of difficulty. However, it is crucial for hard work to be consistently maintained. By combining perseverance and consistency, children are more likely to perform better and increase their chances of success.

On the other hand, excessive emphasis on hard work can have negative consequences for children. The continual pressure to continuously work without any rest can lead to burnout and have a detrimental effect on their mental health. For instance, if children are forced to study without any breaks or days off, they are not likely to achieve success; instead, they may experience stress and even depression. To alleviate some of this pressure, it is important for children to have personal time, such as going on holidays or spending time with friends. Additionally, setting clear goals and objectives can help young people map out their efforts and gain a sense of direction.

In conclusion, while hard work can be a source of motivation for children to achieve their goals and dreams, it is also important to recognize that it can bring about harmful impacts. Therefore, it is essential to strike a balance by setting clear goals, allowing for adequate rest, and engaging in activities that promote overall well-being. By doing so, the negative effects of hard work can be mitigated.
