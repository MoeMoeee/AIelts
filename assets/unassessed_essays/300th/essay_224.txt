Topic:

"Nowadays it is more difficult for children to concentrate or pay attention in schools. What could be the reasons for this? What solutions can you suggest?"

Essay:

"In recent times, children have lower attention spans than in the past. This may be due to many reasons but I will be highlighting the main ones in this essay.

With the advance in technology, gadgets such as tablets and computers have become a common item in every household. Therefore, with the development of apps and games, most parents allow access to them as it gives parents a way to keep children busy. Moreover, these games are designed to give users quick bursts of dopamine which cause children, as well as adults, to be occupied for short game sessions. For example, flappy bird was a famous game which relied on users to tap the screen at the correct time which varied constantly but not exceeding more than a second. Consequently, harming their ability to concentrate for prolonged periods. 

There are limited ways to solve this issue. One way would be for parents to set specific tasks to children within a certain time frame. For instance, clean their room in one hour. This would allow them to avoid procrastination and be focused on the task in hand. Admittedly, this would not be able to work every time but eventually they would develop discipline over time and be able to increase their attention span. 

In conclusion, being able to stay focused is a problem for many children in modern society. Schools and parents share this issue but do not appear to fully understand why. With the solutions proposed, there might be hope for future generations to have increased attention spans."


Revised:

In recent times, there has been a growing concern regarding the reduced ability of children to concentrate or pay attention in schools. This essay aims to explore the reasons behind this issue and suggests possible solutions.

One significant cause of this problem is the prevalent use of technology. With the steady advancement in technology, gadgets like tablets and computers have become commonplace in households. Consequently, children are granted access to a plethora of games and applications that are designed to captivate their attention in short bursts. Notably, these games often provide instant gratification in the form of quick dopamine releases, which serve to engage both children and adults for relatively short periods of time. For instance, the universally popular game "Flappy Bird" required users to tap the screen at precise intervals that kept changing, but never exceeded one second. Consequently, this constant exposure to fast-paced and instant entertainment can harm a child's ability to concentrate for extended periods.

There are several strategies that can be implemented to address this problem. One effective approach is for parents to set specific tasks for their children within allocated time frames. For example, assigning the cleaning of their room within an hour can promote focus and discourage procrastination. While it may not succeed on the first attempt, over time this method can help instill discipline in children and enhance their attention span.

In conclusion, it is evident that the diminished attention spans of children pose a significant challenge in modern society. This issue is not only of concern to schools, but also to parents who are confronted with its impact. By implementing the solutions mentioned herein, there is hope for future generations to develop improved attention spans.
