Revised:

There is an ongoing debate concerning whether the improvement of people's lives should be the primary aim of science. I strongly agree that science should prioritize enhancing people's lives. In this essay, I will discuss the reasons to support this perspective.

Firstly, it is crucial to fund scientific research to carry out investigations effectively. These funds are primarily gathered from the public through taxes. Without adequate financial support, scientists would be unable to fulfill their objectives and make significant advancements. Therefore, improving people's lives should be the main goal of science, as it directly impacts the funding it receives.

Another compelling reason to prioritize the improvement of people's lives is that science plays an essential role in our rapidly evolving world. Science spans various fields, such as technology and healthcare, which greatly impact people's daily lives. Technological advancements, including computers, smartphones, and the Internet, provide individuals with numerous benefits and opportunities. Furthermore, scientific research in healthcare enables the development of new medications and treatments, improving the overall well-being of society. For instance, vaccinations have immensely contributed to creating a healthier and safer environment for everyone.

In conclusion, it is undeniable that science ought to focus on improving people's lives. This is crucial because the financial support needed for scientific projects is derived from the population itself. Additionally, science encompasses various fields that enhance people's lives, such as technological advancements and improved healthcare facilities. By prioritizing the improvement of people's lives, science can make a significant positive impact on society as a whole.
