Topic:

"Some people blame the government for the increase in childhood obesity while others think it is the parents’ fault. Discuss both views and give your opinion"

Essay:

"There has been an evident increase in childhood obesity in the past few decades. Some individuals believe this is completely in the governments hands, while others blame the parents. My view is that every parent is fully responsible for their child, whether it be their health, manners, or personality. The following paragraphs will explore both sides of the argument and why I disagree with immediately pointing fingers at the government in this case.

Although it all comes down to the parents of the child at the end, governments possess the authority to get rid of the child obesity issue. A great first step could be reducing the insurmountable prices of healthy foods. Subsequently, parents with less financial stability would be able to afford nutritious food for their children. Likewise, schools encouraging young pupils to adopt a healthy lifestyle would help immensely. The FDA laws that companies abide by should grow stricter, thus forcing companies to sell nothing but nutritious products.

Conversely, I insist that parents have the ultimate influence on their family. After all, an apple never falls far from the tree. Almost everything regarding a child’s mental or physical health is inextricably linked to the birth giver. All children should be encouraged to take up sports, no matter what the sport is. In addition to that, meals prepared for them must include all that is required by their growing bodies, all the while being delicious and appetizing. Fast food will always exist, as it will forever be consumed. Nonetheless, a young one can be taught not to consume fast food regularly due to the harm it causes his/her health.

In conclusion, it is undeniable that the government has a responsibility to create the best possible environment for a family to have good nutrition. This is achievable by many methods, mainly making it possible for all people to afford the ingredients in a balanced diet. However, I believe the parents will always have the greatest authority, as they are closest to their young ones."

Revised Essay:

There has been a significant increase in childhood obesity in recent decades. Some people argue that the government should be held responsible for this issue, while others believe parents are to blame. In my opinion, both the government and parents play crucial roles in addressing childhood obesity. This essay will discuss these perspectives and explain why I believe a combined effort is necessary.

On one hand, the government can contribute to resolving childhood obesity by implementing effective measures. Firstly, they could take steps to reduce the high prices of healthy foods. This would enable parents with limited financial resources to afford nutritious options for their children. Additionally, schools should actively promote a healthy lifestyle by incorporating physical education classes and providing nutritious meals. Moreover, the government can enforce stricter regulations on food manufacturers, thereby ensuring that only nutritious products are available in the market.

On the other hand, parents bear significant responsibility for their children's well-being. The upbringing and habits instilled at home greatly influence a child's mental and physical health. Parents should encourage their children to engage in physical activities and sports regularly, as this helps in maintaining a healthy weight. Furthermore, they should provide balanced and nutritious meals that meet the dietary needs of their children. It is important to teach them about the harmful effects of consuming fast food on their health and encourage moderation in their diet.

In conclusion, while the government has a role to play in creating an environment conducive to healthy lifestyles, parents deserve the most responsibility as they are closest to their children. The government should focus on making nutritious foods affordable and implementing regulations to ensure the availability of healthy options. At the same time, parents must actively engage in promoting healthy habits, such as encouraging physical activity and providing balanced meals. By working together, both the government and parents can effectively address the issue of childhood obesity and ensure a healthier future for our children.
