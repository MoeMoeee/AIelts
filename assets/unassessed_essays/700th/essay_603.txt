Additionally, governments should incentivize the use of affordable public transit options, including buses and metro trains, to discourage the reliance on personal automobiles. Lastly, an effective measure to deter car ownership would be to introduce higher auto ownership taxes, while simultaneously enhancing the accessibility and reliability of public transportation systems.

In conclusion, it is imperative for governments to implement strong measures aimed at curbing the number of cars on the road, thus reducing traffic congestion and environmental pollution. Alongside infrastructure improvements, public education campaigns highlighting the benefits of alternative transportation modes are also crucial. By adopting these strategies, cities can alleviate traffic woes and create more sustainable and livable urban environments.
