Revised Essay:

There are divergent opinions regarding the practice of keeping animals in zoos. While some believe that it is unethical and inhumane to subject animals to captivity, others argue that zoos serve the purpose of both entertainment and ecological significance. In this essay, both perspectives will be discussed in terms of environmental, personal, and economic considerations.

One of the primary advantages of keeping animals in zoos is protecting them from unfavourable weather conditions, predators, and ensuring their access to food. However, it is crucial to ensure that the animals are not confined to cramped spaces, as this could lead to physical and psychological distress, depriving them of the freedom to breathe fresh air and interact with their species. Therefore, the living and housing conditions of animals are crucial and must be given major attention to allow them to experience natural life.

As many animal species face the threat of extinction, zoos play a crucial role in their protection and preservation. Although a substantial amount of funds are spent on sustaining zoos, the benefits outweigh the costs. For example, in China, pandas are a major source of attraction, leading to a significant influx of tourists who come to see these animals in zoos and circuses every year. Nonetheless, some individuals deem government expenditure on animal conservation as futile, preferring that they should focus on improving infrastructures like hospitals, highways, airports, and so on, to enhance a country's development.

Finally, animal conservation also contributes to scientific research. However, this approach has been criticised on moral and religious grounds as many deem it unethical to use animals for entertainment and laboratory experiments. A vast majority believes that animals have a right to dignified and peaceful existence, free from human interference.

To conclude, keeping animals in zoos has numerous advantages, but it should be done with utmost care to respect the animals' rights and well-being. The appropriate living conditions, food, and care must be provided, and resources must be allocated reasonably to ensure the animals' proper conservation without neglecting other aspects of a country's development.
