Revised:

Here's an edited version of the essay with some IELTS structure corrections:

Nowadays, there is an increasing trend of purchasing well-known brands of vehicles, clothes and other goods. This raises the question of why it is happening and how society will be affected. From my perspective, I believe it will have a predominantly negative impact on society.

There are several compelling reasons why more and more people are obsessed with owning famous brands. Firstly, due to the availability of mass media, people might be unconsciously overwhelmed by commercials that display captivating fashion pieces through their smartphones, TVs, or even while walking on the street. Therefore, the repetitive slogans and remarkable logos of certain brands are implanted into their minds. Not only young people but also the elderly are the target of advertising agencies. Another explanation is the desire for social acceptance, which drives people to buy well-reputed and expensive products to show off and post their stunning pictures on social media platforms like Facebook, to attract more attention and admiration. Lastly, the popularity and high cost of famous brands are often associated with high-quality goods, leading consumers to prefer purchasing them.

However, I believe this trend is largely detrimental to a country's tradition and culture. A country's history and customs have a strong connection to the existence of domestically produced goods. If the endless development of world-renowned brands continues, it will expel locally made products from the commodity market, which may eventually lead to their disappearance and an economic crisis in the future. Given the dominance of internationally famous brands, this will create a difference in clothing styles between the rich and poor and potentially lead to background discrimination.

In conclusion, the consumption of famous brands can have a negative influence on domestically made products, society, and the economic rate of a country due to excessive advertising.
