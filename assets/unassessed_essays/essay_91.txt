Revised:

In modern times, almost every industry sector is experiencing a constant increase in production, resulting in a surplus that leads to environmental damage. This essay will discuss the causes of this issue and propose possible solutions.

There are two main reasons why environmental damage is occurring. Firstly, the use of chemical agents, which are major pollutants, is commonplace. For example, the clothing industry, the most polluting sector, uses these agents to produce products such as fur coats and jeans. Consequently, industrial discharges pollute the water and cause environmental damage. Secondly, the market demand for new products leads to increased production. For instance, it is common to purchase a new telephone every two or three years.

To address this issue, there are two potential solutions. Firstly, setting production limits can reduce the use of chemical agents and limit the amount of surplus production. Secondly, buying used items can decrease market demand for new products, thus reducing production. Companies would no longer find it cost-effective to produce on such a large scale. Implementing these solutions can preserve the environment.

In conclusion, the overproduction of industrial sectors is creating significant environmental problems. However, by setting production limits and promoting the use of used items, we can reduce pollution levels and limit production. As global citizens, it is our responsibility to protect the environment.
