Revised:

Multinational organizations provide development aid to many developing countries with the aim of promoting their growth plans. While supplying financial assistance can uplift countries' economic situation and contribute to declining unemployment rates, I believe that practical support and advice on sustainable development may assist them in finding better solutions to their problems, resulting in efficient performance in every sector.

On the one hand, providing development aid to those nations on their way to progress would help them formulate policies that could be beneficial in terms of economic growth and decreasing unemployment rates. To eliminate economic barriers that can hinder these countries' development, they may spend foreign aid on underdeveloped sectors with the aim of generating more income, which supports sustainable development by increasing countries' total earnings. Furthermore, improving the economic situation may create conditions for small entrepreneurs to take loans and establish successful businesses, leading to a decrease in the unemployment rate. As a result, developing countries may spur their economic growth through overseas aid. For example, in Egypt, the majority of small business owners set up a business with the help of international aid. However, I believe that this approach is not efficient since these states become increasingly dependent on international entities and don't work on their development.

On the other hand, advising these countries on how to form policies and the techniques they should follow to implement them efficiently can result in better solutions and efficient performance in different sectors. If transnational organizations explain the method of successful development to these nations, they can learn about their problems and come up with novel ways to address them. Furthermore, understanding the states' problems in depth and taking responsibility for solving them on their own, as well as identifying immediate concerns and dealing with them in various sectors, can lead to development in these sectors. As a result, developing countries can solve their problems by searching for fundamental issues in their economy without being dependent on any organizations' aid. For instance, World Bank advisory services are provided to 50% of less developed countries with the aim of achieving better development. I believe that this approach is better since it promotes independence and growth.

In conclusion, while providing development aid to developing countries by multinational entities can help them use this money for development, providing training on the efficient policies might be a more effective approach to achieving sustainable growth.
