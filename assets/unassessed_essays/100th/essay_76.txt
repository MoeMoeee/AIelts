Topic:

"Some people say that advertising is extremely successful at persuading us to buy things. Other people think that advertising is so common that we no longer pay attention to it.

Discuss both these views and give your own opinion."

Essay:

"Nowadays, advertisement has become one of the most significant parts of every business across the world. Despite the fact that it is an industry which is second to none in terms of importance to every society, people take some different opinions on this issue. Some of them accept it as a great factor in purchasing but others do not have enough attention to it. This essay will elaborate on both positive and negative effects of this object. I profoundly agree with the first group.

To absorb a wide range of costumers we should spend intensive money on advertising. Roughly, in both of domestic and international vocations, publicizing plays a crucial role. For this reason, that is a stimulating factor for everyone to meet their needs. As an example, when the dwellers are enabled to see some real announces on billboards in one city, it would make unconscious impression on their decision to buy their goods. Thus, this would be a lucrative idea in one community. 

An alternative view is that, having sizable majority of publicities not only are not captivating to the people, but also it can be extremely uneventful to them. By increasing the advertisings, some residents find it as a repetitious problem. As a result, the percentage of shopping will decline dramatically. People do not observe the managers of big companies and their products.  A good example here is, one just goes to the center shop without having any knowledge of the brand, discount, and other elements. 

One can conclude, advertisement has both advantages and disadvantages which are common in every trade. However, by having a powerful management, it can be reasonable and attractive for buyer and beneficial for the merchants."


Revised:

Nowadays, advertising has become an integral part of every business worldwide. Although it is widely recognized as a crucial aspect of society, people hold differing opinions on its effectiveness. Some argue that advertising successfully persuades us to purchase goods, while others contend that advertising has become so common that it no longer grabs our attention. This essay will discuss both views and present my own opinion on the matter. I firmly support the notion that advertising is highly influential in driving consumer behavior.

To capture a wide range of customers, businesses need to invest significant amounts of money in advertising. Advertising plays a pivotal role in both domestic and international markets, making it a vital tool for businesses to meet consumer demands. For instance, when people see advertisements displayed on billboards in their city, it subconsciously influences their decision to purchase certain products. Consequently, advertising can be highly profitable for businesses in a community.

On the other hand, some argue that the abundance of advertising not only fails to captivate consumers but also becomes monotonous and uninteresting. With an increasing number of advertisements bombarding people, some individuals view it as repetitive and tiresome. In such cases, the overall percentage of consumer spending may decline as people no longer pay attention to advertisements. This is exemplified by individuals simply going to a store without any knowledge of specific brands, discounts, or other elements.

In conclusion, advertising has both advantages and disadvantages and is prevalent across various industries. However, with effective management, advertising can be appealing to consumers and beneficial to businesses. Despite the saturation of advertisements in society, I assert that advertising remains a powerful tool in shaping consumer behavior and driving sales.
