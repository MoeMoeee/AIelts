Lexical Resource:

Trong bài viết của bạn, sử dụng từ vựng phong phú và hợp ngữ cảnh đáng chú ý. Tuy nhiên, có một số lỗi nhỏ trong việc sử dụng từ và cụm từ không chính xác hoặc không phù hợp với ngữ cảnh. Dưới đây là một số ví dụ và giải thích cụ thể:

1. "Level of stress will increase because of the constant altercation between husband and wife." 
   Instead of "altercation", which means a noisy disagreement or argument, a more suitable word in this context would be "conflict" or "dispute". "Altercation" implies a more aggressive or intense disagreement, whereas "conflict" or "dispute" can refer to any type of disagreement or tension.

2. "For example, partners who are going through the divorce or on the edge of getting divorced will suffer from mental pressure compared to others who have peaceful family life."
   The phrase "on the edge of getting divorced" is not commonly used in English. A more appropriate phrase would be "on the brink of divorce" or "nearing divorce".

3. "Finding an appropriate job and getting help on the family relationship will definitely reduce the mental and physical struggle among the people."
   The phrase "getting help on the family relationship" could be better phrased as "seeking help for family relationships" or "receiving assistance with family relationships". Using "on" in this context may create confusion as to the specific type of help being sought.

4. "In order to relieve from stress, my friend gave up his software development work and switched his career path to teaching."
   The phrase "relieve from stress" should be "relieve stress" or "alleviate stress". The correct preposition to use with "relieve" in this context is "stress".

5. "Although partners can communicate with each other on the dispute, they should not be shy to reach for external help."
   Instead of "on the dispute", it would be more accurate to use "about the dispute" or "regarding the dispute". Using "on" in this context is not idiomatic.
