Revised:

These days, millions of young people are enrolling in universities every year. There is a certain belief that instead of continuing their studies in tertiary education immediately after high school graduation, young people should consider taking a job for a few years. In this essay, we will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this approach.

On one hand, pursuing higher education involves significant financial requirements. Therefore, some young people may choose to work hard for a period of time in order to save enough money to cover their first year of university expenses, such as tuition fees, living costs, and books. By having saved money, they can focus more on their academic studies without the worry of financial constraints. Consequently, they can achieve higher academic results. Furthermore, working during this time can also provide individuals with valuable soft skills, such as time management and communication skills, which can greatly benefit them in their academic journey.

On the other hand, it is often observed that many young people lose the motivation to continue their studies after working for some time. The desire to learn tends to decline slightly after working. A recent survey revealed that one in three people in their twenties never return to higher education after taking a gap year. This decision puts these individuals at a higher risk of unemployment in the future. Even for those who decide to pursue further studies after a couple of years of working, their ability to learn and grasp new concepts may become slower. Consequently, this can have a negative impact on their overall academic achievement.

To sum up, there are advantages to young people taking a job for a few years after high school graduation. Working during this time can allow them to save money and acquire valuable skills. However, there are also disadvantages. Some individuals may lose their motivation to continue higher education, and their ability to learn may be affected. Therefore, it is important for young people to carefully consider their options and weigh the advantages and disadvantages before making a decision on whether to take a gap year.
