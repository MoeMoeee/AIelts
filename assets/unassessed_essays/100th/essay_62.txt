On the other hand, as individuals reach their forties or fifties, it may not be as wise to take risks in both their professional and personal lives. The ability to recover from failure is more easily achieved in younger years. Furthermore, in personal lives, establishing new relationships becomes more difficult as individuals in this age group usually have stable personal lives and are focused on their families. Taking risks at a later stage may, therefore, be too late and less rewarding.

In conclusion, taking risks is crucial for individuals as they navigate their professional and personal lives. Although there are potential disadvantages, such as the difficulty of taking risks in later stages of life, the advantages outweigh the drawbacks. It is important to seize opportunities and take calculated risks, particularly during younger years when one is more adaptable and can easily recover from failure.
