"In the future, people may no longer be able to pay for things in shops using cash. All the payments may have to be made by card or using phones.
Do you think this will happen one day?
Why do you think some people might not be happy to give up using cash?"

Revised Essay:

In the future, it is possible that cash payments in shops may no longer be accepted and all transactions will have to be made through cards or mobile phones. While this is a likely scenario due to advancing technology and the increasing preference for cashless transactions, it is important to consider the reasons why some individuals may not be pleased with giving up the use of cash.

Advancements in technology have drastically changed the way payments are made. Many people now prefer the convenience of using credit or debit cards or making transactions through digital wallets such as UPI. This trend has been further accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic, as people are increasingly avoiding the use of physical cash for hygiene reasons. For instance, even street vendors are adapting to these changes by accepting other modes of payment. However, it is unlikely that everyone will embrace online payments in the future and a significant section of the population will still prefer to use cash.

One reason why some individuals may be resistant to abandoning cash is the global issue of extreme poverty. According to a survey conducted by the Times in 2021, 16% of the world's population lives in poverty. For these individuals, basic necessities such as shelter, food, and medical access are a challenge, let alone having the means to open a bank account for online transactions. Cash remains their only option for survival. Additionally, there are many people who prefer using physical currency as it helps them in practicing mindful spending and prevents impulsive purchases.

On the other hand, it is plausible that cash could eventually be eliminated altogether and replaced with digital currencies such as cryptocurrencies. This would also have positive environmental implications, as it would reduce the need for producing paper money and the subsequent deforestation for paper production.

Nevertheless, there will always be a segment of the population that will not be comfortable with online transactions. This is especially true in areas with slow or no internet connectivity. In such cases, the availability of cash is crucial for everyday transactions.

In conclusion, while the use of cash may gradually diminish in favor of digital transactions, it is important to consider the limitations and drawbacks of completely abandoning physical currency. The issue of extreme poverty and the need for mindful spending are some factors that make cash indispensable to certain individuals. Therefore, both cash and digital payment methods such as UPI should coexist to ensure that everyone has the means to make transactions conveniently and securely.
