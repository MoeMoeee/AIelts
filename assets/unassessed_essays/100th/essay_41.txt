Revised:

It is often debated whether significant changes in society can only be brought about by the government or if individuals also hold a considerable influence. While some argue that only the government has the authority and resources to make substantial changes, I believe that individuals can have a significant impact on society. In this essay, I will discuss both viewpoints and explain why I lean towards the belief that individuals can make a difference.

Many argue that because the government possesses the authority and decision-making power, it is the primary entity responsible for effecting societal changes. The government has the ability to enact laws that protect the environment, regulate trade and finance, and provide support to both individuals and businesses. In an ideally functioning system, the government's aim is to ensure the welfare and prosperity of the citizens who elect them, making them better suited to navigate the complexities of managing a country.

Moreover, when individuals have grievances or specific needs, they must seek the government's permission or assistance. For example, if citizens see a need for a new kindergarten in their community, they would have to approach the government for funding and support. While this arrangement has its downsides, such as the possibility of having requests denied, civil organizations may step in to provide help. However, these organizations often lack the necessary power and financial resources to create widespread societal changes.

On the other hand, individuals possess the power to initiate change at a grassroots level. History has shown the ability of individuals to lead social movements and shape society. From Mahatma Gandhi in India to Rosa Parks in the United States, individuals have not only influenced policy changes but have also ignited movements that have addressed deep-rooted social issues. Moreover, individuals' actions often inspire others to join their cause, amplifying the impact of their efforts. In a democratic society, individuals have the freedom to express their opinions, participate in activism, and rally support for their ideas.

In conclusion, while the government plays a significant role in effecting societal changes, I strongly believe that individuals also have the capacity to influence society. While the government possesses the authority and resources to drive large-scale transformations, individuals wield the power to initiate change and inspire others to join their cause. Recognizing this potential, individuals must actively engage in the democratic process and express their opinions and concerns. By doing so, they can contribute to creating a society that reflects their values and aspirations.
