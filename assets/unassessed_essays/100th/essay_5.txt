Topic:

"SOME PEOPLE THINK THAT ALL UNIVERSITY STUDENTS SHOULD STUDY WHATEVER THEY LIKE. OTHERS BELIEVE THAT THEY SHOULD ONLY BE ALLOWED TO STUDY SUBJECTS THAT WILL BE  USEFUL IN THE FUTURE, SUCH AS THOSE RELATED TO SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY.
DISCUSS BOTH THESE VIEWS AND GIVE YOUR OPINION."

Essay:

"Some humans believe that students in tertiary institutions should have the right to study the courses they like. However, other people believe that they should offer courses that will be beneficial later in life
In this essay, I will analyze the points and present my view in favour of people who believe that students should be given the right to study the courses they like.
On one side, having a chance to study science and technology-related courses can bring many benefits. First of all, the world is evolving every day with the use of technology especially the health line. Most devices used in the hospital are implemented by technology talking about the intensive care unit. This helps in making work easy and also reduces the rate at which patients die. However, I still believe that students should be given the chance to study courses they like because not everyone has the ability to cope with science and technology-related courses and also studying their dream courses will benefit them and also the community at large just like those who studied science and technology related courses.
On the other side, Students can be better prepared for the career they like because more time will be spent on studying what they are interested in and also they will learn with more enthusiasm because they will learn what they like.
Personally, I think that some courses studied in  tertiary institutions aside from the science and technology-related courses are also well recognized because of their impact laid in  society. Talking about the lawyers and judges who give justice to situations. This has helped  society at large by making it a favourable atmosphere for us all to live in.When students are given the opportunity to study the courses they like it gives them the opportunity to extend their range of interest and also helps them develop important characteristics such as self-confidence, and high self-esteem because they were not forced to study courses they did not like."


Revised:

"Some people believe that university students should have the freedom to study the courses they enjoy. Others, however, argue that students should only be allowed to study subjects that have practical applications in the future, such as those related to science and technology. This essay will discuss both perspectives and offer my own opinion.

On one hand, there are several advantages to studying science and technology-related subjects. In today's rapidly evolving world, technological advancements play a crucial role, especially in fields like healthcare. For instance, hospitals rely heavily on technology, particularly in the intensive care unit, to improve patient outcomes and reduce mortality rates. Therefore, acquiring knowledge in these areas can greatly benefit individuals and society. However, it is important to note that not everyone possesses the aptitude for science and technology-related subjects. Hence, it is equally important to allow students the freedom to pursue their passion and study courses of their choosing.

On the other hand, allowing students to study their preferred courses can better equip them for their future careers. By focusing on subjects they are genuinely interested in, students are more likely to invest more time and effort into their studies, leading to a higher level of enthusiasm and motivation. This, in turn, can contribute to their success in their chosen professions. However, it is important to strike a balance between personal interests and practicality, as some courses outside of science and technology also have a significant impact on society. For example, the legal profession plays a crucial role in ensuring justice and maintaining a harmonious society. Therefore, students should be given the opportunity to explore a wide range of subjects that align with their interests and career aspirations.

In my opinion, while science and technology-related courses are undoubtedly essential for future development, it is equally important to respect the individual preferences and strengths of each student. By allowing students to study their chosen courses, they can develop a sense of self-confidence and self-esteem, which are crucial for their personal growth and happiness. Moreover, diverse fields of study contribute to the overall progress and well-being of society, as they provide a range of expertise and perspectives.

In conclusion, while some argue that university students should focus solely on science and technology-related subjects, I believe that they should have the freedom to study the courses they are passionate about. A balance must be struck between practicality and personal interests, as this will not only benefit the individual students but also contribute to the overall growth and development of society."
