Topic:

"In the future, nobody will buy printed newspapers or books because they will be able to read everything they want online without paying. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"People argue that the advantages of digital books or newspapers will lead to the disappearance of the traditional ones. I strongly agree with this opinion.

The primary reason why nobody will purchase the printed books and newspapers is due to their negative effects on environment. Conventional books and newspapers are made from wood, and hence a plethora of trees has to be cut to provide materials for paper manufacturing process. This could exacerbate the deforestation which is a headache issue of all countries around the globe. To address this critical problem, many goverments have imposed higher taxes on wood-made products, and books or newspapers are one of the bypass products of woods. As a result, the price of books and newspapers shoots up and forces people think about another economic way to replace printed books and newspapers.

On the other hands, there is a variety of advantages of electronic books and newspapers. Firstly, they do not need to be printed out on the papers so that they are very friendly to environment, and the manufacturing and printing of books and newspapers are significantly reduced. Therefore, their price is much cheaper than the printed books and newspapers, and people can afford to buy them. Secondly, people can easily update news or information anytime and anywhere as long as they have a smartphone connecting to wifi. This is an extraordinary benefit for busy people who has a limit of time per day. Finaly, the high quality and vivid image helps the digital books and newspapers outweigh the conventional ones.

In my conclusion, the purchase of printed newspapers or books will be decreasingly dropped and people will prefer to buy digital ones due to their obvious advantages."


Revised:

People nowadays debate whether electronic books and newspapers will ultimately replace printed ones due to their apparent advantages. This essay agrees with the notion that in the future, the majority of individuals will likely opt to read information online rather than pay for physical publications.

The foremost reason why conventional newspapers and books may become obsolete is their negative impact on the environment. Traditional books and newspapers rely on the use of wood, making deforestation an inevitable repercussion of their production. Governments combat this matter by implementing higher taxes on these types of products. The consequence of these actions is that their prices increase, leading customers to prioritize cheaper and hence more environmentally friendly alternatives.

Moreover, electronic books and newspapers offer several favorable attributes. The absence of physical copies makes digital books less hazardous to the environment as they do not require physical materials to manufacture. As a result, their prices are often more affordable than printed publications. Additionally, these items can be updated at any given time, allowing individuals to remain updated on the news regardless of their location, which is not the case with printed copies. Lastly, electronic copies offer high-quality images and videos that print publications cannot match.

To sum up, while printed newspapers and books have seemingly served society for an extended period, it is likely that they will eventually be replaced by digital versions. Prevailing evidence suggests that ebooks and online news outlets offer the same, if not more, advantages than their print counterparts such as environmental friendliness, affordability, and updated information.
