Nowadays, learning foreign languages has become more common. While some people think the only reason why people should learn other languages is for work or travel, I argue that there are more reasons contributing to this case and I personally think they are right.

On the one hand, the primary reason for learning a language is the ability to communicate with others. This is why some people believe that the only reason to learn foreign languages is for practical purposes such as work or travel. In this scenario, learning the basic essentials of the language is sufficient for communication and there is no need to delve into the advanced details. Proponents of this view argue that practical use of the language is of utmost importance. For example, someone who receives a job offer from another country would have a strong incentive to learn the language spoken in that country.

On the other hand, there is more to a language than mere communication, and hence, people believe there are numerous other reasons for learning foreign languages. Learning a foreign language allows individuals to read books from other countries and become more familiar with their respective literature. Additionally, it enables people to watch foreign movies in their original form and gain an in-depth understanding of the culture and lifestyles of the people in those countries. Since translation does not always effectively convey the nuances and intricacies of these cultural aspects, many individuals study foreign languages for these reasons.

In conclusion, while it is true that many people learn foreign languages primarily for work or travel purposes, there are several other factors that contribute to the importance of learning a foreign language. From my perspective, learning a foreign language has numerous advantages beyond just practical use and can greatly enhance one's understanding of different cultures and their associated literature and arts.
