Topic:

"The growth of online shopping will one day lead to all shops in towns and cities closing. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Humanity has always been experimenting with different technologies to make sales perform better. In recent years, online shopping websites and apps have become quite popular, which makes some individuals think that they can close down offline shops. In my opinion this claim is overstated and it is not going to happen in the near future. However, online services may be more preferable for certain people.

On the one hand, offline shops afford people the opportunity to make more deliberate and weighed decisions on items to buy. This is especially the case for clothes, as it is much more convenient to try on them immediately in a fitting room. Another significant benefit of offline shops is that a person can ask for a consultation from a seller. This is crucial for older individuals, who need extra guidance and assistance during their shopping. For instance, my grandparents are not advanced users of the internet, therefore they always choose to see products they want to buy with their own eyes.

On the other hand, online services have developed substantially over the recent years. They are constantly looking for new opportunities to make their apps and websites convenient to as many users as possible. The reason for that is the advanced improvement of internet technologies, which allows them to experiment with different styles of user interface to find the ones that perform better for everyone.

In conclusion, the majority of individuals use online services because of their convenience. However, there will always be people who find them difficult to use or simply enjoy the offline shopping experience. This is the reason why offline shops are going to coexist with online apps and websites making shopping available for all the people."


Revised:

Humanity has always been experimenting with different technologies to enhance sales performance. In recent years, online shopping websites and apps have gained popularity, leading some individuals to believe that offline shops will eventually close down. While I agree that online services may be more preferable for certain people, I believe that the claim of all shops in towns and cities closing due to the growth of online shopping is overstated and unlikely to happen in the near future.

On one hand, offline shops offer several advantages that online shopping cannot replicate. Offline shops provide a unique experience where customers have the opportunity to make more deliberate and well-informed decisions on items to buy. This is particularly evident in the context of purchasing clothes, as trying them on immediately in a fitting room allows for better evaluation of size, fit, and overall satisfaction. Additionally, offline shops provide a personal touch with the presence of sales staff who can offer consultations and guidance. This is particularly beneficial for older individuals who may require extra assistance and support during their shopping process. As an example, my grandparents, who are not proficient internet users, always prefer to physically see the products they intend to purchase.

On the other hand, online shopping services have undergone significant development in recent years. They continuously strive to improve their apps and websites to cater to the convenience of as many users as possible. This is made possible by advancements in internet technologies, which enable constant experimenting with different user interfaces to enhance user experience.

In conclusion, while the majority of individuals opt for online shopping due to its convenience, there will always be a significant portion of the population who either find it challenging to use or simply prefer the tactile experience of offline shopping. Consequently, offline shops will continue to coexist with online apps and websites, ensuring shopping options are available to cater to the diverse preferences of all individuals.
