Topic:

"These days, people work in more than one job, and often change career several times during their life. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this?"

Essay:

"People often tended to have a fixed job that could last for their lifetime in the past, but it is changed now. For many individuals, job change has been their purpose in life nowadays. In this essay, I will outline what I consider to be some positive and negative parts of this trend.

Certainly, there are several key benefits stemming from changing jobs frequently. One of them is to gain more experience in a different environment. In one company, for example, the longer an employee works, the more routined tasks are. Thus, moving to a new organisation can allow the worker to learn a different thing. Another key benefit is that people may get higher pay in a new job. It is reported that the pay may rise up to 30% for those who choose to move on with a new entity, while, in contrast, it is only 10% for a regular annual salary increment. Clearly, changing jobs is very attractive. 

However, it does have its limitations. Job-hopping can be difficult because people need to start afresh. They need to go through a probation period many times, learning the basics and gradually involving in an important project if a manager believes in their abilities and values. I myself, for instance, took 2 years to understand and master a new career but finally quit to return to the previous company due to stress. In addition to this difficulty, the person may end up unemployed if they fail to pass the probation or if they cannot get along with their new colleagues. If they cannot find a new job to continue, it ultimately creates a financial issue as well. 

In conclusion, having a job changed is a choice of each person for their future career and better life. If anyone decides to move on with a new career, it is better for them to take some time to consider all potential problems and be well-prepared to overcome them."


Revised:

In the past, people tended to have a fixed job that could last a lifetime, but nowadays, this trend has changed. Job change has become a common occurrence in many individuals' lives. In this essay, I will outline the advantages and disadvantages of this phenomenon.

Certainly, there are several key benefits to changing jobs frequently. Firstly, it allows individuals to gain experience in different work environments. As employees work for longer periods in one company, they often become accustomed to routine tasks. Moving to a new organization provides an opportunity to learn new skills and experience different ways of working. Secondly, changing jobs can lead to higher pay. Research has shown that individuals who switch to a new job can expect a salary increase of up to 30%, compared to a regular annual salary increment of only 10%. Clearly, changing jobs can be very attractive in terms of career growth and financial rewards.

However, this trend also has its limitations. Job hopping can be challenging as individuals need to start afresh. They often need to go through a probation period, where they have to learn new tasks and gradually become involved in important projects. This period can be time-consuming and may cause stress. For example, in my own experience, it took me two years to understand and master a new career, only to eventually quit and return to my previous company due to the stress involved. Moreover, individuals who change careers frequently may struggle to establish good working relationships with new colleagues, or may fail to pass the probationary period altogether. If they cannot find a new job to continue, it ultimately creates financial difficulties.

In conclusion, changing careers is a choice individuals make to improve their future prospects and quality of life. However, it is important for anyone considering a career change to take some time to consider potential challenges and be well-prepared to overcome them. Moving to a new job can offer new experiences, higher pay, and personal growth opportunities, but it can also be demanding and potentially create financial instability.
