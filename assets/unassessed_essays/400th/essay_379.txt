Revised:

Today, an increasing number of individuals with health problems are turning to alternative medicines and treatments instead of consulting with their regular doctors. In my opinion, this trend can have detrimental effects on people's health as untrained individuals who administer alternative medicine may lack the necessary knowledge in medical subjects. Therefore, the growing popularity of alternative medicine can have negative consequences on patients' wellbeing.

To begin with, advancements in modern medicine have been made worldwide, with medical professionals dedicating their lives to finding effective treatments for highly fatal diseases such as cancer. These professionals undergo extensive training and possess a multitude of skills acquired through years of education at medical schools. In contrast, those who practice alternative medicine, including herbal medicine and acupuncture, often lack formal certification. Consequently, individuals who place their trust in unqualified practitioners put themselves at risk.

Furthermore, there is a lack of scientific evidence to support the effectiveness of alternative medicine. In fact, certain herbal remedies, when combined, can be harmful to the human body, leading to irreversible damage. Moreover, due to the absence of thorough scientific research, doctors may be unable to rectify any adverse effects caused by alternative treatments.

In conclusion, alternative therapies pose a significant disadvantage as they involve the use of potentially dangerous remedies without the supervision of qualified medical professionals. Regrettably, governments have not taken adequate measures to regulate these alternative practices in order to safeguard their citizens' well-being. It is my hope that governments will take proactive steps to discourage the use of alternative medicine in the future.
