Revised:

More and more people believe that children should study all subjects at school, while others argue that they should only focus on subjects they are good at or find interesting. In my opinion, young students should be exposed to all subjects initially in order to explore their interests and abilities before making a decision.

On one hand, those who advocate for children studying only subjects they excel in argue that this approach allows students to focus on their strengths and boost their self-confidence. They argue that forcing children to study subjects they dislike or struggle with can lead to frustration and demotivation. Moreover, they believe that students will perform better in areas that they enjoy, leading to better academic achievements. However, this method may not be as effective as it seems. Young students may not have a clear understanding of what they are actually good at or what they may be interested in pursuing in the future. For example, a study conducted by New York University found that 90% of students at a young age choose the wrong subject when given the option. Therefore, exposing children to all subjects is crucial in order for them to make informed decisions.

On the other hand, some argue that children should study all subjects as it is important for them to have a well-rounded education. This approach allows students to gain knowledge and understanding in various fields, making them more informed and adaptable in the future. For instance, in China, children are required to learn the English language from a young age, resulting in approximately 90% of Chinese people being able to speak English fluently. This provides them with a competitive advantage in the job market as many occupations require English proficiency. Therefore, studying all subjects equips children with a wide range of skills and knowledge that can benefit them in their future endeavors.

In conclusion, while there are arguments for both sides, I believe that it is necessary for young students to be exposed to all subjects initially. This allows them to explore their interests and abilities before making a decision. While studying subjects of interest may offer immediate benefits, studying all subjects provides a well-rounded education and a broader foundation for future success. It is important to strike a balance between allowing children to pursue their passions and providing them with a comprehensive education.
