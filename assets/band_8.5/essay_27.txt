Revised:
A highly contentious issue in today's society is whether the responsibility for reducing excessive packaging of products in supermarkets lies with manufacturers or consumers. This essay will discuss both viewpoints and argue that while customers can contribute, it is primarily the companies' responsibility to take more significant steps in curbing excessive packaging.

On one hand, manufacturers often package their goods to attract consumers and promote their products. However, the materials used in packaging, such as non-eco-friendly plastics, have contributed to the depletion of the ozone layer in recent years. Therefore, industries as a whole stand to benefit greatly from seeking environmentally friendly alternatives for their packaging.

Conversely, consumers also have a role to play in protecting the environment by avoiding the purchase of products with excessive packaging. By doing so, the demand for such items will decrease, leading to a direct reduction in manufacturing and sales. For instance, in Nigeria, a significant decrease in the purchase of meat packaged in plastic led a meat vendor to introduce jute bags for their meat sales. Additionally, customers can opt for reusable shopping bags instead of nylon-packed groceries, which are less harmful to the environment.

In conclusion, while individuals can contribute to reducing excessive packaging, I firmly believe that companies and retailers bear the primary responsibility for ensuring that their products are not detrimental to the ecosystem. It is crucial for manufacturers to prioritize the development and utilization of environmentally friendly packaging materials to mitigate the harmful impact of excessive packaging.
