Topic:
"Some people believe that allowing children to male their own choices on everyday matters(such as food, clothes and entertainment) is likely to result in a society of individuals who only think about their own wishes. Other people believe that it is important for children to make decisions about matters that affect them. Discuss both views and give your own opinion"

Essay:
"Many people think that parents should allow their children make daily decisions, but others are concerned that doing so can raise self-serving individuals. Personally, I believe that we should let them make the decision.

On the one hand, children grew by extremely strict parents tend to be social introverts. They had to obey all of their parents' rules as children, regardless of what they wanted to do. For instance, they might be particularly skilled in gaming or working with technology. However, their parents want them to become physicians or engineers. Since they are unable to pursue their own interests, they develop a lack of ambition. Therefore, it is crucial to allow kids make decisions or think about what they want to do from an early age.

On the other side, individuals who grew up with their parents in relatively open relationships frequently lack empathy for others. This occurs because they always received from their guardian what they want and did not have to consider if it was right or wrong. It is their parents' fault, not their own. Elders should teach kids whether or not they are being sensible so that they understand that things don't always go the way they do in movies. Because of this, it is also crucial to manage some of their early emotions.

My parents educated me and instilled in me the value of considering all possibilities and their effects before acting. Today, I developed into a person who can express my needs to others while also knowing what I really desire. Therefore, I think it is the parents' primary responsibility to always make sure their children are on the right track."

Revised:
Many people hold the belief that parents should grant their children the freedom to make decisions in their daily lives, while others express concerns about the potential development of self-centered individuals as a result. In my opinion, I believe it is important to allow children to make decisions that affect them.

On one hand, children who grow up under the strict supervision of their parents often exhibit introverted social behaviors. These children are compelled to adhere to their parents' rules without regard for their personal preferences. For example, they may possess exceptional skills in gaming or technology, but their parents may pressure them to pursue careers in medicine or engineering. As a consequence of being unable to pursue their own interests, these children develop a lack of ambition. Therefore, it is crucial to empower children to make decisions and explore their interests from an early age.

On the other hand, individuals raised in families with more permissive relationships frequently lack empathy for others. This can be attributed to always having their desires fulfilled by their parents without considering whether it is right or wrong. However, this issue lies with the parents rather than the children themselves. Parents should teach their children to discern between sensible and impractical choices, helping them understand that real life does not always mirror the idealized scenarios depicted in movies. Consequently, it is essential for parents to guide their children's emotional development.

Drawing from personal experience, my parents educated me and instilled the value of carefully considering the consequences of my actions before making decisions. As a result, I have become an individual capable of expressing my needs while also understanding my true desires. Therefore, I believe that parents bear the primary responsibility of ensuring their children are on the right path.

Overall, while some argue that allowing children to make their own decisions may lead to a society of self-centered individuals, I believe it is crucial to strike a balance. Children should be given the autonomy to make decisions about matters that directly affect them, enabling them to explore their interests and passions. Simultaneously, parents should play an active role in teaching their children empathy, discernment, and the ability to consider the consequences of their choices. It is through this holistic approach that we can raise well-rounded individuals capable of making thoughtful decisions.
