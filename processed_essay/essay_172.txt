Introduction

It is commonly argued that governments should allocate more budget for developing railways compared to roads. In this essay, I will discuss why I agree with this suggestion.

Body Paragraph 1

Travelling by train can be safer, quicker, cheaper and more environmentally friendly than travelling by road. For example, in India, where the population is enormous and traffic congestion is a major issue, the railway transport system is frequently utilized by daily commuters. If the same number of people travelled by cars, the government would need wider roads, making it more difficult and expensive to develop an efficient road network.

Body Paragraph 2

In developing nations, railway systems are often government-operated. As a result, investing more in this sector can yield significant financial benefits and provide essentials to a broader populace. Furthermore, the cost of developing rail links, particularly those required for bullet trains, is usually high. Ignoring these investments may be detrimental to creating an efficient transportation system.

Conclusion

In conclusion, I believe that investing more in the railway system does not necessarily imply that roads are being neglected. However, considering the benefits I have mentioned, governments should allocate a larger sum of funds to railway systems over road networks.
