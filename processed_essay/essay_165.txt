This essay will outline the reasons for this problem and propose possible solutions.

Revised:

Here's an edited version of your essay with an improved IELTS structure: 

In the modern world, overpopulation in major urban centers has become a pressing issue. Failing to address this problem may lead to negative consequences, such as a shortage of provisions and accommodations. In this essay, we will examine the causes of this issue and suggest possible solutions.

One significant reason for overpopulation in cities is the abundance of opportunities available. Large corporations and businesses are often located in major cities, providing people with well-paid jobs. Additionally, cities offer a wide range of leisure activities, including museums, cinemas, parks, and shopping centers. As a result, many people prefer to move to these urban centers, leading to overcrowding. Furthermore, poverty can contribute to overpopulation as some families do not have the means to control their birth rate, resulting in significant population growth.

To address this issue, governments should step in and provide support. Firstly, they could introduce policies and regulations that limit the maximum number of children per family. This will create a balance between population growth and decline, ensuring the number of people stays within manageable limits. Additionally, the government can provide free or low-cost access to contraception, especially among individuals who cannot afford it. This approach will enable individuals to take control of their reproductive health, reducing unwanted pregnancies and limiting unplanned children.

In conclusion, the problem of overpopulation in major urban centers is caused by city opportunities and poverty. To tackle this problem, governments should introduce policies and regulations that limit the number of children per family and provide affordable contraception. These measures will promote population growth control, leading to better management of resources and amenities in major cities.
