"Famous sportsmen often earn a lot of more money than people in other professions. Although sports is important in our lives, everyone should be equal and such big incomes of sport professionals are unjustified. Do you agree or disagree?"

"Earning a huge amount of wage by eminent sports personalities, compared to other professional workers, is inappropriate because it is right of every skilled person to get capital equally despite knowing the fact that sport is insurmountable in people's lives. I am in consummate accordance with this notion as well.


There are a number of reasons to support my view and predominant one is more important to the work of other professions than sports personnel. There is no doubt that other proficient individuals not only serve the citizens of a nation, but also help to boost the economy of the country in the form of their work. But I believe that other adepts like soldiers, should get more wages as they safeguard their nation twenty-four seven and sometimes, in unfortunate conditions, sacrifice their lives for saving their citizens. Therefore, giving more salary to popular players than other industrious humans is unjustified.

Here is an edited version of the essay with IELTS structure:

Introduction: 

Famous sports personalities often earn substantially higher salaries compared to professionals in other fields. While sports have undeniable benefits in people’s lives, some argue that such high incomes are unjustified. In my opinion, I strongly agree that income distribution should be equal regardless of an individual’s profession.

Body Paragraph 1: 

Firstly, other skilled professionals’ work is more essential to society than that of sports personalities. While athletes may boost national pride and provide entertainment, other professionals such as doctors, scientists and engineers contribute to the betterment of society in a more tangible and crucial manner. They improve healthcare, develop new technologies and undertake research that leads to advancements.

Body Paragraph 2: 

Secondly, although some argue that famous sports personalities deserve high pay due to the risks they face on the job, I believe that those in other fields, such as soldiers and firefighters, face much higher risks and should be compensated accordingly. Their work is much more critical to society and they make significant sacrifices to keep people safe.

Counter-argument: 

It can be argued that sports personalities are just as deserving of high salaries as other professionals, as they provide entertainment and inspire people. However, I would contend that these benefits do not outweigh the contributions of other professions that play a more essential role in society.

Conclusion: 

While famous sports personalities undoubtedly face challenges and work hard, this does not justify the significantly higher salaries they receive compared to other professionals. In the interest of fairness and equality, salaries should be distributed more evenly among all professions based on the value of their contributions to society.
