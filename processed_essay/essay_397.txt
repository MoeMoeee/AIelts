Topic:

"Some people think that the increasing use of computers and mobile phones for communication has a negative effect on young people’s reading and writing skills. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Electronic devices, such as computers and mobile phones, have become an integral part of communication today. However, pessimists argue that the increasing use of them has a detrimental impact on the language abilities of youngsters. Generally, I have the same concern.

Many young people constantly use acronyms when sending messages on their computers or mobile phones for daily information exchange, and there are software and applications to finish their spelling or correct misspelt words or misused grammar usage automatically. As a result, many of them gradually lose the ability to spell the most commonly used words, and the assisted way of writing leads to the inaccurate use of grammar when they have to compose an essay on their own. Therefore, many of them would suffer from the challenges of the lack of writing competence.

Additionally, indulging in computer and mobile phone chatting is time-consuming, which results in less time for reading. What is worse, daily communication on electronic gadgets is likely to exacerbate the development of short attention spans and gradually lead to a loss of interest in reading. Nowadays, it is not difficult to notice that there are not many youngsters who read for pleasure or have the habit of reading. Thus, their reading interest and competence will be degraded in the long term.

Undoubtedly, there are websites and mobile applications that can improve the literacy skills of the young in a convenient way. For example, dictionary applications are particularly helpful to enlarging lexical resources; e-books can be appealing to young minds who are not attracted to print media. But unfortunately, the young generation now is more inclined to chat with others to expand their social circle than to develop linguistic ability.

Overall, I believe that too much access to computers and mobile phones for the young generation to communicate with each other can indeed exert adverse effects on their capabilities in reading and writing."

Title: The Impact of Electronic Communication on Young People's Reading and Writing Skills

Introduction:
In today's digitally-driven world, electronic devices such as computers and mobile phones have become indispensable tools for communication. However, concerns have been raised regarding the negative influence of their widespread use on the language abilities of young people. This essay will evaluate to what extent the increasing reliance on these devices hampers the reading and writing skills of youngsters.

Body Paragraph 1:
Firstly, the regular use of acronyms and shortcuts during text messaging or instant messaging has become a common practice among young people. This reliance on abbreviation and autocorrect software can result in a gradual decline in their ability to spell commonly used words correctly. Moreover, when faced with the task of writing an essay, these individuals may struggle to formulate coherent sentences and structures due to their reliance on automated aids. Therefore, their writing skills may suffer due to this over-reliance on technology.

Body Paragraph 2:
Furthermore, the extensive use of computers and mobile phones for communication purposes can be time-consuming. As a consequence, less time is dedicated to reading. Moreover, constant engagement with electronic gadgets is associated with shorter attention spans, which can lead to a diminished interest in reading. Nowadays, it is evident that few youngsters engage in leisure reading or develop a reading habit. Consequently, their reading proficiency and enthusiasm are likely to deteriorate over time.

Body Paragraph 3:
While it is true that certain technological resources, such as dictionary applications and e-books, can enhance the literacy skills of young individuals in a convenient manner, unfortunately, the current younger generation is more inclined to use these devices for socializing rather than improving their language proficiency. They prioritize expanding their social circles over developing a firm foundation in linguistic abilities.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, the excessive reliance on computers and mobile phones for communication among young people can have detrimental effects on their reading and writing skills. The regular use of abbreviations and autocorrect software may lead to a decline in spelling abilities and an inaccurate use of grammar. Additionally, the time spent on electronic communication may impede the development of a reading habit and reduce overall attention spans. While certain online resources have the potential to enhance literacy skills, young individuals often prioritize socializing over language development. It is essential to strike a balance by encouraging young people to dedicate time to reading and fostering their writing skills, while also acknowledging the benefits that technology can offer in the acquisition of language skills.
