Revised:

"It is true that a significant amount of food is wasted in restaurants and shops worldwide. This occurs due to various reasons, and certain measures need to be implemented to address this issue and reduce food waste.

There are several reasons behind the wastage of large quantities of food. Firstly, some food and beverages lose their quality quickly when stored. This is often the case with fast food brands, which may include genetically modified ingredients to enhance taste but make them unsuitable for refrigeration or reheating. Secondly, cultural factors also play a role. In some countries, it is common for families to order excessive amounts of food as a way of demonstrating generosity and kindness. These individuals frequently attend social events where lunch invitations or dinners are common, and they feel obligated to request large quantities of food. Consequently, leftovers are often discarded.

To address this issue effectively, several strategies should be implemented. Firstly, governments need to establish regulations and guidelines for shops and restaurants to reduce the use of harmful ingredients in food. By imposing limits on such components, leftover food can be preserved for an extended period without the risk of chemical contamination. Secondly, raising awareness within communities is crucial. People should be made aware of the dire consequences of food waste and the fact that many individuals globally suffer from starvation and poverty. Encouraging individuals to donate or distribute excess food to those in need can not only minimize wastage but also contribute to tackling hunger.

In conclusion, it is imperative that society as a whole takes responsibility for reducing food waste, and governments should play a pivotal role in implementing regulations and raising awareness. By doing so, we can minimize the amount of food that is needlessly discarded."
