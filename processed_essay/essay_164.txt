Revised:

Here is the edited essay with an IELTS structure:

University education is a topic of broad discussion among people. Some individuals propose that students can choose their preferred subjects, while others believe that they should only study subjects related to science and technology. In my opinion, I strongly support the former approach, providing students with the opportunity to select subjects that interest them and help achieve their aspirations.

Firstly, students should have the freedom to study subjects they like, making it easier for them to learn and complete their courses successfully. Every student is unique, with their particular career goals and ambitions. If they are not allowed to follow their passions, they may be unable to fulfill their dreams, causing them to drop out of university. This may create a feeling of worthlessness. Additionally, every subject has its individual value and benefits, irrespective of whether it is science, mathematics, or management. A nation needs everyone to play their role in various sectors like doctors, nurses, engineers, and apprentices for the country's growth.

On the other hand, some individuals suggest that students study subjects related to science and technology as these fields offer the fastest-growing profession, which provides more job opportunities after graduation. Also, they may get a good salary, reputation, and recognition within the industry and society, ultimately improving their standard of living and the nation's growth.

In conclusion, although there are merits and demerits in both views, it is essential to support students' desires and aspirations by allowing them to choose subjects, instead of imposing opinions and wishes that may ruin their future.
