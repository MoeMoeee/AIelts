Topic:

"Some people believe that hobbies need to be difficult to be enjoyable.

To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"Leisure activities are meant to be enjoyable, some argue that they have to be complex in order for them to be amusing. This essay totally disagrees with that statement, I believe that it's not an obligation for hobbies to be difficult .......to be fun.

First of all, when picking a leisure activity, individuals tend to choose something enjoyable, a pastime activity that helps them relax and decompress from a whole week of pressure and work; therefore, the level of difficulty is the last aspect that they base their choice on. Moreover, it has been proven in many studies and documentaries that most people enjoy the kind of hobbies that are simple and take an important amount of time to accomplish; hence, they get to spend most of their free time doing that, for instance, finishing missions of a video game or drawing a painting of a beautiful lake. 

Secondly, humans have a tendency to practice and enjoy collective activities in which every single one of them has a specific task to accomplish so the whole team wins; thus, it would be illogical for this particular game to be difficult. Furthermore, a lot of adults don't prefer hard activities for the simple reason that it requires them to use their muscles or their brain and to think hard to solve complex equations and missions because it makes them feel like they're at work. As a result, they tend to pick simple pastime activities.

To conclude, for the reasons mentioned above, my point of view is that hobbies don't have to be sophisticated to be enjoyable."


Revised:

Leisure activities are meant to bring enjoyment, however, some argue that hobbies must be complex in order to be truly entertaining. This essay completely disagrees with that statement, as I believe that hobbies do not need to be difficult in order to be fun.

First of all, when selecting a leisure activity, individuals usually choose something enjoyable that helps them relax and unwind from the pressures of the workweek. Therefore, the level of difficulty is not an important factor in their decision-making process. Furthermore, it has been proven in various studies and documentaries that most people enjoy hobbies that are simple and can be completed in a reasonable amount of time. For example, completing missions in a video game or creating a beautiful painting of a scenic lake can provide a great deal of enjoyment.

Secondly, humans tend to gravitate towards collective activities where each individual has a specific task to accomplish in order for the entire team to succeed. Therefore, it would be illogical for these activities to be overly difficult or complex. Additionally, many adults prefer leisure activities that do not require them to exert physical or mental effort, as it can remind them of the demands of their work. Consequently, they opt for simpler hobbies that allow them to unwind and relax.

In conclusion, based on the points mentioned above, it is my belief that hobbies do not need to be sophisticated or complex in order to be enjoyable. The most important aspect of a leisure activity is that it brings pleasure and a sense of relaxation to the individual.
