Topic:

"The diagram below shows the average hours of unpaid work per week done by people in different categories. (Unpaid work refers to such activities as childcare in the home, housework and gardening.)

Describe the information presented below, comparing results for men and women in the categories shown. Suggest reasons for what you see."

Essay:

"This diagram looks at the average amount of hours per week spent by men and women doing the housework and carrying the children. The given illustration gives three categories depending on the quantity of kids.

If we look at the data only for women, with the increasing number of  children, the upward trend of working hours can be seen. Moreover, wives carrying more than three children spend twice as much time doing unpaid work compared to those, who have the only child.

On the other side, for men the time spent on the housework insignificantly depends on the number of kids. Moreover, the amount of time even slightly decreases for families with more than five members, in contrast to the smaller ones.

To summarize, the provided line graph highlights that married women spend much more of their time on the occupation related to the house and families, compare to their husbands."

Revised Essay:

The given diagram illustrates the average hours per week spent by men and women in various categories of unpaid work, such as childcare in the home, housework, and gardening. The data is divided into different categories based on the number of children.

Upon examining the data for women, it becomes evident that the amount of time spent on unpaid work increases as the number of children increases. Furthermore, women who have more than three children spend approximately twice as much time on such activities compared to those with only one child.

In contrast, the time spent on housework by men is not significantly affected by the number of children. Additionally, the average time spent on unpaid work even slightly decreases for families with more than five members, as compared to smaller families.

To summarize, the provided diagram clearly shows that married women dedicate a considerable amount of time to household and family-related duties, in comparison to their husbands. This discrepancy in unpaid work may be attributed to traditional gender roles and societal expectations, where women are often seen as primarily responsible for domestic tasks.
