Topic:

"More and more people want to own famous brands of cars, clothes and other items. What are the reason for this. Is this a positive or negative trend?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, the trend of purchasing well-known brands of vehicles, clothes and other goods has been increasing. This raises the question why it is happening and how society will be effected. From my perspective, I suppose it will have a negative impact on society.

Another explanation is their desire for social acceptance that drives them into  buying well reputed and expensive to show off and post their stunning pictures on social media like Facebook, etc,. to attract more attention and admiration. Here's the edited version of your essay with an IELTS structure:

Introduction:
In recent years, there has been an increasing trend of people preferring to own well-known brands of cars, clothes, and other items. This essay discusses the reasons behind this trend and if it is a positive or negative development for society.

Body Paragraph 1:
One reason for the trend of owning famous brands is the influence of mass media on individuals. Advertisements for branded products are found everywhere, from TV to social media, and often feature captivating fashion pieces, catchy slogans, and logo designs. These advertisements unconsciously impact the minds of people, making them more interested in purchasing these branded goods.

Body Paragraph 2:
Another explanation for the trend is the social pressure to conform to certain expectations and standards. Society puts great emphasis on owning expensive and prestigious products as a symbol of high status and success. People buy these brands to show off and flaunt their wealth, posting pictures on social media platforms to gain attention and admiration from their peers.

Body Paragraph 3:
However, this trend can have a negative impact on society. The continuous buying of well-known brands may result in the disappearance of locally made products, leading to an economic crisis in the future. Moreover, it can create a divide between the wealthy and the poor, leading to societal discrimination. Historically, a country's culture and customs have a strong connection with its locally produced goods. If people are not interested in consuming domestic products, this could result in the loss of the country's cultural heritage.

Conclusion:
To conclude, the trend of owning famous brands of items has both positive and negative effects on society. Although it is natural for people to aspire to own prestigious brands, it is important to remember the impact of this trend on society and the economy. People should be encouraged to buy locally produced products to maintain a country's cultural heritage and promote economic growth.
