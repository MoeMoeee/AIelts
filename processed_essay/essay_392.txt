Here is the edited essay according to the IELTS structure:

Some people believe that teenagers should concentrate on all school subjects, while others argue that they should focus on subjects they are good at or find the most interesting. In this essay, I will discuss both sides of the argument and provide my opinion.

Supporters of the first viewpoint argue that studying all subjects allows teenagers to broaden their horizons. By learning a wide range of subjects, students can explore different interests and develop multiple skills and capabilities. For example, a student studying history and mathematics can gain knowledge about ancient times, as well as apply basic statistical skills in real-life situations such as managing personal finances. Moreover, in today's competitive job market, companies often value candidates with a diverse set of skills. Therefore, many people support the idea of students studying all subjects.

On the other hand, proponents of the second viewpoint argue that teenagers should focus on subjects they are passionate about and interested in. This approach eliminates boredom and allows students to fully engage in their chosen subjects. Moreover, focusing on specific subjects can be beneficial for their future career goals. For instance, a student who aspires to become a painter or a financial advisor can start learning the relevant subjects early on, enabling them to acquire the necessary knowledge and skills to achieve their career ambitions.

In my opinion, educational institutions should follow the traditional method of teaching all subjects in the school curriculum. This is because teenagers are often impulsive and have limited decision-making skills. By studying all subjects, students are exposed to a wider range of knowledge, allowing for critical thinking and exploration of interests they may otherwise overlook.

In conclusion, while focusing on subjects of personal interest can make learning more engaging and help students achieve their career goals, studying all subjects provides opportunities for critical thinking, diversifies skill sets, and expands horizons. For these reasons, I strongly believe that the traditional approach of teaching all school subjects is more beneficial for teenagers.
