The issue of keeping animals in zoos has long been a controversial topic. While some people deem it wrong, others believe that zoos play an important role in education and conservation. This essay will discuss both views and provide my perspective on the matter.

On one hand, there are substantial arguments against keeping wild animals in captivity. Firstly, animals in zoos are deprived of their natural habitat, which may cause them to develop severe psychological and physical problems. For instance, they have limited space to move and cannot satisfy their natural instinct to hunt or roam freely. Secondly, some animals may become violent and turn against their caretakers, exposing them to severe harm. Finally, wild animal species that are bred in captivity might not survive in the wild since they lack the necessary skills to survive, such as hunting and self-defense techniques.

On the other hand, zoos serve various purposes, especially in countries where people do not have access to natural habitats and wildlife reserves. For instance, through zoological parks, the public can observe and learn about various species of animals, which may contribute to raising awareness about conservation efforts. Moreover, zoos can also play a role in preserving endangered wildlife species by providing shelter, protection, and breeding programs. For example, the Indian government has successfully saved tigers from extinction by breeding and releasing them into their natural habitats.

In conclusion, while zoos serve various purposes, my opinion is that animals should not be kept in zoos. They deserve to live in their natural habitat, where they can express their natural behavior and live freely. Furthermore, animals in captivity might face various forms of abuse, such as circuses and exhibitions.
