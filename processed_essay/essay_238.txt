Revised:

Most nations are facing a grave issue of weight gain in toddlers. This essay will highlight the major causes of obesity in children as well as the potential alarming effects of this problem in the future.

To begin with, children are becoming obese due to two main reasons. Firstly, they have excessive intake of junk food. They usually prefer burgers, pastries, and pasta over healthy homemade food. For instance, families often go to restaurants on the weekends and consume high-calorie meals, leading to weight gain. Secondly, a sedentary lifestyle is causing weight gain in children. They tend to engage in indoor activities that do not involve physical exertion. For example, a survey conducted in the USA revealed that 80 percent of children opt to stay at home watching television and playing indoor games, contributing to obesity. Thus, a lack of physical activity and the consumption of junk food are major contributors to childhood obesity.

Weight gain among children can lead to severe health issues in the future. With high cholesterol levels in their blood vessels, young people may suffer from heart disease at a very early age. Furthermore, diabetes will become increasingly common among children. Due to obesity, children may have high insulin levels, which can lead to diabetes. For instance, research in northern India has shown that 60 percent of obese individuals suffer from diabetes and heart diseases. Therefore, it can be concluded that obesity is associated with the development of dangerous diseases.

In conclusion, this essay highlighted the major causes of obesity in children, namely junk food consumption and a sedentary lifestyle. It also discussed the potential health effects, such as the increased risk of diabetes and heart diseases. To address this issue, it is important for both parents and schools to promote healthy eating habits and encourage physical activity among children to prevent the onset of obesity-related health problems.
