Topic:

"Some people believe children should get homework everyday, while others think they should not. Discuss both sides and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Education is a very much needed exigency part of our life. If children or youngsters do not get a good education, it will be devastating for their lives. Whether or not children should receive homework every day is a matter of debate. Some people believe that children should get homework every day from school and on the other hand, some people believe that children should not get too much homework from school. 
Both views will be discussed in this essay before concluding.

Firstly, we must understand the concept of homework. The teacher is not able to cover a full course in school so that they give an assignment to children. Furthermore, most of the students are not doing their homework unless it is necessary, so it is important to give them homework. Moreover, some subjects need practice. For example, science, mathematics, and art need constant practice, if they do not, it is very difficult to understand for them. By giving homework it automatically helps to improve. Practice makes perfect.

However, it is important to give homework to youngsters but children spend 7-8 hours in school, after school if a teacher gives to much homework, it pressurizes many children. Besides this, when the children get too much homework, they are not able to do any other physical activity which is also important in their lives. And it is also caused to depreciation.
In my opinion, the concept of homework is not harmful. The problem arises when a teacher gives too much homework and when a student fails to use their time wisely. For example, if the deadline for a specific assignment is after 2 weeks, most students would leave it until the very last minute and by doing this they are putting themselves under unwanted pressure.
In conclusion, I think children should get homework for their improvement."

Revised Essay:

Education is crucial for the development and success of children. The debate surrounding homework is a conflicting issue that has sparked differing opinions. While some argue that children should receive homework regularly, others believe that they should not. This essay will elaborate on both perspectives.

On the one hand, proponents of homework argue that it provides opportunities for students to practice and reinforce what they have learned in school. Homework serves as a supplement to deliver the full course material and helps students to retain what they have learned. Furthermore, subjects such as math, science, and art require regular practice to be mastered, making homework an indispensable tool in achieving academic excellence.

On the other hand, opponents of homework argue that too much homework may have a detrimental impact on children's mental and physical health. After spending 7-8 hours in school, doing homework in the evenings can be a significant burden on children, affecting their emotional well-being. Additionally, students may not get enough exercise or rest, which could result in physical health issues.

From my perspective, homework is essential for children's growth and development, but its quantity and quality should be managed and tailored to individual needs. Too much homework would lead to unnecessary stress and negative outcomes. Conversely, homework that is appropriately structured and targeted, can improve a student's understanding, processing, and retention of the material.

In conclusion, the topic of homework is a complex one, with valid arguments on both sides. While homework serves as a valuable tool in academic performance, it should be given in moderation, giving children the opportunity to engage in physical activities and maintain mental and emotional well-being. As such, it is crucial for both teachers and parents to monitor and manage the homework given to students, ensuring that it correlates with their abilities and is appropriate.
