Topic:

"Do you think it is better for students to work before their university study? Why? Use reasons and specific examples to support your choice."

Essay:

"Critics said that students shouldn't work before enrolling in university as they will have plenty of time to do so sooner or later. I am strongly against  this affirmation for two reasons, firstly taking a gap year provides youngsterswith  a more thorough career-plan beforehand, and ,secondly it can propose an alternative plan for those who perform not so well at school .

To embark on, teenagers can make a better career plan following the internship. Realizing how cruel the workplace is only after they graduate, students have no clues on how to prepare themselves at school in an attempt to fit in the commercial world. For instance, they don't know how to excel the knowledge they learnt from school to the society. Additionally,  discovering the differences between reality and the classroom, it can serve as a driving force for striving for a better grade at school and thus offer a better job in future. 

Apart from that, this can provide an exit plan for those who hate study. Not everyone likes learning, some may be just not suitable for it innately. Through the experiences of working, students with no interest in school can leave school earlier and stop wasting their time. Instead of doing something they may feel suffering and with no promising outcomes, they could explore more possibilities by trying something totally new. A typical case in point, Bill Gates, the founder of apple didn't finish university and still made a fortune afterwards through countless trials in the work setting.  

To conclude, by citing two reasons, as more proper career plans and more possibilities, I strongly encourage students to work before the commencement of college. I believe a gap year internship would definitely bring more good than harm to our students."

Below is the edited version of the essay, structured according to IELTS guidelines:

Introduction:
There is an ongoing debate about whether students should work before starting their university studies. While some critics argue that they will have ample time to work later, I firmly believe that there are two compelling reasons why students should consider taking a gap year for work experience. This essay will highlight the benefits of taking a year off work before starting university.

Body Paragraph 1:
Firstly, students can benefit from taking a gap year as it provides them with a more comprehensive career plan. By working in the real world, young adults can gain practical knowledge of the industry they wish to pursue, which can help them prepare for the challenges and opportunities ahead. For instance, they can learn how to apply the theoretical knowledge they have gained in the classroom to real-world scenarios. Furthermore, this can act as a motivator, prompting them to work harder and aim for better grades at school to gain better job opportunities in the future.

Body Paragraph 2:
Secondly, a gap year can be an alternative option for those students who are not performing well in school but are skilled in other areas. By exploring different work environments, they can figure out which path suits them best. For individuals who do not enjoy school, this can provide them with the opportunity to pursue a different career path that they are passionate about. For instance, the success stories of many entrepreneurs who dropped out of college, such as Bill Gates, demonstrate that a degree is not a prerequisite for professional achievement.

Conclusion:
Overall, I believe that taking a gap year to gain work experience is highly beneficial for students. It helps them develop a more thorough career plan and provides an alternative option for those who dislike traditional schooling. In conclusion, I support the idea of taking a gap year as it can offer a host of advantages to young adults that will assist them in achieving their goals and aspirations in life.
