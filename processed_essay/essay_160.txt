Topic:

"Some people believe that hobbies need to be difficult to be enjoyable. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"It is believed that hobbies have to be challenging and advanced in order to be pleasing. In my opinion, I completely disagree with this view as I think hobbies are personal references and it varies depending on each individual’s aspire.
Everyone has different personality and life; hence their hobbies can be very unique and it is not necessary to require extreme difficulty. In fact, there are a multitude of relaxing hobbies that vast majority of us commonly enjoy such as listening to music, reading books, painting…. Hobbies is subjective and its key aim is to let people discover unseen aspect and nurture themselves in their own preferred ways. We should not set standards, judge or compels anyone to live up to others ideal hobbies as it is starkly a personal choice.
Another point to consider is that too much of a good thing could possibly be detrimental. Hobbies that have high level of difficulty is likely to be beneficial because it boosts your competitive spirit and gives you incentive to be an expert at it. In some case it may become your profession in the future. Nonetheless, continuously pushing and pressure yourself to only enjoy hobbies that are excessively hard could result in stress and anxiety. Furthermore, you would not be able to diversify and explore yourself if you solely seeking for a certain type of hobby. Despite others’ opinions, relaxation and your interests are the most important.
To conclude, hobbies can be either easy or obstinate as long as it is enjoyable. Everyone should feel freely to decide what activity they want to dedicate their spare time to."

Possible IELTS Writing Task 2 Edit:

Introduction:
It is commonly believed that hobbies must be challenging and complex to provide satisfaction. However, in my opinion, this is not always true because hobbies are subjective and depend on personal preference.

Body Paragraph 1:
Firstly, everyone has different personalities, interests, and lives, which means that their hobbies can vary significantly. Therefore, it is not necessary to set strict criteria for the level of difficulty in hobbies. Many people find stress relief and joy in simple hobbies like listening to music, reading books, or painting. These hobbies can be highly beneficial for mental and emotional health, despite their ease. Hence, individuals should be free to choose whatever activity they enjoy without feeling pressured to conform to any specific standards.

Body Paragraph 2:
Moreover, while challenging hobbies can be rewarding and stimulating, they may not always be enjoyable and relaxing, especially if they become too competitive or stressful. For instance, if someone constantly pushes themselves to engage only in difficult hobbies, they may miss out on other engaging activities that are more aligned with their interests and abilities. This can lead to dissatisfaction and boredom. Consequently, it is crucial to strike a balance between exploration and enjoyment of hobbies, and avoid being too rigid or demanding.

Conclusion:
To sum up, hobbies should be seen as a personal preference and not as a means to prove one's skill or competence. Therefore, it is wrong to believe that hobbies must be challenging to be pleasurable. Instead, one should choose a hobby that brings relaxation, enjoyment, and personal satisfaction to one's life. By doing so, one can discover hidden talents, release stress, and enhance one's overall well-being.
