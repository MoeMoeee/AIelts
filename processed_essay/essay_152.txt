Revised:

Here's an edited version of the essay based on IELTS writing structure:

Introduction:

It is well-known that cybercrime, such as hacking and identity theft, is becoming a growing concern for many people today. This essay will discuss the problems caused by cybercrime and provide possible solutions that ordinary people and businesses can adopt.

Body Paragraph 1:

The rising incidence of cybercrime can be attributed to the proliferation of malicious software, as opposed to secure technology. Ordinary users exacerbate the situation by being IT-illiterate, which makes it easier for them to fall prey to hacking and identity theft. As a result, they easily give hackers access to their sensitive personal information, such as bank account numbers and private chats. Furthermore, cybercrime victims often face psychological abuse such as cyberbullying. Corporations are also at risk due to the increased number of people with access to their data through shared servers, and the possibility of data theft through the misuse of official gadgets.

Body Paragraph 2:

To effectively address the problem of cybercrime, several measures must be implemented. Governments should institute a legal framework that focuses on the development of privacy policies in cyberspace and the establishment of a management authority responsible for enforcing these policies. Clear guidelines on investigations and punishment for cybercrime would also go a long way in reducing its incidence. Corporations must reinforce their privacy policies by subjecting employees who have access to their data to rigorous cyber security training and skills assessments. In addition, employees should be prohibited from using official gadgets for personal purposes to reduce incidents of data theft.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, cybercrime is a major concern for individuals and corporations alike. While several factors have contributed to its rise, the implementation of privacy policies and stringent cybersecurity measures can go a long way in mitigating its effects. Ultimately, it is everyone's responsibility to be vigilant in safeguarding their personal information and using technology responsibly.
