Nowadays, there is an ongoing debate about the implementation of a universal legal system. Some argue that having a single law system worldwide would be beneficial, while others believe that countries should have their own laws. In this essay, I will discuss both points of view and give my opinion.

On the one hand, proponents of a universal legal system argue that it would provide equal rights for all individuals worldwide. For example, if the entire world were governed by a single set of laws that eliminated restrictions on travel and visas, people would be able to freely travel to any country without permission. Moreover, another benefit of a universal legal system is that it could potentially reduce racism and discrimination.

On the other hand, there are several disadvantages to having a single legal system because every country has its own unique culture and customs. Adopting a universal legal system would mean that countries would not be able to preserve their own cultural practices. For instance, in India, numerous festivals such as Diwali, the Kite Festival, and Onam are celebrated. However, implementing the principle of "One nation, One rule" could lead to the extinction of these cultural traditions. Additionally, countries having their own laws allows them to cater to the specific needs and contexts of their citizens, which ultimately benefits their economy and productivity.

In conclusion, I personally believe that each country should retain its individual legal system rather than adopting a universal one. This is because a single legal system is not a one-size-fits-all solution and may not take into account the unique cultural, economic, and social aspects of each country. Thus, it is important for countries to have their own laws and regulations in order to better serve the interests and protect the rights of their citizens.
