Topic:

"Some people think that it is a good idea to socialise with work colleagues during evenings and weekends. Other people think it is important to keep working life completely seperate from social life."

Essay:

"Since our job take the majority of our daytime (or nighttime, that's depend on the job) our colleagues are a important part of our social life. We see them everyday and we usually have to interact with them many times. Do those interactions have to stay strictly professional or is it better including then in our private life?

First, that's depend on everyone's personnality. Some people are good to keep distance during the working time's interaction, keep neutral conversations and seprate clearly the private life and professional life. On a other side, some people can't control it and their personnality will push them to be friendly with people who they get along with or be distant tp people with who they feel inconfortable.

In fact, it is important to feel good at work. A heavy atmosphere caused by tensions between coworkers could effect very badly out life even when we come back home.

Socialize with work colleagues in the free time can be good occasion to know people better, away from the working stress and pressure. We can discover another part of personnality, hobbies. or personnal problems and then we can be more able to understand them during hard time at work.

Of course sometimes in an other hand some matters can appear when line between private and professional life start be fuzzy. For example, when two coworkers becam a couple and continue to work together or when a friendship between two workers can be gossips' start point.

But since we are all humans with our own sensitive points, it is important to be keep a good atmosphere at work by speaking out all the problems to release all the tensions. Communication is the key but also sharing a good time with coworkers away from work can be a nice way to understand more each other."


Revised:

Due to the significant time spent at work, some people believe that socializing with colleagues outside of work hours is beneficial. In contrast, others suggest that it is essential to keep one's professional and personal lives separate. In this essay, we will examine both sides of the argument and evaluate the benefits and drawbacks of each.

On the one hand, some individuals prefer to avoid establishing personal relationships with their working colleagues, maintaining a comfortable distance separating their professional and social lives. Conversely, individuals with outgoing personalities may find it challenging to keep their work interactions purely professional. However, it is vital to consider a positive work atmosphere since employees spend a significant portion of their time at their workplace. Various tensions between coworkers can have a detrimental impact on one's private life, resulting in stress and discomfort. Socializing with colleagues outside of work can provide an opportunity to connect with coworkers, learn about one another's hobbies and interests, and create a relaxed, positive atmosphere away from the strain and pressure of work.

On the other hand, there are instances where the line between one's work and personal lives can become blurred, leading to potential issues. For example, dating a coworker may create an unpleasant situation in the event of a breakup, and a friendship between two colleagues can spark unwanted rumors. However, communication is an essential tool in resolving tensions that may arise in such situations.

In conclusion, while there are potential risks associated with socializing with colleagues outside of work, various benefits, such as the positive atmosphere it can create, outweigh the cons of establishing personal relationships in a professional setting. So, it is necessary to maintain a balance between one's professional and personal lives to ensure a harmonious and productive workplace.
