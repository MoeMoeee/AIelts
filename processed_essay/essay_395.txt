Revised:

Nowadays, there has been a significant increase in the number of people opting for cosmetic surgeries worldwide. While this trend may have initially been popularized by celebrities like Michael Jackson, it has now extended to the general public. Many individuals are choosing to alter their physical appearances, driven by a desire to be attractive and gain recognition.

Celebrities in the limelight often feel the need to enhance their appearance to secure job opportunities. Consequently, they undergo various cosmetic procedures to improve their facial features such as the nose, lips, cheeks, and jawline. Additionally, some opt for procedures that aim to change the shape of their face or tighten their skin. Whitening procedures are also gaining popularity. However, it is alarming to note that many people are undergoing these procedures without conducting adequate research.

Unfortunately, individuals who blindly pursue cosmetic surgeries are often unaware of the potential health risks associated with them. Each method carries its own set of side effects, some of which may lead to lifelong disabilities. It is never advised by doctors to take such risks, but in the current era, healthcare professionals are sometimes more concerned with financial gain rather than the well-being of their patients. The rise of cosmetic hospitals worldwide further exacerbates this issue. It is the responsibility of the hospital staff to guide individuals and only perform procedures that are of utmost importance, such as reconstructing the face of victims of acid attacks.

In conclusion, I strongly oppose the idea of opting for cosmetic surgeries solely for the purpose of beautification. While there may be exceptions where such procedures are necessary, individuals should be cautious as there is no guarantee of a successful outcome. It is essential to remember that once a procedure is done incorrectly, reverting to one's previous appearance is often impossible.
