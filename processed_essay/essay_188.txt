Topic:

"Young people these days tend to be less polite and respectful than in the past. Causes and solutions."

Essay:

"Lifestyle has undergone dramatic changes over couple of decades. It is irrefutable that the modern generation nowadays has become less considerate and civil than in the earlier times. Here,i would like to explore the possible causes of this situation and suggest some ways forward.

There are multifarious causes behind this phenomenon.The most important cause is the disintegration of the traditional joint family system. In today‘s nuclear families, there are no grandparents to teach moral values to children. Parents are busy working and do not spend quality time with children. Consequently, children become self-centred and uncaring for the world around them.

Further emphasizing from my point of view. Nowadays,we are part of a consumerist society. We desire to have the latest things and to get those things we are becoming workaholics. We set goals for ourselves and work hard to achieve them. As a result politeness and respect have taken a back seat. Finally, we have the influence of the western culture. There, it is considered quite normal to argue and question the elders. By contrast,others have conflicting views.

They think that it is necessary to install moral values in children at a young age. So, joint family system should be encouraged. Secondly, community welfare programmes should be part of high school curriculum. We can also take the help of media to show programmes which highlight the good points of earlier times and discourage consumerism. But, the final onus is on the people themselves. They should be willing to change.

To put it in a nutshell, I pen down saying that, in the fast-paced world of today, people have changed and become selfish and self centred, but steps can be taken to re-imbibe some virtues of the olden times."


Revised:

Revised Essay:

In recent times, it is commonly observed that the younger generation tends to display less polite and respectful behavior than their counterparts in the past. The reasons and solutions to this phenomenon are manifold, and in this essay, we shall delve into them and suggest some possible solutions.

One of the foremost reasons that contribute to this behavior is the disintegration of the traditional joint family system. In today's era, nuclear families have become prevalent, and with the absence of grandparents, children no longer have the opportunity to be taught moral values. Parents, in general, are busy with their work and do not have enough time to spend with their children, which can cause them to become self-centered and indifferent.

Moreover, consumerism has influenced our lifestyles, and we aspire to possess the latest technology, gadgets, fashion, and so on, which has led to an increasing amount of workaholic behavior. Overemphasis on achieving personal goals often takes precedence over exhibiting politeness and respect towards others. Additionally, the western culture that has infiltrated our society has made questioning and debating elders a commonplace practice and has eroded the principles of traditional respect.

To address these issues, it is suggested that measures should be taken to reintroduce moral values within the young generation at an early age. Reinstating the concept of a joint family system may also be beneficial in infusing the concept of respect and politeness in the younger generation. Incorporating community welfare programs within the high school curriculum can increase awareness among students about the importance of respecting others.

Furthermore, leveraging the media, such as through broadcast programs showcasing the relevance and significance of older times when politeness and respect were given paramount importance, can help combat consumerism.

In conclusion, although the fast-paced lifestyle of the current generation has led them to become selfish and self-centered, steps can be taken to imbibe and reinforce the virtues of olden times. A concerted effort from every individual to bring behavioral changes would eventually reap benefits in fostering society's fundamental fabric of politeness and respect.
