Revised:

Opinions are divided on whether it is beneficial or detrimental when an increasing number of people today are opting for a solitary lifestyle. This essay will explore both the positive and negative effects on individuals.

On the one hand, living a solitary life could be disadvantageous for various reasons related to loneliness and difficulties when individuals change their minds. Solo dwellers might feel lonely or isolated during emotionally vulnerable moments when there is no one around, making it a struggle for them to unburden themselves. This circumstance exacerbates mental and emotional issues, leading to an unhealthy mental life. Furthermore, the negative feelings during festive seasons, such as seeing happy couples and joyful families, can intensify the sense of isolation. Additionally, the decision to live alone could be temporary, and once they want to change in order to start a family, this intention might not be a straightforward task.

Despite the aforementioned downsides, this way of living has its own merits, chief among which are a sense of freedom and self-improvement. People who choose to live alone have the leeway to enjoy the independence of life without worrying about others' opinions or criticisms from co-living partners. For instance, solo dwellers can refurbish their living space or host late-night parties without considering their parents' preferences and limitations. Additionally, solo living is said to be the most advantageous lifestyle for enhancing one's career. Individuals have the opportunity to take advantage of alone time and space to develop personal skills, such as financial management, repair skills, cooking, and nurturing their individual interests. If they settle for a co-living arrangement with their parents or spouses, they tend to rely on others.

In conclusion, I contend that while there are potential drawbacks, the experience of solo living can have favorable impacts from an individual perspective.
