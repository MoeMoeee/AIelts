Title: The Government's Role in Providing Housing for Impoverished Individuals

Introduction:
To ensure individuals' safety and security, a secure and safe home is essential. While some argue that the government should provide free housing for those who cannot afford it, others disagree. I strongly agree with the former notion due to several reasons.

Paragraph 1:
One crucial reason is that providing free housing for the impoverished is a duty of the government in order to protect and support its citizens. Several countries, such as Canada and India, are already offering cost-free houses to underprivileged families. This demonstrates the positive approach of the government towards its people and their well-being.

Paragraph 2:
Moreover, it is the government's responsibility to care for its local population. By providing free housing, the government can effectively demonstrate its commitment to its residents' welfare. Such initiatives foster a sense of security and trust among the citizens, ultimately promoting a harmonious society.

Paragraph 3:
Additionally, providing housing for all individuals helps reduce the rates of death and disease. Access to a home safeguards individuals from extreme weather conditions, such as heat, snowstorms, cold, rain, and more. Homeless individuals, due to their financial constraints, are often forced to live on the streets or in precarious conditions, making them vulnerable to illnesses and even death. By providing housing for the impoverished, the government can effectively address and alleviate such risks.

Conclusion:
While it is true that providing free housing may strain the government's budget, there are several compelling reasons to support this initiative. Ensuring the safety and security of the population, promoting a positive relationship between the government and its citizens, and fostering harmony in the nation are just some of the benefits that can be achieved by providing homes to those in need. Thus, the government should consider offering free housing to individuals who lack the means to afford it.
