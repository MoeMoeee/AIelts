Topic:

"We should return to traditional and older values of respect for family and community. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Essay:

"It is claimed that modern society should re-embrace the time-honored, traditional values of family and community that it has abandoned. In my point of view, it is true that more importance  should indeed be attached to the household and collectivism, but it seems unrealistic to apply those old values in contemporary society.  

Admittedly, more appreciation toward our family and community would help to strengthen family bonding and promote social cohesion. Individualism has eroded the younger generation, making them indifferent to what happens in their own communities or even in their own homes. Once old-fashioned concepts come back, people can be unconsciously influenced and thereby spend more time accompanying their family members and giving an ear to their elderly parents. In the meantime, more participation and support from neighbours would be seen in all kinds of communal activities. This progress, viewed in the long run, will necessarily bring a united and harmonious nation.

However, when trying to arouse people’s awareness of respecting their family units or communities, the risk of hypercorrection should not be ignored. This is because, being well-educated and growing up in this enlightened society, people nowadays have stronger individual consciousness and refuse to have their lives arranged. Facing the huge generation gap, some people may have to sacrifice their personal choices and values and submit to the preference of their parents, if asked to “respect” the decision made by their family. Worse still, some minority groups, in order to be more socially accepted, are likely to weaken their identity and follow the modern social trend blindly, which will also pose a threat to our social diversity.

In conclusion, we are supposed to pay more attention to  traditional family values and the sense of community, but it does not mean that these old ideas should be fully accepted in this ever-evolving world."


Revised:

"It is argued that modern society should reconnect with traditional values of respect for family and community that have been abandoned. In my opinion, while it is true that greater importance should be placed on these values, it is unrealistic to expect a complete return to traditional ways in contemporary society.

Undeniably, greater appreciation for our family and community can help strengthen family bonds and promote social cohesion. Individualism has eroded the younger generation, leading them to become indifferent to what happens within their own communities and even within their own homes. Reintroducing old-fashioned values can subconsciously influence people to spend more time with their family members and lend an ear to their elderly parents. Simultaneously, this can encourage greater participation and support from neighbors in communal activities. In the long run, this progress is likely to foster greater unity and harmony within the nation.

However, as we attempt to instill a sense of respect for family and community, we must not overlook the potential dangers of excessive correction. In today’s highly educated and enlightened society, individuals possess a stronger sense of individuality and reject having their lives dictated by others. Faced with significant generational gaps, some individuals may have to sacrifice their personal choices and values in order to comply with their family’s wishes. Furthermore, some minority groups may weaken their own identities and blindly conform to modern social trends for the sake of acceptance, which threatens our societal diversity.

In conclusion, while traditional family values and the sense of community should be given greater attention, it is important to acknowledge that these values may need to be adapted to suit the ever-evolving world we live in. Striking a balance between maintaining certain principles and accommodating individual preferences can lead to a more harmonious society."
