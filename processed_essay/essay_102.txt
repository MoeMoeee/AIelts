To conclude, I agree that country's development and entertainment is facilitated by the different mixture of groups. My reasoning is based on two main thoughts. Nowadays, there is a widespread belief that a country can benefit both socially and economically by having a diverse population with people from different nationalities. In my view, I strongly agree that a mixture of nationalities plays a crucial role in making a country more interesting and promoting its development.

First and foremost, a varied population can bring about cultural diversity, which leads to a more interesting and fulfilling lifestyle. For instance, Mongolia, my home country, has a multitude of ethnic groups, each with its unique customs, practices, and festivities. This diversity has enriched our vibrant culture and tradition, and I have personally experienced the joy of participating in or observing various ceremonies, dances, music performances, and food fairs. Such cultural exchange and interaction have also helped to foster social cohesion and mutual respect among different communities, preventing conflicts or discrimination based on ethnicity or race.

Moreover, a diverse population can contribute to a country's development by providing a wider pool of talents, skills, and perspectives. As people from different nationalities have different educational backgrounds, vocational expertise, and communication styles, they can bring fresh ideas and methods to solve various problems or challenges that a country faces. For example, the United States of America, one of the most diverse nations in the world, has become a global economic and cultural powerhouse because of its inclusive policies that attract and retain skilled immigrants. These immigrants not only fill the gaps in certain sectors or industries but also generate innovation and entrepreneurship that create jobs, wealth, and social mobility.

However, it should be noted that achieving a successful and harmonious blend of nationalities requires an inclusive and respectful attitude from both the host society and the immigrant population. Governments and authorities need to ensure that there is equal access to public services, education, healthcare, and legal protection for all citizens, regardless of their ethnic, linguistic, or religious backgrounds. Likewise, immigrants need to respect the local laws, customs, and values, while also preserving and sharing their own cultural heritage with the host community.

In conclusion, the presence of a mixture of nationalities can bring various benefits to a country, including cultural diversity, social cohesion, and economic development. Therefore, I firmly believe that a country that embraces diversity and inclusivity can thrive and prosper in the globalized world.
