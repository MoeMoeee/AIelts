Here is an edited version of the essay with IELTS structure:

Introduction:
 
It is a matter of debate whether the government should fund the construction of theatres and sports stadiums or invest in medical care and education. Some people argue that medical amenities and education deserve more funding, while others believe that recreational buildings are equally important. This essay will discuss both viewpoints and elaborate on why I agree with the former.

Body Paragraph 1:
 
Firstly, it is essential to prioritize medical care and education investments since the number of severe accidents is increasing daily. Immediate treatment is often a matter of life or death, and adequate medical care is known to contribute to longer life expectancy. Countries with excellent medical services, such as developed nations, have higher longevity rates. Thus, investing funds in the healthcare sector could help save lives and promote healthier living.

Body Paragraph 2:
 
Furthermore, education is a crucial factor in securing well-paying jobs, especially in a highly competitive environment. People with advanced education qualifications have a higher chance of gaining employment, even during financial crises. In addition, countries with a strong educational foundation, such as Canada, have significant economic advancements, higher employability rates, and a better standard of living.

Body Paragraph 3:
 
On the other hand, some advocate the construction of theatres and sports stadiums. They argue that such buildings provide relaxation and entertainment, with recreational activities playing a key role in improving mental health. For example, attending a theatre play or participating in sporting events can be effective stress management techniques for individuals with highly demanding jobs.

Conclusion:
 
In conclusion, while theatres and sports stadiums can enhance people’s happiness and well-being, medical care and education investments are crucial for a country's economy and its citizens' long-term prosperity. Therefore, it is advisable for the government to prioritize funding medical and educational sectors while still making provisions for recreational buildings.
