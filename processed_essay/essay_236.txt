Revised:

It is often observed in today's world that individuals in high positions tend to receive higher salaries compared to those in lower positions. This essay aims to discuss the extent to which I agree with the notion that people in high positions should be paid more. Moreover, this essay will provide reasons to support the argument for the readers.

First and foremost, I strongly believe that individuals who choose to work in positions of high responsibility should receive higher pay than those in lower positions. This is because those in higher positions take on a significant amount of responsibility within their work. Therefore, it is only fair that they receive a higher salary for the extra burden they bear. For example, CEOs and other high-level executives have a multitude of responsibilities, such as overseeing various departments and ensuring the smooth operation of the company. As a result, the substantial amount of effort they put into their work should be remunerated accordingly.

Conversely, individuals in lower positions generally have less responsibility compared to their superiors. Providing them with exorbitant salaries would not be justifiable. Furthermore, offering a wide range of salaries for lower positions can have a negative impact on a company's finances. However, it is crucial to ensure that these lower positions are compensated adequately but reasonably in order to maintain financial stability.

In conclusion, I strongly support the notion that individuals in high positions should be rewarded with higher salaries due to the increased responsibility they bear. On the other hand, those in lower positions should not necessarily be entitled to receive such high remuneration. By adhering to this principle, a fair and balanced compensation structure can be established within organizations.
