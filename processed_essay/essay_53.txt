Revised:

Here is an edited version of the essay with an IELTS structure:

Nowadays, giving gifts has become a common tradition during special occasions such as birthdays, weddings, and more. Some people believe that money is the best gift to give teenagers, while others disagree. In this essay, both views will be discussed, followed by my personal opinion.

On the one hand, giving money as a gift to teenagers can be beneficial in today's sluggish economic condition. It can help them balance their expenditures and make independent financial decisions. For instance, when I was struggling financially before my 20th birthday, my parents and close friends gifted me money. This helped me handle my situation and was one of the best presents that I have ever received.

On the other hand, some people disagree that money is the best gift to give teenagers. They argue that gifts should be unique and selected based on various factors such as age, occasion, and personality. A well-thought-out gift can have a spiritual value and can create a distinct picture of the giver's personality in the recipient's mind. Additionally, in some cases, giving money can be considered inappropriate and may negatively affect the recipient's attitude towards the giver. For example, according to a recent study, over 87% of newly married couples do not prefer to receive money from each other as it may affect their feelings and attitudes towards their partner.

In my opinion, while money can be helpful and appreciated, it cannot replace the emotional value of a thoughtful gift. Each gift has its own unique sense and plays an important role in creating lasting memories.

In conclusion, although money has its advantages, it cannot replace the emotional value that a well-thought-out gift can provide. Therefore, I suggest that when selecting gifts for teenagers, it is essential to consider the occasion, recipient's need, and personality to ensure that the gift has a sentimental value that they will cherish for years to come.
