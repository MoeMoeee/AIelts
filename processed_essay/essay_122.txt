Revised:

This problem of young people knowing less about traditions and culture than in the past can be effectively addressed through the joint efforts of both teachers and parents. Firstly, parents can encourage their children to participate in family activities and motivate them to interact with their grandparents, who possess a wealth of knowledge and wisdom. Such conversations would allow for the exchange of information between generations and facilitate the intersection of history and technology. Thus, parents are required to find common ground to initiate communication between these two groups.

Regarding schools, they can also play an important role. By assigning tasks that require interaction with elderly people, young people can be stimulated to converse with the elderly. Through these interactions, they can learn about the significance of grandparents in the present generation. By incorporating cultural education into the curriculum, schools can also help foster an appreciation of traditions and culture.

Overall, it is a negative development that young people today know less about traditions and culture than in the past. However, by encouraging intergenerational communication and promoting cultural education, parents and teachers can help bridge this knowledge gap. It is essential that we preserve our cultural heritage and pass it on to the next generation so that they can appreciate and continue to enrich it.
