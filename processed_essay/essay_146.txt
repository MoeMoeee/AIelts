Topic:

"Some people say that music is a good way of bringing people of different

cultures and ages together.

To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?"

Essay:

"Music is a tool by which people from different cultures and age groups can unify together, this is a common belief by some people. In my opinion, I strongly agree. 

For many years, music was one of the criteria by which you can know and understand some people’s culture or background. For example, if you hear a particular melody, you can relate it to a specific group of people or culture. The music of South Egypt differs from the Mediterranean. On the other hand, the music of South Egypt is so close to the music of many African countries. Moreover, you can unify people under the music umbrella.

However, you can find different kinds of music in the same country. Not only does each type identify  the environmental nature of these people, but also it reflects their culture and belonging. Today, I watched a concert for Mohamed Mounir, one of the most famous singers in Egypt. The orchestra contains musicians from different countries. The singer himself is Egyptian Nubian, however, the orchestra has people from 10 different countries. Some of them were from Germany, others were Arabs and, surprisingly, the solo violin musician was from Tokyo. Years back, Mounir used to make concerts in Berlin, although most of the attendees could not understand Arabic,  his concerts had been fully booked months before.

 Music is an easy language that brings people together. For example, I myself enjoy French classical music a lot, but I cannot understand French well. Some instruments belong to a special culture more than others. The harp is a symbol of  ancient Egyptian music. On the other hand, the tambourine belongs to the Nubian and Sudanese cultures.

To sum up, In my opinion, we can use music to unify nations. The arts in general, is a great opportunity to dissolve the conflicts and differences between us. It makes us feel uniquely dissolved in each other."
