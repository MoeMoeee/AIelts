Revised:

People hold different opinions regarding the debate surrounding whether individuals should earn high salaries or not. Some argue that high salaries are beneficial for a country, while others believe that the government should intervene and limit the amount people can earn. In my opinion, both perspectives have valid arguments that should be taken into consideration.

One reason why some people support the idea of earning high salaries is due to the increasing phenomenon of globalization. As globalization continues to grow, underdeveloped and developing countries often need to offer high salaries to attract and retain highly qualified individuals. It is natural for experts in their respective fields to desire higher wages. Failure to provide high salaries can lead to a brain drain, where talented individuals migrate to countries that offer better compensation. For example, in Turkey, a significant number of doctors have immigrated to Germany as their needs were not met by the government. Consequently, the healthcare system in Turkey has suffered, resulting in inadequate services for its citizens.

On the other hand, opponents argue that the government should regulate and limit salaries to promote equality. They assert that excessively high salaries can have negative consequences, one of which is a decline in the quality of education. When individuals earn high salaries, a significant portion of the government budget is often allocated towards these wages. Unfortunately, this leaves insufficient funds for educational facilities and resources. Consequently, many students are unable to afford education due to expensive school fees. To address this issue, governments should prioritize allocating adequate funds to improve education instead of focusing on high wages.

To sum up, after considering both perspectives, it is important to acknowledge the valid points made by both sides. Ignoring any of these arguments can lead to undesirable consequences. Therefore, it is crucial for governments to strike a balance between providing competitive salaries to retain talent and investing in sectors such as education for the betterment of society as a whole.
