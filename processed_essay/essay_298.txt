Many individuals believe that young people who commit serious crimes, such as robbery or violent attacks, should be punished in the same way as adults. However, I strongly disagree with this statement. This essay will discuss both sides of the argument and propose potential solutions to address the issue.

Firstly, it is important to understand the reasons why youth become involved in criminal activities. Poverty is often one of the main causes, as wealthier individuals exploit the vulnerabilities of those less fortunate, leading them to engage in illegal activities such as drug and weapon smuggling, as well as human trafficking. Additionally, the allure of materialistic possessions and a luxurious lifestyle can tempt young people to choose unlawful means, as they perceive it as a fast-track to wealth. Media, including movies and social platforms, also play a significant role in shaping their behavior, with young individuals often imitating the actions and strategies they see on screen in real life.

It is essential for the government to adopt a different approach towards punishing minors and focus on education and prevention. Establishing specialized schools that cater to the needs of young offenders should be a priority. These schools will not only offer academic education but also provide guidance and support to help these individuals make better choices and exit their current critical situations. Moreover, educating young people about the consequences of their actions and offering alternative paths for personal growth and success are crucial in reducing crime rates. Self-defense workshops and awareness programs can also play a significant role in empowering youths and equipping them with the necessary skills to protect themselves in dangerous situations.

In conclusion, it is clear that poverty and external influences contribute to the involvement of young people in serious crimes. Instead of treating them as adults and imposing harsh punishments, the government should focus on addressing the root causes of criminal behavior. By providing education, guidance, and alternative opportunities, we can help steer young individuals away from a life of crime and towards a brighter future.
