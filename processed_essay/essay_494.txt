Topic:

"Many students find it difficult to pay attention at school. What are the reasons for this? What could be done to solve this problem?"

Essay:

"Due to increasing pressure and various other circumstances, children nowadays find it challenging to engage and concentrate on their academics at school. However, there are certain measures that can be taken to resolve this issue and make learning more fun and enjoyable for students. 

Primarily, the lack of attention is due to the traditional teaching techniques followed by institutions. Passive teaching is sure to bore students rather than develop their talents since each student's grasping ability differs. The teachers are instructing students to memorise the answers instead of engaging in brainstorming and creative learning activities. The students rely on their memory and write the answer in the examination, which becomes the reason for frustration among the students. The use of portable devices such as mobile phones during lectures is another source of distraction. Instead of focusing on academics, students spend the majority of their time playing video games and surfing the internet. 

Alternatively, if instructors in schools use active learning techniques, in which each student is given the opportunity to exhibit what they have learned, students' learning will increase. Teachers should also use a variety of instructional strategies to pique students' interest and engagement, such as presenting videos, engaging through presentations, and personally contacting students. Finally, because distractions like video games and cell phones are the most prevalent cause of a child's lack of interest in studying, they should be banned in schools, and parents should impose a daily usage restriction on them. 

To sum up, modern teaching techniques should be implemented in order to develop students' enthusiasm for learning and gadgets should be prohibited and limited because they tend to act as a barrier and reduce students' effectiveness"


Revised:

Due to increasing pressure and various other circumstances, many students nowadays find it challenging to pay attention at school. However, there are certain measures that can be taken to resolve this issue and make learning more enjoyable for students.

Primarily, the lack of attention can be attributed to the traditional teaching techniques followed by institutions. Passive teaching methods tend to be boring for students and do little to develop their talents, as each student's grasping ability differs. Teachers often focus on instructing students to memorize answers rather than engaging them in brainstorming and creative learning activities. Consequently, students often rely on their memory alone to write answers during examinations, leading to frustration. In addition, the use of portable devices such as mobile phones during lectures is another major source of distraction. Instead of focusing on academics, students spend a significant amount of time playing video games and surfing the internet.

Alternatively, adopting active learning techniques in schools can significantly increase students' engagement and learning. In active learning, each student is given the opportunity to demonstrate what they have learned. This approach encourages students to actively participate in the learning process and fosters a greater sense of ownership over their education. Additionally, teachers should employ a variety of instructional strategies to capture students' interest, such as presenting videos, using interactive presentations, and providing personalized feedback to students. These strategies can enhance students' motivation and create a more stimulating learning environment.

Furthermore, it is crucial to address the issue of distractions caused by video games and cell phones. These devices are the most prevalent cause of students' lack of interest in studying, and they should be strictly regulated in schools. Implementing a ban on these devices during school hours will help minimize distractions and promote a more focused learning environment. Parents also play a vital role in reducing distraction by imposing restrictions on the daily use of electronic gadgets at home. By doing so, students will have fewer distractions and be encouraged to engage more actively in their studies.

In conclusion, to improve students' attention and engagement at school, it is necessary to adopt modern teaching techniques that promote active learning. Additionally, measures should be taken to minimize distractions caused by video games and cell phones. By implementing these changes, students will be more enthusiastic about their education and will have a greater ability to concentrate on their studies.
