Revised:

In the modern world, public services and facilities have a significant impact on people's lives. Some argue that government funds should be solely devoted to these services such as transportation, security, education, and healthcare, rather than the arts. I strongly agree with this viewpoint and will discuss my reasons in the following paragraphs.

First and foremost, society as a whole benefits when the government provides essential services. By improving the medical sector, the nation's overall health would improve, leading to increased productivity in work and education. This, in turn, would enable the country to develop economically.

Additionally, if the government fully funds the education sector, the population will continue to acquire knowledge and skills, enabling them to compete with other countries. A well-educated population is essential for a nation's progress and development in today's highly competitive global landscape.

Moreover, investing in public transportation systems is equally important. By allocating funds to improve transportation infrastructure, individuals are less likely to rely on private cars. This not only reduces pollution and traffic congestion but also contributes to a happier society. Furthermore, when regions are safe to live in with low crime rates, they become attractive to tourists, leading to a significant inflow of money into the country. A prime example is islands like Madagascar, Maldives, and Bali, which derive 90% of their income from tourism.

In conclusion, I firmly believe that the government should prioritize spending on public services and facilities instead of the arts. By meeting the basic needs of the population, the government ensures a vibrant and successful future for the entire nation.
