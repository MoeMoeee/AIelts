Revised:

Primary school education has always had a significant impact on students. A well-designed primary education program not only cultivates students' interest in learning but also encourages them to explore their own abilities. Therefore, teaching foreign languages in primary school plays a vital role in a student's development compared to secondary school. 

It is highly recommended that primary educational strategies include teaching both national and international languages. In order to succeed in today's globalized world, language learning at a young age is crucial. The curriculum in primary school should include at least one foreign language, with English being the most popular choice. By starting to learn a language from an early age, students are able to develop strong proficiency in reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills. The younger brain is highly receptive to new information and can absorb language more easily. Learning from a young age also helps students understand the correct syntax and semantics of words, which in turn improves their vocabulary.

In addition, learning a foreign language can be a valuable asset for those seeking higher education abroad. Furthermore, when students enter the workforce, they should be capable of expressing their thoughts fluently. This not only helps them secure better job opportunities within their own country but also increases their chances of working in foreign organizations.

Although there are a few disadvantages, such as students possibly neglecting their national language studies, the benefits of learning a foreign language at the primary level outweigh the drawbacks. Secondary school studies should focus more on the specific areas in which students want to excel.

To conclude, primary education serves as the foundation of a student's academic journey, and it is highly recommended to introduce foreign language learning at the primary level. Learning a foreign language opens up more opportunities in the long run and equips students with valuable skills for their future endeavors.
