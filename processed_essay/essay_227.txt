Topic:

"Some people think schools should group pupils according to their academic ability, but others believe pupils with different abilities should be educated together. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

Essay:

"Different academic abilities exist among students, according to discipline, intelligence, and hard work. Some schools believe students should be grouped into different levels, depending on academic performance, while others disagree. In my view, this should never happen, as it induces numerous issues in both classes. The following paragraphs will explore both sides of the argument and highlight why I support one side.

Indeed, overachievers feel underwhelmed when faced with the workload average pupils receive. It simply does not satisfy them to know they have locked potential, all due to their fellow classmates’ inability to match their own standards. Above average students tend to feel more hungry for knowledge, looking for an actual challenge, which is somewhat understandable. By dividing a class into two, that could be achieved. Instant fiery competitiveness is induced between the overachieving classmates. Likewise, longer hours of teaching the academically average group makes a lot of sense, to ensure they understood the topic and to address any questions they may have. In addition to that, the A+ group can be introduced to advanced level topics, in order to fully unleash their potential, and satisfy their hunger for knowledge.

On the other hand, new problems show up once a class of supposedly the same grade, is split up into two categories. This, essentially, implies to the classmates that there is a superior group and a dumb one. Consequently, members of the “dumb class” will feel inadequate, and immediately lose self-esteem and thus, their motivation. On top of that, the upper division will most likely adopt a superiority complex that leads to looking down upon their peers. A larger workload of homework is bound to inspire a feeling of unfairness among the lower level, as well as applying pressure on the higher level to naturally acquire that knowledge without the same amount of homework. To make the situation further complicated, not every school can afford paying double the salary for each grade.

In conclusion, natural excellence is a blessing and a curse in disguise. While the academically blessed may score large numbers in tests, they feel bored being in the same classroom as less fortunate peers. Dividing the two solves that, while causing even greater problems, such as insecurity or lack of motivation. My opinion, ultimately, is that splitting colleagues of the same grade into two is harmful more than is helpful."


Revised:

Some people argue that schools should group pupils based on their academic abilities, while others believe that students with different abilities should be educated together. In this essay, I will discuss both views and provide my opinion on this matter.

On the one hand, proponents of grouping students according to their academic abilities argue that it allows for tailored instruction and promotes healthy competition. These individuals believe that high-achieving students should be placed in advanced classes, where they can be challenged and develop their full potential. By separating them from their less academically inclined peers, they can receive a more rigorous curriculum that caters to their abilities.

Moreover, supporters of this approach argue that grouping students based on their academic abilities facilitates more efficient teaching. Teachers can focus on the specific needs of each group, ensuring that all students are adequately engaged and challenged. Furthermore, by separating students with different abilities, teachers can provide more personalized attention and support to those who may be struggling academically.

On the other hand, opponents of grouping students based on academic abilities argue that it can lead to various negative consequences. They believe that such a practice may result in feelings of inferiority and low self-esteem among the less academically inclined students. Being labeled as part of a "lower" or "dumb" group may undermine their confidence and motivation to succeed.

Additionally, opponents argue that separating students based on their abilities perpetuates social divisions and can prevent students from developing important social skills. By segregating students with different abilities, schools may miss out on the opportunity to foster an inclusive and supportive learning environment where diversity is valued.

In my opinion, I believe that grouping students based solely on their academic abilities is detrimental and should be avoided. While it may offer benefits for high-achieving students, it often comes at the cost of the overall development and well-being of all students. It is essential to create an educational environment that fosters inclusivity and supports the unique needs of each student, regardless of their academic abilities. By promoting collaboration and diversity within the classroom, students can learn from one another and develop important social and emotional skills that are crucial for their future success.

In conclusion, the issue of whether to group pupils according to their academic abilities or to educate them together with students of different abilities is a complex one. While there may be potential benefits to separating students based on their abilities, such an approach can lead to negative consequences for the overall growth and development of students. It is crucial for schools to find a balance between catering to the unique needs of high-achieving students and fostering an inclusive and supportive learning environment.
