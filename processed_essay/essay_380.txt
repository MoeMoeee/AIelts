Discuss both these view and give your own opinion."

"A group of people have an opinion that guardians should encourage  their kids to utilise their leisure time by enrolling themselves on social or group activities. In conclusion, although being social and being a team player can be learned by participating in a group activity,  making an effort in a personalised development keep children focused and productive."


Revised:

A group of people believe that parents should encourage their children to participate in organised group activities during their free time, while others argue that it is important for children to learn how to occupy themselves independently. In this essay, I will discuss both perspectives and provide my own opinion.

Advocates of group activities argue that engaging in such activities can contribute to the overall development of children. They believe that children need to develop socially and that simply focusing on academics is not sufficient. By participating in group activities, children can learn to become team players, foster friendships, and develop social skills. Additionally, interacting with other children can help reduce their self-centered nature. While these arguments are valid, I personally do not fully agree with this viewpoint.

On the other hand, some people argue that children should learn how to occupy themselves with individual tasks, as this can lead to greater productivity. They advocate for children to spend their free time on activities such as studying, learning indoor skills like cooking or reading, or playing indoor games with siblings. By engaging in personalised activities, children can focus more on their individual growth and less on external influences. This can also help them avoid negative habits often associated with group activities, such as gossiping or peer pressure. Personally, I believe in the importance of children valuing their time and being able to concentrate on personal development.

In conclusion, while participating in group activities can foster social skills and teamwork, I believe that children can benefit more from engaging in personalised tasks during their free time. By focusing on individual growth, children can learn to properly manage their time and prioritize productive activities.
