Here is the edited essay according to IELTS structure:

Forests play a crucial role in producing fresh oxygen and regulating the climate. However, deforestation has become a serious problem leading to a decline in the tree cover of our planet. This essay will discuss the primary causes of deforestation and its resulting consequences.

To begin with, the primary cause of deforestation is human activities. To cater to the growing demands of the population, forests are being cleared for land to construct buildings or for city expansion. Mass deforestation for wood is also regarded as one of the most harmful activities that humans have done to the environment. Moreover, climate change can cause wildfires that destroy a significant number of trees. For instance, the two-year drought in Australia between 2019 and 2020 led to a severe bushfire season, resulting in a state of emergency being declared by the authorities.

As a result of deforestation, the negative effects are a hotter climate and soil erosion. Trees play a vital role in regulating the air by converting carbon dioxide into oxygen. When trees are cut down, the amount of CO2 released by factories and cars increases, which is one of the main reasons for global warming. Additionally, the lack of tree cover leads to soil erosion.  This means that the soil is not able to retain its nutrients and will quickly become a wasteland where plants will have difficulty growing.

In conclusion, deforestation occurs due to human actions such as land development and wood extraction, as well as climate change leading to wildfires. The adverse impacts of deforestation include a rise in global temperature, which may cause many species to become extinct, and soil erosion that can render land unproductive. Therefore, it is crucial to undertake efforts to reduce deforestation and promote reforestation to ensure that our planet can thrive for generations to come.
