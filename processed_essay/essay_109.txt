Here is the edited version of your essay with improvements made to the IELTS structure:

Introduction:
It is undeniable that certain advertising techniques employed by some companies are perceived as unethical and unacceptable in contemporary society. This essay concurs with this perspective and will explore the reasons behind this viewpoint by examining specific advertising methods. Ultimately, it will be argued that advertising that manipulates consumers or misleads them into purchasing products is unacceptable.

Body Paragraph 1:
One of the primary reasons individuals object to certain advertising methods relates to the misrepresentation of products. Misleading advertisements with slogans such as "limited time offer" can pressure consumers into buying a product without careful consideration. For instance, in India, TV channel Shop CJ uses this method consistently by displaying countdown clocks with products to create a sense of urgency to encourage sales.

Body Paragraph 2:
Another form of advertising that elicits resentment among consumers is the use of pop-up websites that irritate users with numerous pop-up ads before they can access the desired content. For instance, the tech company Techmahindra.com is notorious for displaying numerous pop-up sites, making it frustrating for users. Similarly, certain companies hire celebrities to promote their brand, and this endorsement can sometimes be perceived negatively, especially if the product promotes unrealistic beauty standards. An infamous example would be the promotion of fairness creams by Bollywood actor Varun Dhawan, perpetuating the idea that fairer skin is superior.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, while advertisers use various methods to market their products, many consumers disapprove of these strategies as they often manipulate or mislead them into buying a product. This essay has highlighted some of the more dubious advertising methods, such as misrepresenting products and using pop-up ads, which can irritate consumers and lead to a negative perception of a brand. Therefore, marketing efforts should prioritize ethical practices and transparency, so consumers can make informed decisions about purchases.
