Revised Essay:

Parents play a vital role in their children's success. While some parents do not interfere in their child's education, others put too much pressure on them to do well in school. In this essay, I will explore the reasons behind this practice and discuss the role of parents in their children's education.

Parents may put an excess of pressure on their children because they want their children to have a brighter future. However, this may have a negative impact on their children as they may not be able to focus on their performance due to their fear of disappointing their parents. This may lead them to take the wrong path, such as cheating, bribing teachers for high marks and skipping classes and exams. Moreover, childhood is a crucial stage in life in which children decide on their future with the help of their parents. Hence, it is essential for parents to show love and care towards their children. If they put too much pressure on them, the children may choose to associate with bad society, leading to weaker bonds with their family members.

Parents should take steps wisely and consider both the negative and positive outcomes while providing guidance to their children. While corporal punishment may be an option, it can have negative impacts. Therefore, parents should build strong relationships with their children so that they can share their feelings without fear or worry, enabling the parents to make decisions with their child's best interests taken into account. Additionally, parents should guide their children on the benefits of education, and help them make a schedule for studying and playing, which can make a significant difference in sharpening the young minds.

In conclusion, parents should treat their children with love and care instead of putting excessive pressure on them. This approach will motivate children to pursue a positive path and avoid associating with bad society. By doing so, parents can foster a strong bond with their children and guide them towards a successful future.
