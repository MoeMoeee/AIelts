Revised:

Food security is a major concern worldwide, leading countries to invest a significant portion of their GDP in agriculture. However, despite advancements in the farming industry, many regions still suffer from food insecurity. This essay aims to explore the reasons behind this issue and discuss potential solutions.

One of the main factors contributing to the uneven distribution of food cultivation worldwide is the disparity in access to advanced agricultural machinery. Developed countries have benefitted from industrial advancements, while developing nations and those with lower incomes struggle to access and utilize these technologies. Moreover, long-standing conflicts in certain regions have led to economic collapses and food insecurities. Political instability further exacerbates the problem, as poor decision-making by governments can lead to collapses in the agricultural sector. For instance, in Sri Lanka, the sudden ban on all chemical pesticides without a gradual withdrawal caused a severe food shortage within a year. Therefore, it is evident that poor decision-making can trigger starvation in various parts of the world despite the availability of resources.

To address this issue, countries should establish concrete agricultural policies that are immune to frequent changes by politicians. Additionally, governments need to invest more in infrastructure for agronomy. However, without a skilled workforce, even the most advanced machinery would be in vain. Hence, it is imperative to educate farmers and encourage the development of crop science graduates, as this would provide a long-term solution to the problem.

In conclusion, strengthening the food industry requires educating farmers and providing them with newer technological infrastructure. Additionally, these efforts should be supported by stable and transparent policies, safeguarding against corruption and exploitative practices by intermediary sellers. By implementing these measures, global food security can be strengthened, ultimately eliminating hunger worldwide.
