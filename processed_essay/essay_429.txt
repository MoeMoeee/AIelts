Introduction:
It is believed by many that entertainment in the form of video games and social media has a detrimental impact on both individuals and society as a whole. This essay will explore the reasons behind this assertion, focusing primarily on the health-related issues associated with excessive use of such devices. While there may be some benefits to these forms of entertainment, the overwhelming evidence suggests that the negative consequences far outweigh the positives. Thus, this essay agrees with the statement that electronic games and social media are harmful and discusses the negative effects on individuals and society.

Body Paragraph 1:
One of the key concerns associated with excessive use of electronic games and social media is the detachment from reality and the impact on social interactions. Spending hours scrolling through social media feeds or playing video games hampers individuals' ability to effectively communicate and develop meaningful relationships. Furthermore, this prolonged screen time results in reduced attention spans, making it challenging for individuals, particularly students, to focus on important tasks. While there may be exceptions, such as watching a film with family, the overall excessive use of electronic devices has a detrimental impact on social interactions and communication skills.

Body Paragraph 2:
Moreover, prolonged engagement with electronic games and social media has been found to have negative effects on individuals' physical and mental health. The overstimulation provided by electronic games makes mundane daily activities seem uninteresting and less satisfying. The excessive release of dopamine, a pleasure-inducing chemical, encourages individuals to prioritize electronic entertainment over more beneficial activities such as reading books or engaging in physical exercises. Consequently, this sedentary lifestyle contributes to obesity and the deterioration of friendships due to reduced face-to-face interactions.

Body Paragraph 3:
The addictive nature of electronic devices further exacerbates the adverse effects on individuals' well-being. The constant need to tap screens and engage with social media or games creates a dependency that leads to various health issues, both mental and physical. Overuse of portable devices increases the risk of obesity, causes eye strain, and has been linked to depression, particularly among teenagers. The excessive time spent on screens replaces meaningful activities, leading to a decrease in motivation and overall productivity.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, while there may be some limited instances where electronic games and social media can offer harmless entertainment, the overwhelming evidence showcases the negative impact on individuals and society at large. Excessive use of these devices results in detachment from reality, reduced social interactions, diminished attention spans, physical health issues, and mental health problems such as depression. Therefore, it is crucial to limit screen time and prioritize more meaningful activities to ensure the well-being and development of individuals and society as a whole.
