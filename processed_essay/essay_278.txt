This saturation has led to an overload of information and a decrease in the effectiveness of advertising. Many individuals have developed a resistance to advertisements and often dismiss them as noise or distractions. They believe that they are no longer influenced by advertising and make purchasing decisions based on their own research and personal recommendations.

In my opinion, while it is true that advertising has become more common and people are more cautious about it, it still has the power to attract consumers. Even though we may not always consciously pay attention to advertisements, they subconsciously influence our buying decisions. Advertisements create brand awareness and familiarity, making the products or services more memorable when the need arises. Moreover, companies are constantly evolving their advertising techniques to stay relevant and engage with consumers. The rise of influencer marketing and interactive online ads are examples of this evolution.

In conclusion, advertising is undoubtedly successful in persuading people to buy things. However, with its ubiquity in modern society, some argue that it has become ineffective. In my view, while people may not consciously pay attention to every advertisement, they still have the potential to influence our purchasing decisions.
