Revised:

Revised Essay:

In today’s society, there are different perspectives on where is the better place to settle down - rural or urban areas. While some individuals prefer to enjoy the peace and tranquillity of nature, others find the city life more appealing due to better amenities and a wider social network. In this essay, I will discuss both views and provide my opinion.

On the one hand, some people argue that living in the countryside provides a better quality of life as it offers access to natural beauty, such as rivers, mountains, forests and farmlands, and a hustle-free lifestyle. They claim that this results in a better mental and physical health. For instance, in Australia, a survey has shown that 20% of the population has moved from downtown to the suburbs in search of a peaceful life.

On the other hand, living in a metropolitan area provides a plethora of amenities for its residents. Such benefits include a better education system for children, more job opportunities for the youth, advanced healthcare facilities, a convenient transportation system, among others. For example, a friend of mine, Amit Kumar, found more job opportunities after switching jobs a few times due to the abundance of options available in a large city.

Apart from an improved lifestyle, living in the city also allows individuals to expand their social circle as they interact with various personalities and cultures. This enhances their knowledge and social skills and eliminates the possibility of loneliness or depression.

In conclusion, while it is true that living in the countryside provides a better natural environment, the benefits of living in the city including better amenities, job opportunities, and social network outweigh the advantages of living in the countryside. Therefore, I believe that settling in the city is a better choice for long-term living standards and social growth.
