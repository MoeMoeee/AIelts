Do you agree or disagree?"

To begin with, shows made for TV and electronic games are made based on the popularity among the crowd. Secondly, these type of contents are dangerous for children as they might not understand how abnormal it is. For instance a survey conducted by American Psychological Association among students points that the kids are more leaning towards games like Bulletstorm which are very violent in nature. Hence it would be very useful for parents if these violent products are banned on the government level.

In conclusion, eventhough these shows and games are made as part of their popularity it could never deny the fact that it increases the criminality in the society and also increases the chance that the future generation might behave as in the characters of these shows and games as it is normal."


Revised:

There is a viewpoint that suggests vicious TV programmes and hostile video games should be prohibited by governments. In my opinion, I strongly agree with this stance as these forms of media can contribute to an increase in criminal behavior among younger generations.

To commence, TV shows and electronic games are generated based on their popularity among the public. This is partially due to individuals seeking an outlet for their frustrations in their social lives, often found in playing games or watching shows. However, as societal discrimination persists, these forms of entertainment have become more violent. Many people find solace in identifying with the characters in these shows and games, allowing them to channel their anger towards other virtual characters, which they are unable to do in real life. For instance, a survey conducted by an online platform showed that 70% of respondents enjoy playing boxing video games to release their anger towards their bosses. This behavior is dangerous, as it can eventually become patterned in their real lives, leading to an increase in real-life violence.

Furthermore, these types of violent content are particularly perilous for children, who may not fully comprehend the abnormality of such behavior. As children are exposed to these shows and games, they may perceive these actions as normal, subsequently incorporating them into their own lives without understanding the potential dangers. For example, a survey conducted by the American Psychological Association showed that children are increasingly drawn to games like Bulletstorm, which are highly violent in nature. Thus, it would be highly beneficial for parents if these violent products were banned at a governmental level.

In conclusion, while these shows and games are created based on their popularity, it cannot be denied that they contribute to an increase in criminal behavior within society. Additionally, there remains a heightened risk that future generations may emulate the behavior exhibited by the characters in these shows and games, perceiving it as normal. Therefore, banning violent TV shows and aggressive video games at the governmental level is essential in maintaining societal well-being.
