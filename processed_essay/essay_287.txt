Topic:

"Many people use new forms of technology to communicate these days. What are the positive effects of this trend on personal and professional relationships? Could online forms of communication lead to superficial connections?"

Essay:

"In our modern time, people relay information differently as a result of technological advancement. In my opinion, relationships have, for the most part, improved in two major ways through this phenomenon. I am also convinced the use of new means of communication, made available through the Internet, could potentially give rise to less meaningful interactions.

It is my view that, on a personal level, technology has enabled us to stay in touch more easily. People used to communicate through the postal system if they wished to contact someone who lived far from them. This system was cumbersome and often led to loss of connection. Through programs such as Skype or WhatsApp, everyone can now speak to their friends and relatives around the world whenever they please. In addition, whereas working professionals had limited opportunity to get to know their colleagues before, they can, at the present time, use various websites to find out about them. Website such as Instagram or LinkedIn have helped staff members to find common interests to talk about at work.

I further believe that Internet-based platforms sway individuals into staying connected mainly in the virtual world. For example, there are people who have hundreds of friends on sites such as Facebook, yet they rarely speak to them about important issues in life. Another reason why relationship mat at times remain superficial could be attributed to impaired social skills. Since some people refrain from socializing in real-life situations and instead chat with others through the Internet, they fail to establish deep connections through face-to-face communication. The youth, in particular, might prefer to spend their time online and miss the chance to practice their skills to form intimate relationships.

In conclusion, technological innovations have revolutionized both our personal and professional lives by allowing us to communicate with friends, relatives and colleagues less effortfully. We can also discover a lot more about others’ interests through social media. From my  perspective, however, since more people prefer to spend their time online, missing the chance to develop their social skills, engaging in cutting-edge forms of communication could diminish the depth of their relationships."


Revised:

In our modern society, the way people communicate has been transformed due to technological advancements. This trend has had both positive effects and potential drawbacks on personal and professional relationships. While new forms of technology have made it easier to stay connected with others, there is a concern that online communication can lead to superficial connections.

On a personal level, technology has enabled us to stay in touch more easily. In the past, contacting someone who lived far away would require the use of the postal system, which was often inconvenient and led to a loss of connection. However, with the advent of programs such as Skype and WhatsApp, individuals can now communicate with their friends and relatives around the world whenever they please. Moreover, new means of communication, such as Instagram or LinkedIn, have provided working professionals with the opportunity to learn more about their colleagues and find common interests to discuss at work.

However, there is a growing concern that online communication may result in less meaningful interactions. Many people have hundreds of friends on social media platforms like Facebook, yet they seldom engage in deep conversations or discuss important issues in life. One possible reason for this is impaired social skills. As some individuals refrain from interacting in real-life situations and opt to chat with others through the Internet, they fail to establish deep connections through face-to-face communication. This issue is especially prevalent among young people who spend a significant amount of their time online, missing valuable opportunities to practice and develop their skills in forming intimate relationships.

In conclusion, technological advancements have revolutionized both our personal and professional lives by providing us with more effortless ways to communicate with others and discover their interests. However, it is important to consider the potential negative effects of this trend, such as the possibility of superficial connections and impaired social skills. To avoid these pitfalls, individuals should strive to balance their online interactions with face-to-face communication and actively work on developing meaningful relationships.
