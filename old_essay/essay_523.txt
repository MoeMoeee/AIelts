Revised:

Today, people have a variety of vehicles to choose from when traveling. However, these vehicles contribute to various environmental problems such as air pollution, global warming, and climate change. Hence, it is crucial for cities to implement comprehensive plans to address the negative impacts of vehicle usage. To promote the use of public transport, some cities have introduced vehicle-free days. In my opinion, even though there are some drawbacks that should not be overlooked, the advantages of having vehicle-free days outweigh the disadvantages.

Firstly, the advantages of using public transport should be considered. The primary benefit is that it promotes better health and significantly reduces environmental problems. Vehicle emissions caused by private cars, trucks, and motorcycles are harmful to our health and contain greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change. If people fail to grasp the possibility of climate change, they will inevitably face its consequences, such as famine, drought, and flooding. For instance, a survey found that in cities where public transport is not widely used, over 45% of the population is at a higher risk of experiencing asthma and breathing difficulties due to exposure to toxic gases.

Another significant advantage of using public transport is that it helps conserve non-renewable energy resources like oil. With the ever-increasing population, these energy resources are depleting rapidly. However, these resources are essential as they are energy-rich and relatively inexpensive to process. Vehicle-free days provide an opportunity for people to realize the detrimental effects of oil consumption and encourage them to find alternative means of transportation.

In conclusion, the implementation of vehicle-free days has proven to be effective in mitigating the adverse effects of private vehicle use. While there are some drawbacks, such as inconvenience and dependency on public transport, the benefits of having vehicle-free days outweigh these disadvantages. By promoting the use of public transport, we can improve our health and reduce environmental pollution. Furthermore, it allows us to conserve non-renewable energy resources and move towards a more sustainable future.
