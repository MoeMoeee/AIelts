Revised:

In recent years, energy prices have experienced a significant increase, leading to a phenomenon known as "fuel poverty". This essay will outline the situation and problems associated with fuel poverty, followed by a discussion of potential solutions and an evaluation of their effectiveness.

Fuel poverty refers to the inability of individuals to afford enough money to adequately heat their homes. This issue arises due to low income, high fuel prices, and inefficient energy use (Jones, 2023). Living in cold and damp places exacerbates the problem. Research by Lynos (2023) reveals that homes without proper insulation can result in annual heating costs that are €1,368 higher compared to insulated homes. Another factor contributing to rising energy bills is the escalating cost of polluting energy sources like gas and oil. Moreover, the current pricing system leads to individuals with lower earnings paying higher energy costs, while the wealthy benefit from decreased prices as they consume more (Nickless, 2023).

To improve the situation, several potential solutions can be considered. Firstly, a transition from fossil fuels to clean, renewable energy generated by sources such as solar, wind, and wave power could permanently reduce costs. This requires swift action and investment in renewable energy technologies. Secondly, the implementation of a fair pricing system could alleviate the burden. Nickless (2023) suggests that supplying essential energy for cooking, heating, and lighting should be priced equitably, recognizing these basic needs as human rights. Thirdly, artificial intelligence (AI) can aid in the fight against fuel poverty. A project is currently utilizing modern AI software to analyze data from various sources and identify households in need of additional support (Winser, 2023). Lastly, the government should introduce a comprehensive street-by-street insulation program to minimize energy waste and lower costs.

In conclusion, the number of people affected by fuel poverty is projected to increase to 8.4 million by April 2023 (Sandiford & McRae, 2023), highlighting the urgency and magnitude of this issue. Governments should play a significant role in addressing fuel poverty, as no individual should have to choose between heating their home and meeting other basic needs (Lynos, 2023). Overall, transitioning to renewable energy, implementing a fair pricing system, utilizing AI technology, and promoting insulation programs can help alleviate the problem.
