Topic:

"More children in developed countries are becoming overweight. This is a serious problem for wealthy countries.

Discuss some causes and effects of this problem. Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience."

Essay:

"Obesity is one of the distinguished problem faced by developed countries, according to a few surveys conducted worldwide. Furthermore, the percentage of children, between 4-14 years, suffering from overweight is particularly higher when compared to the rest of the population. In this essay, I shall discuss some major reasons along with the impacts of it.

There are many entities which play a vital role in a substantial increase in obese children. Nowadays, the dietary habits in rich nations gradually changed over the years. Especially in countries like the United States, London and also few Asian territories, consumption of unhealthy products, such as packaged foods, fast-food, carbonated cool drinks and desserts with higher sugar levels, increased. In addition, another potential cause is due to the abundant availability of money in consequence with lack of proper time. Parents work hard round the clock to provide a comfortable life for their family; however, because of their high wages, they are at ease to afford excess food. Moreover, the trending culture of grocery shopping in high-end markets, for example, Walmart, lure adults and children in purchasing unnecessary edible items, which eventually increase intake, followed by corpulence.

Consequently, the problems faced by such children keep developing over time. Predominant ones among many others are, serious health issues, for instance, high cholesterol level, heart diseases, hormonal changes, PCOD in women, skin complications, hair loss and many other vital worries. In fact, depression is one common struggled issue by people with any of these health troubles. However, everything can be put to control with proper diet, physical exercise and medication under proper professional guidance.

In conclusion, identifying this problem and understanding the negative effect caused by minimalistic actions throughout childhood could ruin one's healthy life. It is high to start improvising it through baby steps and succeed in every aspect of their career."


Revised:

Overweight children have become a serious issue in developed countries. This essay will examine the causes and effects of this problem, along with reasons and examples.

One primary cause of childhood obesity in developed countries is the change in dietary habits. The consumption of processed foods, fast food, sugary drinks, and desserts has increased in countries such as the United States, London, and several Asian territories. In addition, children and parents are lured into purchasing unhealthy foods from high-end grocery markets, which can add to the problem.

Another contributing factor is the availability of excess finances and lack of time. Due to high wages, parents may provide their children with an overabundance of food without considering the consequences. Furthermore, children may spend less time playing outdoors and being physically active because of busy schedules.

The effects of childhood obesity can be serious and long-lasting. It can lead to health complications such as heart disease, high cholesterol levels, hormonal changes, skin problems, and depression. Appropriate diet, exercise, and medication under professional guidance can help control these issues.

Childhood obesity is a growing problem in developed countries and must be addressed. Understanding the negative effects of unhealthy habits is essential to preventing obesity and associated health risks. By taking small steps towards a healthier lifestyle, children can avoid severe health problems in their future.
