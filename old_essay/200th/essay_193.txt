Topic:

"Some universities now offer their courses on the Internet so that people can study online. Is this a positive or negative development?"

Essay:

"Amidst Covid-19 spread in the world, people were asked to work from home and students had to take their classes online. According to recent discussions, people are considering continuing this trend ahead. But the question arises, Is it beneficial to study from home? Can we replace classroom studies with the ones given online?  In the forthcoming paragraphs, I will discuss the impacts of taking courses online from home.

To begin with, According to Hindu mythology in India, during the early days of education, students were asked to leave their homes and live the entire period of education with their teachers. While not being in their comfort zone, they lead a disciplined life and were transformed into scholars. On the contrary, things have transformed now. Nowadays it is observed that students wake up and join their classes online without even getting up from their beds due to which a proper concentration is not formed. 

However, students going to universities not only obtain knowledge on their courses but also develop interpersonal skills which are hard to learn online. Students get to be with other students from diverse cultures and learn a very valuable skill of friendship, these traits can be built provided all are under one roof. Additionally, professors are able to deliver their lessons smoothly without any interruptions which might occur during online classes.

To conclude, studying on the internet rather than going to universities can be seen as an overall negative development for students. Some of the merits such as saving time during travelling or saving the cost of infrastructure in universities cannot outweigh the demerits."


Revised:

In recent times, universities have made their courses available on the internet to make education more accessible. This has become particularly relevant during the Covid-19 pandemic. While some support this trend, others are skeptical about its effectiveness. This essay will discuss the positive and negative impacts of offering courses online.

On a positive note, online courses offer convenience and flexibility. With online courses, students can learn from the comfort of their own homes, without having to travel to physical classrooms. This saves time and expenses. Moreover, online courses provide flexibility, as students can learn at their own pace and at a time that suits them. In addition, online courses offer a wide range of courses and programs that one might not have access to in traditional universities.

On the other hand, there are several negative impacts of online courses. Firstly, online courses do not provide the same level of interaction with professors and peers as physical classrooms. Students learn not only from course materials, but also through engaging in discussions, debates and asking questions to professors. In addition, students studying online often miss the opportunity to develop interpersonal skills and establish connections with fellow students from different cultures which are essential for personal growth and career development. Furthermore, online courses require a high level of self-discipline and motivation which some students might lack.

In conclusion, while online courses offer convenience, flexibility, and access to a wide range of programs, they cannot replace the benefits of traditional education. Universities provide invaluable opportunities for personal and professional growth through face-to-face interactions with professors and students. Therefore, we should aspire to strike a balance between online and traditional classroom education, rather than replacing one with the other altogether.
