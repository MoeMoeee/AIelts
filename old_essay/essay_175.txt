Here is the edited essay with adjustments to structure and grammar:

It is a historical fact that people have constantly attempted to create a perfect society. Nevertheless, it remains difficult to define what a perfect society entails. In this essay, I will argue that peace is the most important element of an ideal society. I will also discuss some actions that ordinary people can take to create a more perfect society.

Initially, peacefulness is an essential tool for people to lead a satisfying life. In this sense, citizens can have the freedom to enjoy life without fear of being attacked by others. In a peaceful society, crime rates are likely to be low. Examples of such crimes may be theft, murder, and money laundering. In addition, equality, not only between genders, income groups, but religions as well, must be present.

However, maintaining peace in society is a complex task due to multiple factors. Firstly, accepting the country's system, despite social and economic differences, is crucial. By adhering to this ideology, there are less chances of experiencing any breach of peace in society. Additionally, we rarely see the rise of criminals and manipulators in such societies. On the contrary, if people challenge their government's laws, it may lead to social unrest and conflicts. For instance, when observing the World Happiness Index, it reveals that countries where citizens adhere to their constitution, experience more contentment, and are considered peaceful.

In conclusion, although building a perfect society may seem like a utopia, I opine that if everyone starts following their country's laws and order, it will result in a peaceful community. This is the most significant factor in creating a perfect society.
