"Nowadays, the majority of individuals marry and have babies in their thirties than at a younger age. This is a positive development as it allows the couples to develop a strong career and be financially stable. However, because of the concerns of an increasing number of infertility issues, I completely disagree with this development. 
Firstly, late marriage is beneficial to the individuals in many ways. One of the prominent benefits of the same is the ability to develop their career paths. Before marriage, people are generally free to explore different careers and because of having plenty of time, they can decide better about the career that they can pursue during the rest of their life. Revised:

Nowadays, most people get married and have children in their thirties rather than at a younger age. This trend has advantages and disadvantages, and in my opinion, the latter are more significant. Therefore, I disagree with this development, despite its benefits for career and financial stability.

On the one hand, delaying marriage can be advantageous for the individuals involved. By the time they reach their thirties, they may have completed their education, gained work experience, and established a stable income. This enables them to pursue their chosen career path and achieve their professional goals, without the distractions and responsibilities of family life. Furthermore, they may be more mature and ready to commit to a long-term relationship, with better communication skills and more realistic expectations. This can lead to a stronger and healthier marriage, which can benefit both partners and their children, if they have any.

On the other hand, postponing marriage and childbearing can have negative effects, especially for women. As they age, their fertility declines, and the risk of infertility, miscarriage, and genetic disorders increases. According to the World Health Organization, women over thirty-five have a higher chance of experiencing complications during pregnancy and childbirth, such as high blood pressure, diabetes, and premature birth. Moreover, older mothers may face more social stigma and discrimination, especially in traditional societies that value youth and beauty. This can affect their self-esteem, mental health, and relationships with their husbands, families, and peers.

In conclusion, while it is true that getting married and having children later in life may offer some advantages, such as career and financial stability, in my opinion, it is not a positive development overall. The risks and challenges of delayed reproduction, including infertility and social pressure, outweigh the benefits, and may lead to regret, disappointment, and conflict. Therefore, I believe that individuals should consider starting a family earlier, when they feel ready and able, and balance their personal and professional aspirations. This approach can result in a fulfilling and meaningful life, with fewer regrets and more joy.
