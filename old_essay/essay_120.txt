Some people argue that the main environmental issue of our time is the extinction of particular species of plants and animals, while others assert that there are more pressing environmental problems. In my opinion, although the loss of biodiversity is significant, there are other environmental dilemmas that deserve equal or more attention.

On the one hand, individuals who believe that species extinction is the most important environmental problem often base their argument on the fact that social media has increased awareness of endangered species. Furthermore, the loss of biodiversity can impact the food chain, which is critical for sustaining life on earth. Consequently, this leads to the notion that species loss is the biggest issue in our environment.

On the other hand, I contend that there are more vital environmental challenges than species loss. Firstly, the depletion of natural resources such as water and oil is a major concern, and it often leads to conflicts between nations. Countries worldwide are grappling with water scarcity due to the increased pollution caused by ongoing industrialization. Thus, it is crucial to address these issues before they escalate into more severe problems.

In conclusion, I agree that the loss of many species of plants and animals is an important issue, but it is not the only environmental challenge that we face in our current time. The depletion of natural resources and water scarcity are equally significant dilemmas that need to be addressed immediately. Therefore, governments, industries, and individuals should work together to find sustainable solutions to these environmental problems.
