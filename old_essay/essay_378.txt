Revised:

Parenting and dealing with teenagers can be challenging tasks in the modern era. Some argue that conflicts between parents and young adults during the teenage years are necessary, while others believe that these conflicts are unnecessary. In this essay, I will discuss both viewpoints and share my opinion on the matter.

On one hand, there are valid reasons why parents should monitor their children during their teenage years. Firstly, adolescents have limited life experience compared to their parents. If parents fail to intervene when their children are making poor decisions, it can lead to future difficulties. For example, some teenagers rely on their friends or classmates to influence their educational choices, resulting in a lack of interest and poor academic performance. Secondly, parents have the right to guide and support their children so they can become successful individuals. Many teenagers nowadays prioritize social media over education, which can leave them without the necessary skills or qualifications to compete in the job market later in life.

On the other hand, proponents argue that parents should give their children the freedom to make decisions based on their preferences. This might include making choices about their subjects in high school. Allowing youngsters to make independent decisions can help them develop into confident and self-reliant individuals. Furthermore, conflicts between parents and teenagers only lead to strained relationships within the family. It is therefore suggested that families should avoid conflicts and instead spend quality time together.

In conclusion, there are differing opinions regarding conflicts between parents and young adults during the teenage years. However, I believe that conflicts are ultimately fruitless and a waste of time. Instead, it is better for parents and teenagers to make decisions together, fostering understanding and reducing tension. By doing so, both parties can navigate the challenges of the teenage years in a more harmonious manner.
