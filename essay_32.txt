Revised:
Nowadays, there is a growing belief that schools should prioritize molding children into good citizens and workers, rather than fostering their independence. In my opinion, while it is crucial for schools to equip students with the skills needed to excel in the workforce, it is equally important to nurture their independence. This essay will explore both perspectives and provide relevant examples.

On one hand, schools have a responsibility to prepare students to become productive workers, aligning with government objectives. By focusing on developing the skills necessary for the workforce, schools enable students to contribute not only to their own success but also to the betterment of society. For instance, a graduate with expertise in data analysis can assist the administration in making well-informed decisions based on extensive data processing. Thus, schools serve as a valuable tool for governments to achieve their developmental goals.

On the other hand, it is imperative for educational institutions to foster independence among learners. This is because individuals will inevitably face various challenges in their lives, requiring them to think critically and solve problems on their own. By allowing students to work autonomously and overcome obstacles individually, schools equip them with invaluable skills. Moreover, this approach enhances students' confidence and abilities, as they learn important life lessons through firsthand experiences. Consequently, individuals who have encountered and surmounted hardships will be better equipped to navigate diverse circumstances successfully.

In conclusion, I strongly disagree with the notion that schools should prioritize creating good citizens and workers over fostering independence. Educational institutions have a vital role in helping children discover and develop their unique characteristics, ensuring their preparedness for a future filled with challenges. Additionally, cultivating independence allows students to compete effectively in various domains by enabling them to tackle problems autonomously.
